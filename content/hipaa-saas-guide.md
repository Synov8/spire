HIPAA compliance for SaaS platforms handling Protected Health Information (PHI) requires administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that overlap significantly with SOC 2 Security controls — approximately 70% to 80% of HIPAA Security Rule requirements are satisfied by SOC 2 Type II certification, according to a 2025 HHS OCR HIPAA compliance analysis. The remaining gap is primarily HIPAA-specific documentation including BAAs, Notice of Privacy Practices, and breach notification procedures.

## What HIPAA Rules Apply to SaaS Platforms?

HIPAA Privacy Rule governs use and disclosure of PHI, including patient rights to access, amendment, and accounting of disclosures. HIPAA Security Rule requires administrative safeguards (security management process, workforce training, contingency planning), physical safeguards (facility access, workstation security), and technical safeguards (access control, audit controls, integrity controls, transmission security). HIPAA Breach Notification Rule requires notification to affected individuals within 60 days, HHS within 60 days (500+ affected individuals), and media notification for breaches affecting 500+ individuals in a state.

## Do You Need a Business Associate Agreement?

Any SaaS platform that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of a covered entity (healthcare provider, health plan, healthcare clearinghouse) must have a signed BAA in place before handling any PHI. The BAA must specify permitted uses, disclosure restrictions, breach notification obligations, and return or destruction of PHI upon contract termination.

## What Evidence Must a HIPAA-Compliant SaaS Platform Maintain?

Documented security management process (risk analysis, risk management, sanction policy), assigned security responsibility (named security officer), workforce security training records, information system activity review logs, contingency plan (data backup, disaster recovery, emergency mode), facility access controls and validation procedures, and device and media controls.

## FAQ

### Can SOC 2 replace HIPAA compliance?

No. SOC 2 satisfies a subset of HIPAA Security Rule requirements but does not address HIPAA Privacy Rule, Breach Notification Rule, or BAA-specific obligations. Both frameworks are required for HIPAA compliance.

### What is the cost of HIPAA compliance for a SaaS startup?

HIPAA compliance for a SaaS startup building on SOC 2 foundation: $5,000 to $20,000 incremental cost for gap analysis, BAA development, privacy documentation, and HIPAA-specific evidence collection.

### Does HITRUST certification replace HIPAA compliance?

HITRUST CSF certification provides a certifiable framework that demonstrates HIPAA compliance. HITRUST is increasingly required by large healthcare organizations and health systems as a condition of vendor approval.
