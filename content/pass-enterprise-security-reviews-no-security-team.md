Small B2B SaaS companies with zero dedicated security hires are passing enterprise security reviews at increasing rates — 62% of companies with fewer than 30 employees completed at least one enterprise security review in 2025 according to a CSA benchmarking study, up from 38% in 2023. The key enablers are compliance automation platforms, AI-assisted questionnaire response tools, and virtual CISO services that provide security expertise without full-time headcount.

## What Can Replace a Security Team for Enterprise Reviews?

Three alternatives combine to cover the security team gap: compliance automation platforms handle evidence collection and control monitoring (replacing the compliance engineer function), AI-assisted questionnaire response tools handle security questionnaire completion (replacing the security response function), and virtual CISO services provide security expertise, policy review, and auditor liaison (replacing the security leadership function).

The combined cost of these three services ranges from $15,000 to $40,000 per year — substantially less than a single junior security hire at $90,000 to $130,000 salary plus benefits. A 2025 Gartner report found that 44% of companies under 50 employees now use a combination of automation and vCISO rather than a FTE to manage security reviews.

## What Documentation Do You Need to Prepare?

Without a security team, documentation becomes your proxy for security competence. Essential documents include: SOC 2 Type II report (the single most important document that answers 50% of enterprise security questions), penetration testing report (within 12 months), incident response plan (tested within 12 months), business continuity plan, data processing agreement, and sub-processor list.

Each document should be pre-prepared and stored in a virtual data room or trust center that can be shared with prospects on 24-hour notice.

## How Do You Handle Technical Security Questions Without Expertise?

Common questions about encryption algorithms, network architecture, identity provider configuration, and log management require specific technical knowledge. The most efficient approach is to develop standardized technical answers based on your infrastructure's actual configuration, validated by your cloud provider's documentation or a vCISO engagement.

## What Enterprise Security Review Tools Do You Need?

The minimum tool stack for no-security-team SaaS companies is: compliance automation platform for SOC 2 evidence and control monitoring, questionnaire automation tool (Spire, Secureframe AI, or Vanta AI) for response automation, and a trust center for prospect self-service. This stack costs $10,000 to $25,000 per year combined.

## FAQ

### Can I outsource the entire security review process?

Parts of the process can be outsourced — vCISO for policy and review, automation for evidence collection — but a company representative must own the relationship with enterprise prospects and answer questions about company-specific practices.

### Will a SOC 2 report replace the need to answer questions?

SOC 2 reports answer approximately 50% of enterprise questionnaire questions. The remaining questions cover areas outside SOC 2 scope — insurance, data deletion timelines, specific breach history — requiring supplement responses.
