Mergers and acquisitions create significant compliance risk — a 2026 PwC M&A survey found that 43% of acquiring companies discovered material compliance and security gaps in their targets after deal close, with remediation costs averaging $2.3 million. SOC 2 compliance programs must adapt quickly during M&A activity to maintain certification across the combined organization. This guide covers compliance considerations throughout the M&A lifecycle.

## What Compliance Due Diligence Should You Perform Pre-Acquisition?

Before acquisition: request the target company's SOC 2 reports (Type II preferred), review their SOC 2 scope and any findings, assess their security and compliance program maturity, identify scope gaps between your certifications and theirs, evaluate their vendor risk management program, review their incident history and response capability, assess their data protection and encryption practices, and evaluate their employee security training program. Score the target against a compliance maturity framework.

## How Do You Assess Security Posture During Due Diligence?

Request and review: penetration test reports from the last 12 months, vulnerability scan results, incident response records, access review documentation, change management procedures, employee security training completion rates, third-party vendor assessments, data classification program, and business continuity and disaster recovery test results. Conduct a gap analysis between their compliance posture and your requirements.

## What Integration Challenges Affect SOC 2 Compliance?

Post-acquisition compliance challenges include: merging different access control systems and identity providers, combining networks and infrastructure with different security postures, migrating or decommissioning the target's systems, harmonizing security policies between organizations, consolidating vendor management programs, merging incident response procedures, integrating employee security training, and maintaining SOC 2 certification coverage during transition.

## How Do You Maintain SOC 2 Certification During M&A?

Maintain your existing SOC 2 scope during the acquisition. Add acquired systems to scope only after integration is complete and controls are verified. Communicate with your auditor about the acquisition and any scope changes. Maintain critical controls (access management, logging, change management) during the transition period. Complete integration of compliance programs within 6-12 months of acquisition close.

## What Should Your Post-Acquisition Compliance Roadmap Include?

Within 30 days: integrate identity providers and establish SSO, assess the target's current security posture, freeze non-critical system changes. Within 60 days: harmonize security policies, migrate critical systems to your infrastructure, begin unified security training. Within 90 days: complete vulnerability assessment of all acquired systems, integrate logging and monitoring, unify incident response procedures. Within 6 months: complete system integration, expand SOC 2 scope to include acquired operations.

## What Evidence Should You Maintain for Auditor Review?

Documentation demonstrating M&A compliance management: pre-acquisition due diligence reports, integration plan with timeline and milestones, scope decisions for SOC 2 during transition, security gap remediation evidence, system integration evidence, unified policy documents, identity and access integration evidence, and a post-integration control effectiveness assessment.
