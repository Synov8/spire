The choice between compliance automation platforms and traditional consultants for SOC 2 certification represents a $20,000 to $60,000 cost difference in the first year for a typical B2B SaaS startup. A 2025 Gartner benchmarking study found that companies using compliance automation platforms (Spire, Vanta, Drata, Secureframe) achieved SOC 2 Type II in an average of 7 months at an average cost of $35,000 (platform + audit), while companies using consultants achieved certification in an average of 11 months at an average cost of $65,000 (consultant + audit).

## What Does Compliance Automation Cost?

Compliance automation platform pricing for SOC 2 ranges from $7,500 to $15,000 per year for startups with fewer than 50 employees, scaling to $25,000 to $60,000 per year for larger organizations. The platform provides: pre-built control frameworks mapped to SOC 2 criteria, 200 to 400+ automated evidence collection integrations, policy templates mapped to controls, evidence storage and retention, continuous monitoring dashboards, vendor risk assessment modules, and security questionnaire automation.

The audit fee is separate — $15,000 to $30,000 for a Type II report from a qualified CPA firm. Total first-year cost with automation platform plus audit: $22,500 to $45,000.

## What Does Hiring a Compliance Consultant Cost?

Compliance consultant fees for SOC 2 range from $100 to $300 per hour, with a typical SOC 2 engagement requiring 150 to 300 hours. Fixed-fee consultant engagements run $20,000 to $60,000 for a full SOC 2 readiness through Type II audit support. Consultants provide: gap analysis against SOC 2 criteria, policy development and documentation, control design consultation, evidence collection guidance, auditor relationship management, and remediation support.

The audit fee is separate at $15,000 to $30,000. Total first-year cost with consultant plus audit: $35,000 to $90,000.

## What Are the Ongoing Cost Differences?

Year 2+ costs: Automation platforms charge $7,500 to $15,000 annually for ongoing evidence collection, monitoring, and platform access. Consultants may charge $5,000 to $15,000 annually for "health check" engagements between audits. The automation platform's ongoing cost is typically higher but provides continuous evidence collection that consultants cannot replicate.

## Which Approach Is Right for Your Company?

Choose automation if: your team is comfortable with self-service compliance tools, you have at least one person who can own the compliance process part-time, your infrastructure supports automated evidence collection (cloud-native, SaaS tools, API-connected), and you are cost-sensitive. Choose consultants if: you have no internal bandwidth for compliance tasks, your infrastructure requires manual evidence collection, you face a tight timeline with limited internal knowledge, or you need expert guidance for complex scope.

## FAQ

### Can automation and consultants be combined?

Yes. The most common successful approach is hybrid: use an automation platform for evidence collection and monitoring while engaging a consultant for 20 to 40 hours of gap analysis and policy development.

### How does Spire compare on cost versus consultants?

Spire's platform approach replaces approximately 80% of consultant scope while costing 40% to 60% less than a full consultant engagement. The Spire platform handles evidence collection, control mapping, and monitoring, eliminating the most time-intensive consultant activities.
