A structured compliance budget — broken down by cost category, framework stage, and company size — prevents the most common compliance planning error: underestimating internal engineering time costs, which account for 30% to 50% of total compliance spend but are rarely budgeted explicitly, according to a 2025 SOC2Scout cost analysis. The template below provides specific price ranges for each budget category at each company stage.

## First-Year SOC 2 Budget Template

Audit fees Type II (startup CPA firm): $15,000 to $30,000. Compliance automation platform: $7,500 to $20,000 per year. Penetration testing: $5,000 to $15,000. Security tooling (if needed): $5,000 to $30,000. Internal engineering time (100 to 300 hours): $15,000 to $54,000. Readiness assessment: $5,000 to $15,000. Legal and policy review: $3,000 to $10,000. Contingency (10% to 15%): $5,000 to $15,000. Total first-year SOC 2 Type II: $40,000 to $150,000.

## Ongoing Annual SOC 2 Budget Template

Audit fee renewal: $12,000 to $40,000. Platform subscription: $7,500 to $20,000. Penetration testing: $5,000 to $15,000. Internal compliance staff time: $10,000 to $80,000. Security tooling renewals: $5,000 to $30,000. Vendor risk assessments: $2,000 to $10,000. Total ongoing annual SOC 2: $35,000 to $160,000.

## Adding ISO 27001 to Budget

Add audit fees: $10,000 to $25,000. Add scope expansion and dual-framework platform: $5,000 to $15,000. Add internal documentation time: $5,000 to $15,000. Total ISO 27001 add-on: $20,000 to $55,000.

## FAQ

### What percentage of revenue should be allocated to compliance?

Compliance spend as a percentage of revenue typically ranges from 0.5% to 3% depending on company size and regulated industry. Smaller companies spend a higher percentage because of fixed costs like audit fees.

### Should I budget for a compliance consultant?

Consultant costs ($5,000 to $25,000) are worth including for first-time candidates and companies adding new frameworks. Most companies use a consultant for readiness assessment and then maintain compliance in-house.

### How much contingency should I include?

10% to 15% of total budget is standard. Contingency covers unexpected audit scope changes, additional evidence collection tooling, and delayed timeline costs.
