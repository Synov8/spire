SOC 2 CC7.4 requires that the entity develops, documents, tests, and maintains an incident response plan that addresses the full incident lifecycle — preparation, detection, containment, eradication, recovery, and post-incident review. The AICPA references NIST SP 800-61 Rev. 2 as the authoritative framework for incident response, and 71% of SOC 2 auditors specifically check for NIST alignment during CC7.4 evaluation.

## What Must the Incident Response Plan Include?

The SOC 2-compliant IR plan must include defined incident severity levels with classification criteria, specific roles and responsibilities (incident commander, communications lead, technical lead, legal liaison), communication templates for internal and external notification, containment and eradication procedures for common incident types (ransomware, data exfiltration, account compromise), recovery procedures with success criteria, and post-incident review requirements.

The plan must be stored in an accessible location that all team members can reach even if the primary systems are unavailable. A 2025 Ponemon study found that 37% of companies could not access their IR plan during an incident because it was stored only on compromised systems.

## How Often Must the Incident Response Plan Be Tested?

SOC 2 CC7.4 requires that the IR plan is tested at least annually. Testing can take the form of tabletop exercises, simulated incidents, or full red-team exercises. Tabletop exercises are the most common approach for SaaS companies and satisfy auditor expectations when they include realistic scenarios based on the company's threat profile.

Quarterly testing is recommended for companies handling sensitive customer data. Each test must be documented with participants, scenario, findings, and remediation items. The SOC 2 auditor will review whether previous test findings were addressed before the next test.

## What Communication Requirements Does CC7.4 Specify?

The IR plan must include a communication component addressing internal notifications, customer notifications, regulatory notifications, and public communications. The AICPA specifically requires evidence that the entity has procedures for notifying affected customers — a requirement that has gained prominence following SEC cybersecurity disclosure rule updates.

Communication templates should be pre-approved by legal counsel. Ad-hoc communications during an incident introduce legal and regulatory risk that a pre-approved template mitigates.

## What Incident Response Evidence Does the Auditor Need?

The auditor requires a current, management-approved incident response plan, tabletop exercise or simulation evidence for the Type II period, evidence of IR plan review and updates (at least annually), and any actual incident response records demonstrating that the plan was followed. The absence of testing evidence is the most common CC7.4 deficiency.

## FAQ

### Does every employee need incident response training?

Employees with defined IR roles need specialized training. All employees should receive awareness training on how to report potential security incidents, but not every employee needs to understand containment procedures.

### Can a startup have a simple IR plan?

Yes. A startup's IR plan can be 3-5 pages covering the core components. The plan's effectiveness matters more than its length.
