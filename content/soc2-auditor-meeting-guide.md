SOC 2 audit fieldwork typically involves 4 to 8 hours of auditor meetings for a Security-only scope startup and 12 to 25 hours for a multi-TSC enterprise, and companies that prepare structured control walkthroughs reduce fieldwork duration by 35% according to a 2025 CPA firm survey. Each meeting type — kickoff, control walkthrough, evidence review, and closing — has specific preparation requirements.

## Kickoff Meeting

The kickoff meeting (1 to 2 hours) covers audit scope, timeline, evidence delivery format, stakeholder roles, and communication protocols. Prepare an agenda including the system description overview, a list of control owners with contact information, the evidence index showing how each control is evidenced, and the auditor's evidence request list with status. Send the system description, control matrix, and evidence index 1 week before the kickoff.

## Control Walkthrough Meetings

Control walkthroughs (30 to 60 minutes per control area) are where the auditor verifies control design with the control owner. Each walkthrough covers control objective, control design and implementation, evidence sources, monitoring and review procedures, and control effectiveness assessment.

## Evidence Review Sessions

Evidence review sessions (2 to 4 hours per session) are where the auditor examines collected evidence. Organize evidence by control number with a clear naming convention — "CC6.1_AccessControlConfiguration_2026-10-01" — to minimize auditor follow-up questions.

## Closing Meeting

The closing meeting (1 to 2 hours) reviews preliminary findings, any identified exceptions, and the remediation timeline. Exceptions discovered during fieldwork can often be remediated before the final report if addressed quickly.

## FAQ

### How many auditor meetings should I expect?

For a typical SOC 2 Type II audit: 1 kickoff meeting, 5 to 8 control walkthrough meetings, 2 to 4 evidence review sessions, and 1 closing meeting. Total: 10 to 15 meetings over 3 to 6 weeks.

### Should I record auditor meetings?

Ask the auditor's permission before recording. Most auditors prefer not to be recorded. Take detailed notes instead and share meeting minutes for confirmation.

### What if I don't have an answer during a walkthrough?

Say "I will follow up with documentation" and provide the requested evidence within 24 to 48 hours. Auditors prefer honest acknowledgment over speculative answers.
