Building a compliance team requires matching headcount to your compliance maturity stage, and companies that hire the right roles at the right time reduce compliance program costs by 25% to 35% compared to those that hire too early or with the wrong skill sets, according to a 2025 IANS Research compliance staffing analysis. The compliance team should scale with the compliance program complexity, not with company revenue.

## What Compliance Roles Matter at Each Stage?

Pre-SOC 2 (under 25 employees): assign an engineering lead as part-time compliance owner — no dedicated hire needed. SOC 2 readiness to first certification (25 to 50 employees): hire a compliance manager ($100,000 to $140,000) or a GRC analyst ($80,000 to $110,000). Multi-framework expansion (50 to 100 employees): add a security engineer focused on evidence automation ($140,000 to $180,000) and a privacy counsel ($130,000 to $170,000). Enterprise compliance program (100+ employees): add a Director of Compliance ($180,000 to $220,000), vendor risk manager ($110,000 to $145,000), and additional GRC analysts.

## What Certifications Are Valuable for Compliance Roles?

CISA (Certified Information Systems Auditor) is the most relevant certification for SOC 2 audit management. CISM (Certified Information Security Manager) is valuable for compliance program management. CISSP is relevant for technical security engineer roles. CRISC is useful for risk assessment and vendor risk roles. IAPP CIPP/US or CIPP/E is essential for Privacy TSC coverage.

## Should You Hire In-House or Use Fractional Talent?

Fractional compliance officers ($3,000 to $10,000 per month) are cost-effective for companies under 50 employees. In-house hires become more cost-effective at 50+ employees where compliance workload justifies full-time headcount.

## FAQ

### Can a compliance manager be remote?

Yes. Compliance managers are among the most remote-friendly roles. Approximately 65% of GRC roles are fully remote or hybrid in 2026.

### What is the typical compliance team size for a SOC 2 company?

Under 25 employees: 0 dedicated hires. 25 to 50 employees: 1 to 2 dedicated hires. 50 to 200 employees: 3 to 5 dedicated hires. 200+ employees: 5 to 10 dedicated hires.

### Should the compliance team report to the CTO or CEO?

Compliance can report to either, with pros and cons. Reporting to the CTO ensures technical alignment but may create conflicts when compliance findings require engineering resources. Reporting to the CEO provides independence but may lack technical context. The AICPA recommends compliance reporting to a level with authority to enforce findings.
