The three dominant security questionnaire frameworks — SIG (Standardized Information Gathering), CAIQ (Consensus Assessments Initiative Questionnaire), and custom enterprise questionnaires — serve different purposes, and companies that maintain mappings across all three reduce questionnaire response time by 50% compared to treating each as a separate exercise, according to a 2025 Shared Assessments Program efficiency study. Understanding which framework to use and when is critical for sales efficiency.

## SIG: The Comprehensive Enterprise Standard

SIG (Standardized Information Gathering), maintained by the Shared Assessments Program, contains approximately 1,200 questions across 18 risk domains. Used by approximately 60% of enterprise organizations as their standard vendor assessment. SIG is the most comprehensive framework — completing a SIG assessment satisfies 80% to 90% of follow-up questions from enterprise buyers. SIG Lite (approximately 200 questions) is available for smaller or lower-risk vendors.

## CAIQ: The Cloud-Focused Framework

CAIQ (Consensus Assessments Initiative Questionnaire), maintained by the Cloud Security Alliance, contains approximately 300 questions focused specifically on cloud service provider security. Used heavily by cloud-native enterprise buyers and organizations with CSA STAR program participation. CAIQ is more manageable than SIG but covers cloud-specific controls in greater depth.

## Custom Questionnaires: The Wild Card

Custom questionnaires — created by individual enterprise buyers — account for approximately 40% of security assessments. There is no standard format or structure. Each custom questionnaire reflects the buyer's specific security requirements, industry regulations, and risk appetite.

## Which Framework Should You Prepare?

Prepare responses for all three frameworks. Use SIG for enterprise buyers without a standard questionnaire. Use CAIQ for cloud-native and CSA-influenced buyers. Use your response library to handle custom questionnaires by mapping questions to your SIG or CAIQ answers.

## FAQ

### Can SIG replace CAIQ?

SIG is more comprehensive but CAIQ provides deeper cloud-specific coverage. For cloud-native SaaS companies, CAIQ may be more relevant. For general enterprise, SIG is preferred.

### How long does it take to complete SIG Full?

SIG Full (1,200 questions) takes 15 to 30 hours for a prepared team with existing response materials. SIG Lite takes 3 to 6 hours.

### Do buyers accept SIG Lite instead of SIG Full?

Many buyers accept SIG Lite for initial assessments, but full SIG may be required for high-risk vendors or large contract values. Check the buyer's requirements before choosing.
