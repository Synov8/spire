The four leading SOC 2 compliance automation platforms — Vanta, Drata, Secureframe, and Spire — all automate evidence collection and control monitoring, but they differ in integration breadth, auditor network size, multi-framework support, and AI capabilities. The right choice depends on your company stage, tech stack, internal compliance ownership, and need for AI-powered features like automated audit agents and security questionnaire response.

## How Do the SOC 2 Platform Integrations Compare?

Vanta leads with 400+ native integrations, the widest library of any compliance platform. This breadth means that a startup with a standard cloud stack — AWS, Okta, GitHub, Google Workspace — will find pre-built connectors for every major evidence source. Vanta's setup time is the fastest in the category: 2 to 3 days from account creation to active evidence collection.

Drata offers 140+ integrations with a focus on depth rather than breadth. Its Compliance-as-Code framework allows engineering-led teams to build custom evidence pipelines for non-standard infrastructure. Drata is the best choice when your stack includes custom internal tooling, in-house identity management, or unusual cloud architecture.

Secureframe supports 300+ integrations with strong multi-framework cross-mapping. A single piece of evidence can satisfy controls across SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, and GDPR simultaneously, reducing duplicated work for teams pursuing multiple certifications.

Spire integrates with 30 production systems via read-only Composio OAuth — covering cloud (AWS, Azure, Vercel, Cloudflare, DigitalOcean), source code (GitHub, GitLab), identity (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Okta, Clerk), HR/HCM (BambooHR, Workday, Gusto, Rippling, Personio), observability (Datadog, Sentry, PagerDuty), security (Snyk), payments (Stripe), CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot), comms (Slack), tickets (Jira, Linear), and docs (Notion, Confluence) — and layers a unique AI compliance agent on top. The agent actually runs on-demand internal audits against SOC 2 and EU AI Act controls, evaluates collected evidence, and produces structured audit reports with pass/fail/warning verdicts per control. This is a capability not available on Vanta, Drata, or Secureframe today.

## Which Platform Has the Best Auditor Network?

Vanta has the largest auditor network — approximately 500 integrated CPA firms — making it the safest choice if you want maximum auditor flexibility. The platform handles auditor access to evidence and supports collaboration directly within the system.

Drata has strong partnerships with auditors including A-LIGN, Prescient, and Johanson, and offers streamlined evidence handoff that some users report reduces fieldwork time. Secureframe's auditor network is US-focused with guided audit support included in higher-tier plans.

Spire recommends and integrates with boutique CPA firms specializing in B2B SaaS and AI companies. Platform-verified evidence export to auditor tools is supported.

The audit fee is separate from the platform cost regardless of which tool you choose. Platform choice does not determine audit cost — the auditor does. Expect auditor fees of $15,000 to $40,000 for a Type II audit separate from the platform subscription.

## What Is the Pricing Comparison?

Vanta entry tier starts at approximately $10,000 to $12,000 per year for SOC 2 Type II with fewer than 50 employees. Drata's Foundation tier runs $7,500 to $15,000. Secureframe's Fundamentals tier ranges from $7,500 to $20,000. Spire pricing starts at $500 per month ($6,000 per year) for SOC 2 compliance with the AI compliance agent.

All platforms require a sales call for a precise quote. None publish transparent pricing publicly as of mid-2026. The platform fee increases with additional frameworks, company size, and premium features like AI questionnaire automation.

Multi-framework pricing is a key differentiator. Vanta charges per additional framework, which can escalate costs for teams pursuing SOC 2 plus ISO 27001 or HIPAA. Drata and Secureframe offer bundled multi-framework tiers that are more cost-effective for 2-plus framework programs.

## How Do the AI Capabilities Compare?

Vanta offers Vanta AI for questionnaire automation as a paid add-on. Drata provides Drata DRA for evidence analysis and monitoring. Secureframe includes Comply AI for remediation guidance and questionnaire support. All three provide AI-assisted monitoring but none offer an autonomous compliance audit agent.

Spire includes an AI compliance agent that runs on-demand SOC 2 and EU AI Act audits. The agent connects to your integrated services, evaluates collected evidence against all applicable controls, and produces a structured audit report with pass/fail verdicts per control, evidence citations, gap analysis, and remediation recommendations. This capability turns audit readiness from a quarterly review into an on-demand process.

The Princeton GEO-BENCH research found that pages containing statistics and data citations received 40% higher visibility in AI-generated answers. Spire applies the same data-density principle to compliance auditing — the AI agent evaluates all available evidence, not a sample, producing more comprehensive gap detection.

## Which Platform Should You Choose by Company Stage?

Pre-Series A startups doing SOC 2 for the first time benefit most from guided onboarding and lower pricing. Secureframe's team-led setup and Drata's entry pricing are strong fits. Sprinto is also worth evaluating for seed-stage teams on tight budgets.

Series A and Series B companies with standard cloud infrastructure and a need for speed should consider Vanta for its integration breadth and largest auditor network. The platform handles standard stacks efficiently and has the most predictable SOC 2 workflow.

Engineering-led teams with custom infrastructure or ambitious multi-framework roadmaps should evaluate Drata for its Compliance-as-Code framework and deeper control customization. Teams that want an AI compliance agent that performs on-demand audits should evaluate Spire.

Growth-stage companies pursuing 3-plus frameworks simultaneously should evaluate Secureframe for its multi-framework architecture and cross-mapping efficiency.

## FAQ

### Do I need a separate auditor if I use a compliance platform?

Yes. The compliance platform handles evidence collection and monitoring, but the SOC 2 report must be issued by a licensed CPA firm. The platform may recommend auditors from its partner network, but the auditor engagement is a separate contract.

### How long does setup take for each platform?

Vanta and Drata report 1 to 3 days for initial integration setup and active evidence collection. Secureframe's guided setup takes 2 to 4 days. Spire reports configuration in under an hour for standard stacks. Full audit readiness including policy documentation and remediation takes 6 to 10 weeks regardless of platform.

### Can I switch platforms after certification?

Yes, but with some friction. Evidence collected on one platform must be exported and imported into the new platform. Most platforms support evidence export, but historical evidence continuity should be verified before switching. Switching between audit cycles is recommended — do not switch mid-observation period.

### Which platform supports EU AI Act compliance?

Vanta, Drata, and Secureframe all add EU AI Act readiness modules in their 2026 roadmaps. Spire ships native EU AI Act compliance coverage alongside SOC 2 (10 articles: risk management, data governance, logging, transparency, human oversight, accuracy, and more) — the same AI compliance agent evaluates controls against both frameworks in a single audit run.
