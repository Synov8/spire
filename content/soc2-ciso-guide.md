SOC 2 certification is the most widely adopted security standard for B2B SaaS companies, with a 2026 ISACA survey finding that 86% of CISOs at SaaS companies consider SOC 2 their primary compliance framework. For CISOs, SOC 2 is not just about passing an audit — it provides a structured approach to building a mature security program. This guide covers SOC 2 from the CISO's perspective: control design, evidence strategy, continuous monitoring, and auditor management.

## How Should CISOs Design SOC 2 Controls?

Design controls that are effective (achieve the control objective), efficient (minimal operational overhead), and auditable (clear evidence trail). Classify controls into automated (preventive controls that operate continuously), semi-automated (controls with some manual steps), and manual (fully human-performed). Automate as many controls as possible — automated controls produce continuous evidence and reduce audit friction. Map each control to its underlying policy and the specific trust service criterion it satisfies.

## What Evidence Strategy Should CISOs Implement?

Implement a three-tier evidence strategy: continuous evidence (logs, automated scans, CI/CD pipeline artifacts), periodic evidence (access reviews, vulnerability scans, training completion), and on-demand evidence (policy acknowledgements, incident reports, change requests). Centralize evidence collection in a compliance platform. Establish evidence retention periods aligned with your data retention policy. Validate evidence quality before each audit.

## How Do CISOs Build Auditor Relationships?

Engage your SOC 2 auditor early — ideally 6-12 months before the first audit. Schedule a pre-audit readiness assessment to identify gaps. Maintain open communication about scope changes, control modifications, and incident history. Provide proactive evidence access rather than waiting for auditor requests. Treat the audit as a collaborative improvement exercise rather than an adversarial examination. Good auditor relationships reduce audit time by 20-30%.

## What Continuous Monitoring Program Satisfies CISO Requirements?

Implement continuous control monitoring covering: vulnerability scanning (weekly), threat detection (real-time through SIEM), user access reviews (quarterly), change management (continuous through CI/CD), incident response (continuous), and third-party vendor monitoring (quarterly). Automate alerts for control failures with defined remediation SLAs. Generate monthly compliance dashboards for board reporting. Continuous monitoring transforms SOC 2 from a point-in-time certification to an ongoing security program.

## How Do CISOs Scope SOC 2 Effectively?

Define scope boundaries based on: systems that process customer data, infrastructure supporting those systems, teams with access to customer data, and third-party vendors with customer data access. Document scope in your system description and risk assessment. Use scope as a competitive advantage — a narrow scope speeds certification but may limit enterprise sales if prospects want broader coverage. Reassess scope annually.

## What Metrics Should CISOs Track?

Key SOC 2 metrics for CISO reporting: control effectiveness rate (target 95%+ automated controls passing), mean time to remediate control failures (target under 24 hours for critical), evidence collection coverage (100% of controls with evidence), audit finding trends (year-over-year improvement), and third-party vendor risk acceptance counts. Present these metrics in monthly security reviews and board reporting.
