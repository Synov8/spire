NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 — released in February 2024 with the new Govern function — provides a risk-based cybersecurity framework that 65% of US enterprise buyers reference in their security requirements, and SaaS companies that map their SOC 2 controls to NIST CSF 2.0 complete security questionnaires 40% faster according to a 2025 NIST adoption survey. CSF 2.0 is not a certification but a framework that demonstrates security maturity.

## What Are the Six NIST CSF 2.0 Functions?

Govern (GV): new in CSF 2.0 — establishes cybersecurity governance, risk management strategy, and supply chain risk management. Identify (ID): asset management, business environment, risk assessment. Protect (PR): access control, awareness training, data security, platform security. Detect (DE): continuous monitoring, anomaly detection, event analysis. Respond (RS): incident response planning, communications, mitigation. Recover (RC): recovery planning, communications, post-incident improvement.

## How Does NIST CSF 2.0 Map to SOC 2?

Govern maps to SOC 2 CC1.x and CC2.x (control environment, information and communication). Identify maps to CC3.x (risk assessment) and CC9.x (vendor risk). Protect maps to CC6.x (access control, encryption, network security). Detect maps to CC7.x (monitoring, anomaly detection). Respond maps to CC7.4 and CC7.5 (incident response). Recover maps to A1.x (availability, disaster recovery).

## What Implementation Approach Works for SaaS?

Most SaaS companies implement NIST CSF 2.0 at Tier 2 (Risk-Informed) or Tier 3 (Repeatable). Tier 2 is achievable with SOC 2-level controls. Tier 3 requires additional maturity in governance, supply chain risk management, and cyber threat intelligence integration.

## FAQ

### Is NIST CSF 2.0 required for SaaS companies?

NIST CSF 2.0 is not mandatory for SaaS companies (unless serving US federal government as a contractor). However, enterprise buyers increasingly reference CSF 2.0 in security questionnaires, and demonstrating CSF alignment provides competitive differentiation.

### Can NIST CSF 2.0 replace SOC 2?

No. SOC 2 is an attestation report issued by a CPA firm. NIST CSF 2.0 is a voluntary framework. They serve different purposes. CSF 2.0 provides a risk management structure; SOC 2 provides audited assurance.

### What is the cost of NIST CSF 2.0 implementation?

Implementation cost for a SaaS startup: $5,000 to $20,000 in internal time for gap analysis, policy updates, and mapping. No external audit cost unless pursuing a third-party assessment. Most SaaS companies implement CSF 2.0 as a byproduct of SOC 2 compliance.
