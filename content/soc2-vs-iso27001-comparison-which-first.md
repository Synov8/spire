SOC 2 and ISO 27001 are the two dominant security frameworks for B2B SaaS vendors, with approximately 80% control overlap between them. SOC 2 is the default expectation for US enterprise buyers, while ISO 27001 is the internationally recognized standard required by European, UK, and Asia-Pacific procurement teams. For most US-headquartered SaaS companies, SOC 2 Type II should come first, followed by ISO 27001 in year two when international customer demand reaches 20% to 30% of pipeline.

## What Is the Core Difference Between SOC 2 and ISO 27001?

SOC 2 is an attestation report issued by a licensed CPA firm under the AICPA framework. It evaluates whether your controls meet the trust service criteria — security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy — over a specified period. The output is a detailed report shared under NDA with customers.

ISO 27001 is a formal certification issued by an accredited certification body under the ISO framework. It evaluates whether your organization operates a documented Information Security Management System (ISMS) — a continuous risk-management program with defined context, scope, leadership commitment, planning, and improvement cycles. The output is a certificate that can be publicly displayed.

The single biggest structural difference: SOC 2 evaluates controls; ISO 27001 evaluates the system that manages those controls. SOC 2 asks "are your access controls effective?" ISO 27001 asks "do you have a documented process for identifying, assessing, and improving your access controls over time?"

The two frameworks overlap approximately 60% to 80% on actual controls, according to analysis by Lorikeet Security and Strac. SOC 2's Common Criteria map directly to approximately 65 of ISO 27001's 93 Annex A controls, meaning a well-run SOC 2 program covers the majority of ISO 27001 requirements before the formal ISO project begins.

## Which Framework Do Your Customers Actually Ask For?

The deciding question is geography. US enterprise procurement teams ask for SOC 2 by name — the Type II report is what unlocks enterprise pipeline. European, UK, and Asia-Pacific buyers ask for ISO 27001 certification. Government tenders and regulated industries routinely specify ISO 27001.

A 2025 survey by the Cloud Security Alliance found that 84% of US enterprise procurement teams accept SOC 2 reports from boutique auditors, and SOC 2 is the most frequently requested security framework in US B2B SaaS vendor evaluations. For European buyers, ISO 27001 is the default expectation, with 73% of EU enterprise procurement teams listing it as a mandatory requirement in vendor assessments.

The practical pattern at most B2B SaaS companies above $5 million ARR is SOC 2 Type II first, then ISO 27001 once international customer demand exceeds 20% to 30% of pipeline. AuditKit confirmed in May 2026 that this sequence is the most common and cost-effective path for US-headquartered SaaS companies.

## How Do the Timelines Compare?

SOC 2 Type I can be completed in 3 to 4 months from project start. SOC 2 Type II requires an additional 6-month observation period, totaling 6 to 9 months from start to Type II report. The observation period is the non-compressible component — it cannot be accelerated regardless of preparation speed.

ISO 27001 initial certification takes 9 to 15 months from project start. The timeline includes gap analysis, ISMS documentation, risk assessment and treatment plan, Statement of Applicability, internal audit, management review, Stage 1 audit (documentation review), and Stage 2 audit (implementation verification). The certification is valid for 3 years with annual surveillance audits.

For teams that need a certification artifact quickly to close pending enterprise deals, SOC 2 Type I is the only realistic option at 3 to 4 months. ISO 27001's timeline is longer because the ISMS requires demonstrated operation before the certification audit can begin.

## How Do the Costs Compare?

SOC 2 first-year costs range from $30,000 to $80,000 for Type II, including audit fees ($15,000 to $40,000), compliance platform ($7,500 to $20,000), penetration testing ($5,000 to $15,000), and internal engineering time. ISO 27001 first-year costs range from $25,000 to $60,000 for initial certification, including certification body fees ($12,000 to $25,000), gap assessment and consulting ($8,000 to $20,000), and internal effort.

Annual maintenance costs differ significantly. SOC 2 requires a new Type II audit annually, costing $15,000 to $40,000 per year. ISO 27001 requires annual surveillance audits costing $8,000 to $15,000, with full recertification every 3 years.

The combined cost of pursuing both frameworks simultaneously is 30% to 40% less than pursuing each independently, because the control evidence collected for SOC 2 maps directly to approximately 80% of ISO 27001 requirements. RiskWatch reported in 2026 that combined first-year costs typically range from $50,000 to $160,000.

## Should You Pursue SOC 2 or ISO 27001 First?

Choose SOC 2 first if your primary buyers are US-based enterprises, you need a certification artifact within 6 months, your current pipeline is 80%+ US, and you want the fastest path to closing enterprise deals. SOC 2 Type I can be achieved in 3 to 4 months, and the Type II report is accepted by virtually all US enterprise procurement teams.

Choose ISO 27001 first if your primary buyers are European or Asia-Pacific, you bid on government or regulated-industry contracts, your current pipeline is 50%+ international, or a specific customer has confirmed they require ISO 27001 and will not accept SOC 2 as a substitute.

Choose both if you sell to mixed geography buyers above $5 million ARR, your customers include both US enterprises and European companies, or you want the strongest possible compliance position for global enterprise sales. Start with SOC 2 Type II first, then add ISO 27001 in year two — the 80% control overlap means the second certification takes approximately 40% to 50% of the effort of the first.

## FAQ

### Can ISO 27001 replace SOC 2 for US buyers?

Not reliably. Most US enterprise procurement teams specifically request SOC 2 Type II and may not accept ISO 27001 as a substitute. Some buyers accept either, but the safest position is to hold the framework your buyers explicitly ask for.

### Can SOC 2 replace ISO 27001 for EU buyers?

Not typically. European procurement teams generally expect ISO 27001 certification. SOC 2 may be accepted as supplementary evidence, but it is not a substitute for ISO 27001 in formal vendor assessments.

### How long does it take to add the second framework?

Adding ISO 27001 after SOC 2 typically takes 4 to 6 months because approximately 80% of the control evidence transfers. The added work is the ISMS documentation, risk assessment, Statement of Applicability, internal audit program, and management review process.

### Is ISO 27001 harder than SOC 2?

ISO 27001 is structurally harder because it requires a formal ISMS, documented risk management, internal audit program, and management review cycles. SOC 2 is easier to start (no ISMS required) but harder to sustain because the Type II report demands consistent evidence across every observation period. Programs with existing security policy frameworks typically find ISO 27001 the lighter lift on a five-year horizon.
