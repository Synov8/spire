Google's Vendor Security Assessment Questionnaire (VSAQ) is one of the most widely adopted vendor security assessment frameworks, used by Google itself and hundreds of enterprise organizations as their standard vendor risk assessment, and companies that maintain VSAQ-ready responses reduce security review cycle time by 50% for Google-as-customer deals according to a 2025 Cloud Security Alliance analysis. The VSAQ covers 60+ questions across 7 security domains.

## What Are the 7 VSAQ Domains?

Domain 1 — Information Security Program: company security organization, policies, certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001), risk management process. Domain 2 — Access Control: user provisioning and deprovisioning, MFA enforcement, access review frequency, privilege management. Domain 3 — Data Encryption: encryption at rest standards, encryption in transit (TLS version minimum), key management practices.

Domain 4 — Incident Response: incident response plan existence and testing, breach notification timeline, forensic investigation capability. Domain 5 — Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery: BCP and DR plan existence, RTO and RPO targets, DR testing frequency. Domain 6 — Vendor and Third-Party Management: vendor assessment process, subservice organization management, supply chain security. Domain 7 — Data Privacy and Protection: data classification, data retention and deletion, privacy notices, data subject rights.

## What Is the VSAQ Scoring Methodology?

Each VSAQ answer is scored on a maturity scale: Not Implemented, Partially Implemented, Largely Implemented, and Fully Implemented. The overall assessment produces a risk score. Google recommends vendors achieve "Largely Implemented" or "Fully Implemented" across all domains. Scores in any domain below "Largely Implemented" typically trigger follow-up questions or additional evidence requests.

## How Should You Prepare for a VSAQ Assessment?

Prepare a VSAQ response library with specific answers for each domain, evidence artifacts supporting key claims (policy documents, SOC 2 report, pen test results, training records), and a response playbook for follow-up questions. Target "Largely Implemented" or higher across all domains.

## FAQ

### Is VSAQ scoring consistent across all Google divisions?

VSAQ scoring methodology is standardized, but individual Google divisions may apply different risk thresholds. In general, Google Cloud has the most stringent VSAQ requirements followed by Google Ads.

### How long does a VSAQ assessment take to complete?

VSAQ completion typically takes 1 to 3 hours for a prepared team with existing response materials. First-time completion without prepared responses takes 4 to 8 hours.

### Does passing VSAQ guarantee Google as a customer?

No. VSAQ is a security due diligence tool, not a sales qualification. Passing VSAQ satisfies the security review requirement but does not guarantee a business relationship.
