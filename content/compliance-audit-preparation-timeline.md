The 12 weeks before SOC 2 fieldwork determine your audit outcome — a 2026 AICPA audit readiness study found that organizations completing a structured 12-week preparation cycle had 60% fewer audit findings than those who started preparation less than 4 weeks before fieldwork. This timeline provides a week-by-week countdown to SOC 2 audit fieldwork.

## Week 12-10: Pre-Audit Readiness Assessment

Week 12: conduct a pre-audit readiness assessment. Review all controls and identify any gaps. Inventory all evidence artifacts. Identify missing, incomplete, or stale evidence. Week 11: begin remediation of identified gaps. Update any policies that are past their review date. Start collecting any missing evidence. Week 10: complete readiness assessment and gap remediation. Verify all evidence is collected and organized by control. Provide preliminary evidence index to your auditor.

## Week 9-7: Evidence Finalization and Organization

Week 9: finalize evidence collection for full audit period. Organize evidence by control in your compliance platform. Verify evidence quality — timestamps clear, descriptions accurate, controls identifiable. Week 8: conduct internal evidence quality review. Ask control owners to verify their evidence. Fill any remaining gaps. Week 7: close evidence collection. Run an evidence coverage report showing 100% of controls with complete evidence. Provide evidence access to your auditor.

## Week 6-4: Employee Preparation and Scheduling

Week 6: identify employees who will be interviewed by the auditor. Schedule interview times. Conduct employee interview preparation sessions covering how to answer auditor questions. Week 5: provide interview guides to participating employees. Conduct practice interviews with key control owners. Finalize interview schedule with auditor. Week 4: confirm all interviews are scheduled. Verify all attendees are aware of their scheduled times. Prepare the auditor workspace and evidence access.

## Week 3-1: Final Readiness

Week 3: conduct final evidence review. Verify all controls have complete, current evidence. Test evidence access from auditor perspective. Week 2: conduct pre-audit walkthrough with compliance team and control owners. Review emergency procedures for unexpected findings. Prepare opening presentation materials. Week 1: confirm fieldwork schedule with auditor. Brief executive team on audit process and expectations. Prepare for opening meeting. Get a good night's sleep — you're ready.

## Opening Day Checklist

On the first day of fieldwork: confirm auditor has evidence system access. Present opening presentation covering system description and scope changes. Make control owners available for interviews. Establish communication channels (Slack, email) for auditor questions during fieldwork. Set expectations for response times (within 4 hours for standard requests, 24 hours for complex requests). Begin fieldwork with confidence.
