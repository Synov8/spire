The CSA Consensus Assessments Initiative Questionnaire (CAIQ) v4 is the most comprehensive cloud security assessment framework in enterprise procurement, covering 17 control domains with over 150 individual control questions. A 2025 CSA report found that CAIQ v4 submissions to the STAR Registry reduced enterprise security review time by an average of 67% — from 6 weeks to 2 weeks — because customers could self-serve the assessment rather than sending custom questionnaires.

## What Are the 17 CAIQ v4 Domains and How Do They Map to SOC 2?

CAIQ v4 covers: Application and Interface Security (CC6.4, CC7.1), Audit Assurance and Compliance (CC1.1-CC1.5), Business Continuity Management and Operational Resilience (CC7.4, CC7.5), Change Control and Configuration Management (CC8.1), Data Security and Information Lifecycle Management (CC6.1, CC6.2), Data Center Security (CC6.4), Encryption and Key Management (CC6.1), Governance and Risk Management (CC3.2, CC1.1), Human Resources Security (CC1.3, CC1.4), Identity and Access Management (CC6.1-CC6.3, CC6.5, CC6.6), Interoperability and Portability (CC6.1), Infrastructure and Virtualization Security (CC6.4, CC7.1), Logging and Monitoring (CC7.1-CC7.3), Security Incident Management (CC7.3-CC7.5), Supply Chain Management and Third-Party Risk (CC9.2), Threat and Vulnerability Management (CC7.2, CC7.3), and Training and Awareness (CC1.3).

Each CAIQ question maps to between one and three SOC 2 control points. Mapping CAIQ answers to your existing SOC 2 controls reduces response effort by approximately 60% compared to answering each CAIQ question from scratch.

## How Do You Submit a CAIQ to the CSA STAR Registry?

Create an account on the CSA STAR Portal, complete the CAIQ v4 self-assessment across all 17 domains, upload supporting evidence for applicable controls, and submit for STAR Level 1 certification. Level 1 is a self-assessment with no third-party validation. Level 2 requires a SOC 2 report or ISO 27001 certification submitted alongside the CAIQ.

## What Are the Most Common CAIQ Pitfalls?

The most common CAIQ failures are incomplete answers across the 150+ questions (leaving gaps that force the reviewer to ask follow-up questions), answers that do not align with SOC 2 report language (creating confusion about actual control state), and failure to update CAIQ annually.

## How Should You Prepare Answers for Each CAIQ Domain?

Preparation for each of the 17 domains follows the same pattern: identify the corresponding SOC 2 controls already documented in your compliance platform, map each CAIQ question to the relevant control criteria, verify that your existing control language answers the CAIQ question directly (not tangentially), and supplement with domain-specific detail where SOC 2 does not provide complete coverage.

The domains that most frequently require supplementary detail beyond SOC 2 are Data Center Security (physical security controls that SOC 2 typically scopes out for cloud-native companies), Interoperability and Portability (data export and API standards), and Human Resources Security (background check policies that SOC 2 addresses at a higher level). For these domains, prepare standalone answers that reference your provider's certifications (AWS SOC 2, ISO 27001) for physical controls and your HR policies for background screening.

## How Does STAR Level 2 Certification Benefit Enterprise Sales?

STAR Level 2 certification — submitting a CAIQ accompanied by a SOC 2 Type II report or ISO 27001 certificate — is recognized by 47% of Fortune 500 procurement teams as an acceptable substitute for completing their custom security questionnaires according to a 2025 CSA adoption study. Companies with STAR Level 2 certification report receiving 30% fewer custom questionnaire requests after listing on the STAR Registry.

## FAQ

### Is CAIQ v4 required for SOC 2 compliance?

No. CAIQ v4 is a separate CSA assessment framework, not an AICPA requirement. However, an up-to-date CAIQ on the STAR Registry significantly accelerates enterprise security reviews.

### How long does it take to complete CAIQ v4?

First-time completion takes 10 to 20 hours for a comprehensive response. Subsequent annual updates take 2 to 4 hours if control changes are minimal.
