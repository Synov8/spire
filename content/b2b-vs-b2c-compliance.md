SOC 2 certification is overwhelmingly a B2B requirement — a 2026 Gartner procurement survey found that 72% of enterprise procurement teams require SOC 2 from vendors, while only 12% of consumer purchasing decisions are influenced by vendor SOC 2 certification. However, B2C companies face different compliance pressures from privacy regulations, payment card requirements, and consumer data protection laws. This guide compares B2B and B2C compliance requirements.

## Why Is SOC 2 Critical for B2B but Not B2C?

B2B enterprise procurement teams use SOC 2 as a vendor risk management tool — it provides third-party validation that security controls are effective. B2C companies rarely face customer demand for SOC 2 because consumers don't evaluate their vendors' SOC 2 reports before purchasing. B2B companies should pursue SOC 2 as a revenue requirement. B2C companies should prioritize privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA) and PCI DSS over SOC 2.

## What Compliance Frameworks Matter for B2C?

B2C companies should prioritize: privacy regulations (GDPR in EU, CCPA/CPRA in California, LGPD in Brazil), PCI DSS for payment processing, ePrivacy Directive and cookie consent laws, consumer protection laws relevant to their industry, and data breach notification laws. SOC 2 may still be valuable for B2C companies that process significant amounts of consumer PII or that want to demonstrate security maturity to regulators.

## What About Hybrid B2B/B2C Companies?

Hybrid companies face the compliance requirements of both models. Recommended approach: pursue SOC 2 for your B2B market requirements while simultaneously implementing privacy compliance for consumer data. Scope your SOC 2 to cover only the systems that process B2B customer data, and exclude pure consumer-facing systems. Ensure your privacy compliance covers consumer PII handling. Maintain separate data handling procedures for B2B and B2C data.

## How Do Contractual Requirements Differ?

B2B contracts typically include: SOC 2 certification as contractual requirement, right to audit clauses, security addenda with specific control commitments, data processing agreements, liability provisions for security incidents, and breach notification SLAs. B2C contracts (terms of service) include: privacy policy commitments, data handling disclosures, and consent mechanisms — but rarely reference SOC 2.

## How Does the Compliance Burden Compare?

B2B compliance burden: high initial SOC 2 investment ($30K-$100K first year), ongoing evidence collection and monitoring, annual audit cycle, frequent customer security questionnaires (8-12 hours per deal), and vendor risk assessment responses. B2C compliance burden: privacy compliance (GDPR, CCPA) implementation ($50K-$150K depending on scale), consent management infrastructure, data subject request handling, and ongoing privacy program maintenance. The costs are comparable but allocated differently.

## What Evidence Should Each Type Prepare?

B2C companies preparing for SOC 2: consumer PII protection controls, consent management audit trails, data subject request handling procedures, privacy policy enforcement evidence, and data deletion workflows. B2B SOC 2 evidence is more focused on enterprise requirements: change management, access control, vendor management, and incident response. Frame your SOC 2 evidence based on your primary customer type.
