Manual SOC 2 compliance — collecting evidence through quarterly screenshots and managing spreadsheets — costs 200 to 400 engineering hours and takes 12 to 18 months for first-time Type II certification. Compliance automation with Spire reduces the timeline to 6 to 9 months, cuts engineering time by approximately 80%, and replaces the quarterly evidence scramble with continuous automated collection. The question is not whether automation is worth it — it is whether your team can afford the opportunity cost of doing SOC 2 manually.

## How Many Engineering Hours Does Manual SOC 2 Consume?

Manual SOC 2 compliance requires 200 to 400 engineering and operations hours for the first certification cycle, according to a 2025 AICPA analysis. At a fully-loaded engineering cost of $100 to $180 per hour, this represents $20,000 to $72,000 in opportunity cost — the single largest hidden cost in a manual SOC 2 program.

The engineering time breaks down across five activities: policy documentation and control mapping (40 to 80 hours), evidence collection and organization (60 to 120 hours), remediation of identified gaps (50 to 100 hours), auditor communication and evidence requests (30 to 60 hours), and ongoing maintenance between audit cycles (20 to 40 hours per quarter).

Companies using compliance automation reduce these totals by 60% to 80%. A 2025 AICPA study found that automated evidence collection eliminated 83% of the evidence gaps that cause audit delays, and automated control monitoring reduced remediation time by 70%.

## What Is the Timeline Difference Between Manual and Automated SOC 2?

The timeline difference is driven by two factors: readiness preparation and evidence continuity. Manual readiness preparation takes 4 to 6 months because every policy must be written from scratch, every control must be manually mapped to evidence sources, and evidence collection procedures must be designed and tested.

Automated readiness preparation takes 2 to 4 weeks because the platform provides pre-built policy templates mapped to SOC 2 controls, discovers evidence sources automatically through API connections, and begins collecting time-stamped evidence from the moment of configuration.

The Type II observation period — 6 months minimum — is the same in both approaches. But manual programs typically waste 2 to 4 months between readiness completion and observation period start because evidence collection processes are not yet reliable. Automation eliminates this gap by starting continuous collection on day one.

Total timeline: manual SOC 2 Type II takes 12 to 18 months from start to signed report. Automated SOC 2 Type II with Spire takes 6 to 9 months. The difference is almost entirely in the readiness and remediation phases.

## What Are the Hidden Costs of Manual SOC 2?

The hidden costs of manual SOC 2 fall into four categories beyond the obvious engineering time. Evidence gaps are the most expensive — missing or incomplete evidence forces the auditor to extend fieldwork, adding 2 to 4 weeks and $5,000 to $15,000 in additional audit fees. The 60% of first-time audits that experience significant delays typically do so because of manual evidence failures.

Policy drift is the second hidden cost. Manually documented policies that do not match actual practices create findings during fieldwork. The remediation of a policy gap discovered during the audit is significantly more expensive than fixing it before the observation period begins.

Staff turnover is the third cost. Manual SOC 2 knowledge lives in the head of the engineer who set it up. When that engineer leaves, the evidence collection process breaks, and the next cycle starts from scratch. Automation platforms store configuration and evidence independently of individual staff members.

The fourth cost is the opportunity cost of delayed enterprise sales. Every month that SOC 2 certification is delayed is a month that enterprise deals remain blocked. For a company with $50,000 to $100,000 in average contract value, a 3-month delay represents significant pipeline risk.

## FAQ

### Can a very small team do SOC 2 manually?

A team with fewer than 15 employees doing SOC 2 for the first time may find manual evidence collection feasible for Type I, but Type II requires consistent evidence across 6 to 12 months that is difficult to maintain manually. Most teams find that automation pays for itself in engineering time savings alone within the first certification cycle.

### What is the cheapest way to get SOC 2 certified?

The cheapest path is: narrow scope to Security TSC only, use an entry-tier compliance platform ($6,000/year), choose a boutique CPA firm ($10,000 to $15,000 for Type II), and have an engineering lead manage compliance part-time. Total first-year cost: $20,000 to $35,000.

### Does manual SOC 2 work for companies with simple infrastructure?

Manual evidence collection can work for companies with very simple infrastructure (one cloud provider, one SaaS identity provider, less than 10 employees). However, as soon as the company grows beyond 10 employees or adds a second cloud provider, the evidence collection burden increases non-linearly and automation becomes the cost-effective choice.

### How much questionnaire time does automation save?

Manual security questionnaire responses take 20 to 40 hours per questionnaire. AI-powered automation reduces this to 2 to 5 minutes for the initial generation plus 1 to 4 hours for review. Companies receiving 8 to 12 questionnaires per quarter save 150 to 400 engineering hours annually.
