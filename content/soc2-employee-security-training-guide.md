SOC 2 controls CC1.3 (commitment to ethical values and integrity) and CC1.4 (management philosophy and operating style) require documented security awareness training for all employees, with evidence of completion and demonstrated understanding. The 2025 Verizon DBIR found that 68% of data breaches involved a human element — phishing, credential misuse, or configuration errors — making employee security training one of the highest-impact controls in the SOC 2 framework for reducing actual security risk.

## What Training Content Does SOC 2 Require?

The training program must cover at minimum: phishing and social engineering awareness (how to identify and report suspicious communications), data handling procedures (classification levels and permitted data handling), incident reporting procedures (how and when to report security concerns), password and authentication requirements (MFA usage, password managers, credential security), and acceptable use policy (permitted use of company systems and data).

Content should be role-tailored. Engineering teams need additional training on secure coding practices and vulnerability management. Customer support teams need additional training on handling customer data and privacy requirements. Executive teams need additional training on governance and regulatory responsibilities.

## How Often Must Training Be Completed?

The SOC 2 minimum is annual security awareness training for all employees. Quarterly phishing simulations supplemented by annual training is the industry standard that most SOC 2 auditors expect for B2B SaaS companies.

New employees must complete security awareness training within 30 days of hire. A 2025 KnowBe4 study found that 34% of data breaches involving new employees occurred within the first 90 days — before they had completed security training programs.

## What Training Completion Rate Is Acceptable?

SOC 2 auditors expect 90% or higher training completion rates. Completion rates below 90% typically trigger a control finding. Rates below 80% often result in an exception being noted in the SOC 2 Type II report. Automated training platforms with reminder escalation help maintain completion rates — most platforms achieve 95%+ with automated reminders and manager escalation.

## What Training Evidence Must Be Collected?

The auditor needs a security training policy documenting the program requirements, training content inventory or curriculum description, completion records for all employees covering the Type II period, new hire training completion within 30 days, and phishing simulation results if applicable.

## FAQ

### Does SOC 2 require phishing simulations?

Phishing simulations are not explicitly required by SOC 2 but are strongly recommended and expected by most SOC 2 auditors for companies with Access Security scope. Simulations provide evidence of employee security awareness that annual training alone does not demonstrate.

### Does training need to be documented in writing?

Training content and completion records must be documented. In-person verbal training without a documented curriculum or completion tracking will not satisfy the auditor's evidence requirements.

### Do contractors need security training?

Contractors with system access must complete security training equivalent to employee training. The contractor's employer may provide training if it meets the same content requirements.
