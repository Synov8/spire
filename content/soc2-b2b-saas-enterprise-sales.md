B2B SaaS companies with SOC 2 Type II certification close enterprise deals 3 to 5 months faster and at 15% to 25% higher ACV than uncertified competitors, according to a 2025 TrustArc pricing analysis. For companies selling to enterprises with $100K+ ACV deals, SOC 2 Type II certification is effectively mandatory — 74% of enterprise buyers require it before scheduling a security review, and the absence of SOC 2 adds 4 to 12 weeks of due diligence to every sales cycle.

## When Should a B2B SaaS Company Start SOC 2?

The optimal time to begin SOC 2 is when at least 25% of the sales pipeline comes from deals that trigger security review requirements. Premature SOC 2 certification before product-market fit diverts engineering resources from product development. Delaying SOC 2 past the point where enterprise deals are being lost due to certification gaps leaves revenue on the table.

For most B2B SaaS companies, the right timing is between $500K and $2M ARR — early enough to capture enterprise deal value, late enough that the company has the operational maturity to maintain controls effectively.

## Should You Start With Type I or Type II?

Companies with immediate enterprise deal pressure should start SOC 2 Type I first (2 to 4 months to completion) to satisfy initial customer requirements, while beginning the Type II observation period in parallel. Companies with 6 to 9 months before enterprise deals close should go directly to Type II. Direct Type II saves $8,000 to $15,000 in duplicative audit costs and 2 to 3 months of administrative overhead.

Type I reports satisfy approximately 40% of enterprise procurement requirements. Type II reports satisfy approximately 90% of enterprise requirements.

## What SOC 2 Scope Do Enterprise Customers Actually Require?

The Security trust service criterion is universally required. The Confidentiality criterion is required for companies handling non-public sensitive information. The Availability criterion is required when contractual SLAs include uptime commitments. The Processing Integrity criterion is required for companies handling financial transactions or data transformation where accuracy is critical. The Privacy criterion is required when the company processes personal data regulated by GDPR, CCPA, or similar privacy laws.

Over-scoping is a common mistake. Adding criteria beyond what customers require increases the control set by 15 to 25 controls per additional criterion, increasing audit cost by 20% to 30%.

## What Sales Enablement Materials Support SOC 2?

Prepare: a one-page SOC 2 summary for non-technical procurement stakeholders, the full SOC 2 Type II report available under NDA, a trust center for prospect self-service, a list of control mappings to common security questionnaires (SIG, CAIQ), and customer references willing to discuss the SOC 2 value in their procurement.

## FAQ

### Can smaller B2B SaaS companies skip SOC 2 if they have ISO 27001?

For US enterprise sales, SOC 2 Type II is required regardless of ISO 27001 certification. US enterprise procurement teams are trained to ask for SOC 2 and often do not accept ISO 27001 as a substitute.

### How do you handle the 6-month Type II observation period when a deal is closing?

Offer the prospective customer a Type I report (if available) or a security assessment report showing current control state, with a contractual commitment to provide the Type II report within 12 months of signing. Most enterprise buyers accept this with contractual language.
