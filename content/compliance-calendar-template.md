A well-structured compliance calendar ensures that no SOC 2 activity falls through the cracks — a 2026 Compliance Week study found that organizations with annual compliance calendars are 2.3x more likely to complete all pre-audit activities before fieldwork begins. This template provides a month-by-month schedule of compliance activities including evidence collection, policy reviews, testing, and auditor communication.

## January: Annual Compliance Planning

Review and update: annual risk assessment, compliance roadmap for the year, and control owner assignments for the coming year. Schedule annual penetration test for Q2. Begin planning: annual business continuity test for Q3, employee security training updates for Q2, and auditor engagement for the year.

## February: Policy Review and Training

Conduct annual policy review cycle: information security policy, acceptable use policy, data classification policy, incident response policy, business continuity policy, and vendor management policy. Begin employee security awareness training rollout. Schedule quarterly internal audit dates. Update system description if there have been material changes.

## March: First Quarter Compliance Activities

Complete Q1 access review. Perform Q1 vulnerability scan. Review Q1 change management records for completeness. Complete employee training by March 31. Conduct quarterly internal control testing. Verify vendor SOC 2 reports are current and collect any missing reports.

## April: Penetration Testing and Pre-Audit Prep

Conduct annual penetration test. Begin pre-audit readiness assessment. Identify and remediate control gaps. Prepare audit evidence packages. Contact audit firm to schedule fieldwork dates. Review penetration test findings and remediate critical and high findings.

## May: Second Quarter and Audit Preparation

Complete Q2 access review. Perform Q2 vulnerability scan. Finalize audit evidence organization. Conduct pre-audit walkthrough with internal team. Remediate any remaining penetration test findings. Verify all policies are current and acknowledged. Complete any outstanding training.

## June: Annual SOC 2 Audit Fieldwork

Conduct annual SOC 2 audit fieldwork. Make employees available for auditor interviews. Provide evidence requests promptly. Attend opening and closing meetings. Address auditor questions within 24 hours. Review preliminary findings with the audit team.

## July: Post-Audit Remediation

Address any audit findings with remediation plans. Implement corrective actions for identified deficiencies. Provide remediation evidence to auditor. Obtain final audit report. Share results with leadership and board. Update compliance program based on lessons learned.

## August: Third Quarter and Mid-Year Review

Complete Q3 access review. Perform Q3 vulnerability scan. Conduct mid-year compliance program effectiveness review. Update risk assessment if there have been material changes. Review vendor risk management program status. Begin third-party and vendor SOC 2 report collection.

## September: Business Continuity Testing

Conduct annual business continuity and disaster recovery test. Document test results with evidence of successful recovery. Update BCP/DR plan if needed based on test results. Review and update escalation contact lists. Test incident response procedures.

## October: Fourth Quarter Readiness

Complete Q4 access review (final of the year). Perform Q4 vulnerability scan (final of the year). Begin evidence collection preparation for next year's audit. Review control effectiveness trends from the past year. Update your compliance calendar based on experience and lessons learned. Verify all vendors have current SOC 2 reports.

## November: Annual Compliance Review

Complete annual compliance program effectiveness assessment. Review all policies for annual updates. Update system description for any changes during the year. Conduct annual risk assessment update. Review and update incident response procedures. Prepare annual compliance report for board.

## December: Year-End Close and Planning

Complete any remaining annual activities. Archive all evidence from the past year. Verify all access reviews are completed for the year. Confirm all training is current. Confirm all vendor assessments are current. Begin planning next year's compliance calendar. Celebrate another year of successful compliance.
