A structured pre-audit walkthrough — completed 4 to 6 weeks before auditor fieldwork begins — reduces the likelihood of audit exceptions by 55% according to a 2025 AICPA audit quality study. The pre-audit checklist covers policy readiness, evidence completeness, control operation verification, and stakeholder alignment. Each item should be verified, not assumed.

## Policy Documentation Verification

Verify all 12 to 18 required policies are approved and current, each policy has a version number and revision history, approval signatures are dated within the last 12 months, policies reference the specific SOC 2 controls they support, and the information security policy is signed by the CEO or equivalent executive.

## Evidence Collection Verification

Verify evidence is collected for every control in scope, automated evidence collection runs are completing without errors, manual evidence is stored with timestamps and reviewer signatures, evidence retention covers the full observation period, and evidence can be mapped to specific controls in a structured format.

## Control Testing Verification

Verify access reviews were completed for all critical systems, incident response procedures were tested within the review period, disaster recovery plan was tested at least once during the observation period, vendor risk assessments were completed for in-scope vendors, and employee security training was completed by 100% of staff.

## Stakeholder Preparation

Verify the compliance owner is available during fieldwork, executive sponsor is briefed on audit scope and timeline, technical team leads know which controls they own, customer references are prepared if the auditor requests interviews, and the system description is current and accurate.

## FAQ

### How long does a pre-audit walkthrough take?

A thorough pre-audit walkthrough for a startup with Security-only scope takes 1 to 2 days. For multi-TSC scope, plan 2 to 4 days.

### Who should participate in the pre-audit walkthrough?

The compliance lead, CTO or VP Engineering, and control owners for each control area should participate. The pre-audit walkthrough is both a verification exercise and an auditor preparation session.

### What is the most common item missed in pre-audit checks?

Documentation of emergency changes. Most teams implement change management controls for standard changes but fail to document the emergency change process separately. Emergency changes must be approved post-hoc and documented to satisfy CC8.1.
