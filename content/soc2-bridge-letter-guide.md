A SOC 2 bridge letter — also called a gap letter or bring-down letter — is a written statement from your auditor confirming that no material changes have occurred to your control environment since the SOC 2 report date, and approximately 65% of enterprise procurement teams require one when the SOC 2 report is more than 90 days old. The bridge letter serves as interim assurance between annual certification cycles and prevents deal delays during the audit gap period.

## When Is a SOC 2 Bridge Letter Required?

Enterprise procurement teams typically require a bridge letter when the SOC 2 report is more than 90 to 120 days old. Some buyers with strict requirements demand a current bridge letter at the time of contract execution — defined as fewer than 60 days since issuance. The bridge letter is most commonly requested during Q4 procurement cycles when annual SOC 2 reports may be approaching their issuance anniversary.

## What Does a SOC 2 Bridge Letter Contain?

A SOC 2 bridge letter includes the addressee and date, the SOC 2 report reference (issuing CPA firm, report date, trust service criteria covered), the auditor's statement confirming no material changes to the control environment since the report date, the effective date of the confirmation (typically the letter issuance date), the auditor's signature and firm letterhead, and a scope limitation statement specifying what the letter does not cover.

The letter explicitly does not constitute a new SOC 2 opinion. It covers only the period between the report date and the letter date. It does not replace the annual re-certification audit.

## How Long Is a SOC 2 Bridge Letter Valid?

Most auditors issue bridge letters with a validity period of 90 to 120 days. After the validity period expires, a new bridge letter or the next annual SOC 2 report is required. Some auditors charge a fee of $500 to $2,500 per bridge letter, while others include a limited number in the annual audit engagement fee.

## FAQ

### Can a company issue its own bridge letter?

No. A SOC 2 bridge letter must be issued by the same CPA firm that performed the SOC 2 audit. Self-attestation bridge letters are generally not accepted by enterprise procurement teams.

### How quickly can an auditor issue a bridge letter?

Most auditors issue bridge letters within 3 to 10 business days of request, assuming no significant changes have occurred in the control environment.

### What happens if there have been material changes since the SOC 2 report?

If material changes have occurred, the auditor cannot issue a standard bridge letter. The company may need to discuss the changes with the auditor and potentially provide compensating controls documentation. In cases of significant change, the auditor may recommend an updated SOC 2 report rather than a bridge letter.
