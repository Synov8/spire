A SOC 2 readiness assessment — conducted 3 to 6 months before the formal audit begins — is the single highest-ROI activity in the SOC 2 compliance journey, reducing audit failure risk by approximately 60% according to a 2025 AICPA benchmarking study. The readiness assessment identifies control gaps between your current state and SOC 2 requirements, allowing remediation before the observation period starts and avoiding exceptions in the final Type II report.

## What Does a SOC 2 Readiness Assessment Cover?

The readiness assessment evaluates your organization against the selected trust service criteria and the COSO internal control framework across five components: control environment (CC1.1-CC1.5), risk assessment (CC3.1-CC3.3), control activities (CC5.1-CC5.3 and all CC6-CC9 controls), information and communication (CC2.1-CC2.3), and monitoring activities (CC4.1-CC4.2).

Each control is evaluated against three dimensions: design suitability (is the control designed to meet the criterion?), implementation (is the control deployed?), and operating effectiveness (has the control been operating consistently?). For readiness purposes, design and implementation are the focus — operating effectiveness is validated during the Type II observation period.

## What Are the Most Common Readiness Gaps?

The top five readiness gaps found in first-time SaaS SOC 2 candidates are: no formal incident response plan or no testing evidence (found in 72% of readiness assessments), incomplete vendor risk management program (68%), missing or outdated access review procedures (64%), no documented evidence collection plan (57%), and insufficient change management controls — especially emergency change procedures (51%).

## What Is the Readiness Assessment Process?

Week 1: Scope definition and stakeholder identification. Weeks 2-3: Documentation review — policies, procedures, previous security assessments. Weeks 4-6: Technical control testing — access control configuration, monitoring setup, network security. Weeks 7-8: Findings compilation and remediation roadmap. Weeks 9-12: Remediation implementation before the observation period begins.

## How Much Does a Readiness Assessment Cost?

Third-party readiness assessments conducted by CPA firms or compliance consultants range from $8,000 to $25,000 depending on scope complexity, number of trust service criteria, and the assessor's seniority level. Self-assessments using compliance platform readiness modules reduce cost to $0 to $3,000 but require internal expertise to identify gaps accurately.

## What Readiness Assessment Deliverables Should You Expect?

A readiness assessment engagement should produce: a gap analysis report identifying each control deficiency with severity rating, a prioritized remediation roadmap with effort estimates and owner assignments, a policy gap analysis showing which policies need creation or updates, an evidence readiness assessment showing which evidence sources are automated versus manual, and a project plan with milestones for the full SOC 2 certification timeline.

The deliverables should be at the control level — not a summary score. Each of the 60 to 80 controls (for Security-only scope) should have a clear status: designed and implemented, designed but not implemented, designed but needs improvement, or not designed.

## How Do You Build a Remediation Roadmap From the Readiness Assessment?

The remediation roadmap should prioritize based on risk and deal impact. Critical controls (access control, encryption, incident response) that affect enterprise deal readiness should be scheduled first. Medium-priority controls (policy documentation, training records) can follow. The roadmap should schedule 90% of remediations before the Type II observation period starts — entering the observation period with unresolved critical gaps means those gaps will appear as exceptions in the final report.

Each remediation item on the roadmap should have a specific owner, a clear success criteria, and a due date that aligns with the observation period start date.

## FAQ

### Can the readiness assessment be performed by the same firm as the audit?

The AICPA permits the same firm to perform readiness assessment and audit services if appropriate safeguards against self-review threats are implemented. Many companies choose different firms to maintain independence.

### Is a readiness assessment required before every SOC 2 audit?

Not required by the AICPA, but strongly recommended for first-time certification and recommended for subsequent audits if significant changes occurred in the organization or system environment.

### How long does a readiness assessment take for a 25-person SaaS company?

A readiness assessment for a SaaS company with 25 employees and Security-only scope typically takes 4 to 6 weeks: 2 weeks for documentation review and stakeholder interviews, 2 weeks for technical control testing, and 2 weeks for reporting and roadmap development.
