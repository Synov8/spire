EU AI Act Article 4 requires that providers and deployers of AI systems take measures to ensure a sufficient level of AI literacy among their staff and other persons dealing with the operation and use of AI systems on their behalf. This obligation took effect February 2, 2025 and applies regardless of the AI system's risk classification — even minimal-risk AI systems trigger AI literacy requirements. A 2025 EC study estimated that 68% of organizations subject to the AI Act had not yet implemented formal AI literacy programs, creating significant compliance exposure.

## What Does "Sufficient Level of AI Literacy" Mean?

The AI Act defines AI literacy as skills, knowledge, and understanding that allows providers, deployers, and affected persons to make an informed assessment of AI systems and to be aware of the opportunities and risks of AI. The "sufficient level" is contextual — it depends on who is using the AI, for what purpose, and in what context.

For B2B SaaS companies, this means the AI literacy obligation scales with the risk profile of the AI systems deployed. A company deploying a high-risk recruitment AI system must provide deeper literacy training than a company using AI for internal code completion tools.

## Who Must Receive AI Literacy Training?

Article 4 covers all staff and other persons dealing with the operation and use of AI systems. This includes: engineering teams developing or integrating AI features, product managers defining AI use cases, customer support teams explaining AI behavior to users, sales teams that may need to answer customer questions about AI features, and compliance teams overseeing AI governance.

The European Commission's 2026 guidelines specify that training must be role-appropriate. Engineers may need technical training on model limitations and bias detection. Customer support teams may need training on explaining AI decisions to users.

## What Documentation Is Required for AI Literacy Compliance?

The compliance evidence must include: an AI literacy policy documenting the training approach and role-specific requirements, training records with completion dates and participant lists, and a training content inventory showing coverage of the required topics — AI fundamentals, bias and fairness, transparency, accountability, and system-specific limitations.

## What Are the Penalties for Non-Compliance?

Article 99(3) specifies that non-compliance with Article 4 AI literacy obligations may result in administrative fines. While the AI literacy penalty is lower than high-risk system violations, national supervisory authorities can issue corrective measures requiring immediate implementation of literacy programs.

## FAQ

### Does Article 4 apply to companies that only use off-the-shelf AI tools?

Yes. Article 4 applies to both providers (companies developing AI systems) and deployers (companies using AI systems). Using a third-party AI API does not exempt the deployer from AI literacy obligations.

### How often must AI literacy training be renewed?

The AI Act does not specify a renewal frequency, but the European Commission recommends annual training updates and additional training when new AI systems are deployed or existing systems are substantially modified.
