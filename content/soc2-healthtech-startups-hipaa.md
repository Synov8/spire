Healthtech startups handling protected health information (PHI) must comply with both SOC 2 standards and HIPAA Security, Privacy, and Breach Notification Rules — creating one of the most complex compliance environments in B2B SaaS. A 2025 HHS Office for Civil Rights enforcement report showed that 63% of HIPAA settlements involved business associates (covering most healthtech SaaS companies), with average settlements exceeding $250,000 for violations involving unaddressed security gaps that SOC 2 controls would have covered.

## How Do SOC 2 and HIPAA Security Rule Align?

SOC 2 controls map extensively to the HIPAA Security Rule's administrative, physical, and technical safeguards. HIPAA Security Rule §164.308 (administrative safeguards) maps to SOC 2 CC1.1-CC1.5 (control environment) and CC7.4-CC7.5 (incident response). HIPAA §164.310 (physical safeguards) maps to CC6.4 (network security) and CC6.6 (third-party access).

HIPAA §164.312 (technical safeguards) maps to CC6.1-CC6.5 (access control, authentication, and transmission security). The key difference is that HIPAA requires specific safeguards for ePHI that SOC 2 does not explicitly address — including unique user identification, emergency access procedures, automatic logoff, and integrity controls.

## What Additional Controls Do Healthtech Companies Need?

Healthtech companies must add HIPAA-specific controls to their SOC 2 control set: business associate agreements with all subcontractors handling ePHI, ePHI inventory and data flow mapping, HIPAA Privacy Rule procedures (minimum necessary standard, patient rights), and HIPAA Breach Notification Rule procedures (60-day notification requirement for breaches affecting 500+ individuals).

## Should Healthtech Startups Pursue HITRUST Certification?

HITRUST CSF certification provides a certifiable framework that combines SOC 2, HIPAA, ISO 27001, and other standards into a single assessment. For healthtech companies selling to hospitals and large healthcare systems, HITRUST certification is increasingly required — 71% of hospital procurement teams prefer HITRUST-certified vendors according to a 2025 HITRUST Alliance survey.

The HITRUST assessment process takes 6 to 12 months and costs $50,000 to $150,000 for the first certification cycle. For earlier-stage healthtech startups, SOC 2 Type II with HIPAA alignment achieves the same control outcomes at 30% to 50% lower cost.

## What Evidence Must a Healthtech Auditor See?

A healthtech SOC 2 auditor requires the SOC 2 Type II report plus Business Associate Agreements with all subcontractors, ePHI data flow diagrams, HIPAA Security Rule risk assessment, breach notification procedures, and evidence of annual HIPAA training.

## FAQ

### Does SOC 2 Type II satisfy HIPAA audit requirements?

SOC 2 Type II provides evidence of many HIPAA Security Rule requirements but does not constitute a HIPAA compliance audit. OCR HIPAA audits are separate and evaluate compliance with the full HIPAA regulatory framework.

### What happens if we experience an ePHI breach?

The HIPAA Breach Notification Rule requires notification to affected individuals within 60 days for breaches affecting 500+ individuals, with simultaneous notification to HHS and the media. Breaches affecting fewer than 500 individuals must be documented and reported annually.
