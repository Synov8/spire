Enterprise security questionnaire response time directly impacts deal velocity — companies that respond within 5 business days close deals at 2x the rate of those taking 2+ weeks, and 42% of enterprise buyers impose automatic disqualification for responses exceeding 15 business days, according to a 2025 Gartner security procurement analysis. Setting and meeting response SLAs requires a structured process, not just good intentions.

## What Response SLAs Should You Set?

Initial acknowledgment: within 24 hours of receiving the questionnaire. This confirms receipt and sets expectations. Standard questionnaire completion: 5 to 10 business days. Custom questionnaire completion: 10 to 15 business days. Question follow-up response: 48 hours. Define these SLAs in your trust center or security documentation so prospects know what to expect.

## How Do You Prioritize Questionnaires?

Prioritize by deal stage (close within 30 days = priority 1, close within 90 days = priority 2, prospecting = priority 3), deal size ($100K+ ACV = priority 1, $25K to $100K = priority 2, under $25K = priority 3), and customer segment (strategic accounts first).

## What Automation Reduces Response Time?

AI-powered questionnaire response tools that auto-populate answers from your response library, integrate with your compliance platform for evidence references, track response time against SLAs, and provide collaborative review workflows reduce response time by 60% to 80%. ## FAQ

### What if I cannot meet the response SLA?

Communicate proactively with the prospect — explain the expected timeline and offer a preliminary response for critical questions first. Most buyers appreciate transparency over silence.

### How do you handle questionnaire follow-up questions?

Standard follow-up SLA: 48 hours. Escalate follow-ups that require engineering or legal review to the appropriate team member with a clear deadline.

### Should I track questionnaire response SLAs as a metric?

Yes. Track average response time, SLA compliance rate (target 90%+), and deal impact of response delays. Report these metrics to the sales and compliance teams monthly.
