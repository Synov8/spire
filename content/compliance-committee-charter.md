A formal compliance committee charter defines the governance structure for SOC 2 oversight and is viewed favorably by auditors as evidence of CC1.1 and CC1.4 control environment maturity, according to a 2025 AICPA governance best practices guide. Companies with a documented compliance committee charter and quarterly meetings report 40% fewer control exceptions than those without formal governance.

## What Should a Compliance Committee Charter Include?

The charter should define: committee purpose and authority (oversight of the compliance program, approval of policies, review of audit findings), membership (required roles and qualifications), meeting cadence (quarterly minimum, with special meetings as needed), quorum requirements (majority of members present), decision-making process (majority vote, with escalation to board for material findings), and reporting requirements (minutes of each meeting, annual report to board).

## Who Should Serve on the Compliance Committee?

Core members: compliance program lead or CCO, CTO or VP Engineering (technical control oversight), CFO or COO (budget and resource allocation), legal counsel or privacy officer (regulatory and contractual compliance). Board representation (through a board member or designated board reporting channel) for organizations with formal boards.

## How Should the Compliance Committee Operate?

The committee should meet quarterly with a standard agenda: review of compliance metrics and KPIs, status of audit readiness and findings remediation, risk assessment review and updates, policy and control change approvals, budget and resource review, and upcoming audit preparation status.

## FAQ

### Does SOC 2 require a compliance committee?

SOC 2 does not explicitly require a compliance committee, but the COSO control environment principles (CC1.1 to CC1.5) establish governance expectations that are best met through a formal committee structure.

### How often should the compliance committee meet?

Quarterly is the minimum recommended cadence. Monthly meetings are appropriate during active audit preparation periods.

### What is the difference between a compliance committee and a security committee?

A security committee focuses on security operations, incident response, and threat management. A compliance committee focuses on control effectiveness, audit readiness, and regulatory compliance. Mid-size companies often combine both into a risk and compliance committee.
