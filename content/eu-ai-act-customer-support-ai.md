Customer support AI systems — including chatbots, AI agent assistants, and sentiment analysis tools — fall into the limited-risk or high-risk category depending on their deployment context, and approximately 70% of customer support AI deployments qualify as limited-risk under the EU AI Act requiring only transparency obligations under Article 50, according to a 2026 EU Commission AI classification guidance. However, customer support AI used in regulated contexts (healthcare, financial services) may be high-risk.

## When Is Customer Support AI Limited-Risk vs High-Risk?

Limited-risk customer support AI includes general-purpose chatbots providing product information, FAQ answering, basic troubleshooting, and ticket routing — none of these involve decisions that significantly impact individuals' rights or opportunities. High-risk customer support AI includes support AI in healthcare that provides medical advice or triage, support AI in financial services that makes credit-related decisions, support AI that profiles customers for differential treatment, and support AI that makes eligibility or access decisions for essential services.

## What Transparency Obligations Apply to All Customer Support AI?

Article 50 requires transparency for all customer-facing AI: users must be informed they are interacting with an AI system (not a human), AI-generated content must be labeled as such, and emotional inference or manipulation intent must be disclosed. Chatbot transparency is the easiest AI Act requirement to implement but also the most commonly cited in early enforcement actions.

## What Documentation Does Customer Support AI Require?

Limited-risk customer support AI requires technical documentation of system purpose and capabilities, transparency notice design and deployment, and user feedback mechanism for reporting AI errors. High-risk customer support AI requires full high-risk compliance documentation including risk management, data governance, human oversight, accuracy, and conformity assessment.

## FAQ

### Do AI agent assistants need transparency labeling?

Yes. AI agent assistants that interact with customers must be identified as AI. The transparency requirement applies regardless of risk classification. A customer interacting with an "AI agent" must know they are not speaking to a human.

### Can customer support AI use emotional detection?

Emotional detection in customer support AI — analyzing sentiment, tone, or frustration — is limited-risk if used for quality improvement but may be high-risk if used for differential treatment (e.g., redirecting frustrated customers to lower-tier support). Disclose emotional detection use to customers.

### What is the compliance timeline for customer support AI?

Limited-risk customer support AI must comply with transparency obligations by February 2027 (12 months after the Act's entry into force for limited-risk systems). High-risk customer support AI must comply by August 2026.
