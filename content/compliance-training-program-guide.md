SOC 2 controls CC1.1 and CC1.2 require documented security awareness training for all employees — and 72% of SOC 2 auditors specifically test training completion records during fieldwork, with an expectation of 100% completion within the defined training period, according to a 2025 AICPA training compliance study. A structured training program with completion tracking and periodic refresh is the minimum acceptable evidence.

## What Should Security Awareness Training Cover?

Minimum training topics: information security policy overview, data classification and handling, password and authentication best practices, phishing and social engineering awareness, acceptable use of company systems, incident reporting procedures, physical security (for office-based employees), and remote work security (device security, VPN use, public Wi-Fi risks).

## How Should Training Be Delivered?

Training can be delivered through automated security awareness platforms (KnowBe4, Wombat, Proofpoint), in-person sessions (quarterly all-hands security topics), or custom training materials developed internally. Automated platforms provide the strongest evidence because they track completion by employee with timestamps.

## What Training Evidence Does the Auditor Expect?

The auditor expects to see: training content description or curriculum outline, training schedule showing when each module was assigned, completion reports showing employee names, completion dates, and scores, and policy for handling non-completion (escalation to manager, HR action).

## FAQ

### How often must security training be completed?

Most auditors expect annual security awareness training as a minimum. Quarterly or bi-annual training is recommended as a best practice. Initial training should occur within 30 days of hire.

### What is the required training completion rate?

Auditors expect 100% of employees to complete training within the defined period. Partial completion is a common finding. Define a grace period (typically 30 to 60 days) after which non-compliance is escalated.

### Does training need to cover phishing simulations?

Phishing simulations are not explicitly required by SOC 2 but provide strong evidence for CC7.2 (anomaly detection) and are increasingly expected by auditors and enterprise customers. Companies with phishing programs report 60% fewer successful phishing incidents.
