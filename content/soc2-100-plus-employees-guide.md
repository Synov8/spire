Companies with over 100 employees managing SOC 2 across multiple trust service criteria should maintain a dedicated compliance department of 3 to 7 people with annual compliance costs ranging from $300,000 to $800,000, according to a 2025 Gartner compliance function benchmarking survey. At this scale, SOC 2 is one component of a broader governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) program that spans multiple regulatory frameworks and jurisdictions.

## What Compliance Department Structure Works at 100+ Employees?

A compliance team at enterprise scale typically includes: a Director of Compliance or CCO leading the function, SOC 2 program managers (1 to 2 people) managing the audit lifecycle and evidence collection, a security engineer focused on compliance automation and technical control evidence, a privacy officer managing Privacy TSC and data protection regulations, and a vendor risk manager handling third-party assessments.

## How Does Multi-Framework Integration Work at Scale?

Enterprise companies with 100+ employees typically maintain SOC 2 plus ISO 27001, HIPAA, PCI DSS (if handling payment data), GDPR compliance program, and increasingly the EU AI Act. Unified control framework (UCF) mapping — where a single control satisfies requirements across multiple frameworks — reduces duplication by 40% to 60%.

## What Board-Level Compliance Reporting Is Expected?

Board-level reporting at enterprise scale includes quarterly compliance dashboard showing control effectiveness rates, audit readiness status by framework, findings and remediation tracking, compliance risk metrics, and budget vs actual tracking.

## FAQ

### How often should enterprise companies change auditors?

Enterprise companies should conduct an auditor RFP every 3 to 5 years to ensure competitive pricing and service quality. Annual audit fees at this scale: $60,000 to $150,000.

### What is the SOC 2 observation period at enterprise scale?

Enterprise companies typically maintain a continuous SOC 2 observation period — one Type II report period ends and the next begins with no gap. This is the most audit-efficient approach for large organizations.

### Do 100+ employee companies need SOC for cybersecurity?

Many enterprise companies with 100+ employees add SOC for Cybersecurity (AICPA Cybersecurity Risk Management Report) as a complementary attestation. While not a SOC 2 requirement, the SOC for Cybersecurity provides additional assurance during enterprise sales.
