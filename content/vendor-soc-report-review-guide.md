Reviewing a vendor's SOC 2 report is a core CC9.2 control activity, and companies that follow a structured SOC report review process identify material findings 3x more frequently than those that rely on summary assessments, according to a 2025 Shared Assessments Program study. The SOC report review should take 30 to 60 minutes per vendor using the structured approach below.

## What Are the Five Steps of a SOC Report Review?

Step 1 — Verify report type and period: confirm the report is a Type II (not Type I), verify the report period covers the timeframes you need, check the report date is within the last 12 to 15 months. Step 2 — Read the independent auditor's opinion: check for qualified opinions, adverse opinions, or disclaimers. An unqualified opinion is standard. Any modification requires investigation.

Step 3 — Review the system description: verify the vendor's system description matches the services you use from them. Step 4 — Check the trust service criteria: confirm the report covers the TSC you require (Security is minimum). Step 5 — Review the test of controls section: look for exceptions, especially exceptions that relate to services you use.

## What Are Red Flags in Vendor SOC Reports?

Red flags include: qualified opinion or opinion modification, exceptions in controls that directly affect your services, report older than 15 months, scope excludes the specific service you use, significant control exceptions with incomplete remediation, and Type I report instead of Type II.

## How Should You Document Vendor SOC Report Reviews?

Document each review with: vendor name and report date, reviewer name and review date, TSC coverage confirmed, material exceptions identified (or none), remediation status of previous exceptions, and recommendation (accept, request additional evidence, or escalate).

## FAQ

### What if the vendor's SOC report has exceptions?

Evaluate whether the exception affects your specific use of the vendor. If the exception is in an unrelated service, it may be low risk. If it affects your data or service, request compensating evidence or a remediation timeline.

### Can I accept a SOC 3 report instead of SOC 2?

SOC 3 reports provide only the auditor's opinion — they omit the detailed control descriptions and test results needed for due diligence. Accept SOC 3 only for low-risk vendors.

### How often should I review a vendor's SOC report?

At minimum annually, aligned with your SOC 2 audit cycle. Quarterly reviews are recommended for critical-tier vendors to catch emerging issues earlier.
