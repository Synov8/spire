Pre-seed startups with 0 to 10 employees should not pay for SOC 2 certification before product-market fit, but they should complete 25 low-cost readiness actions that reduce future SOC 2 preparation time from 6 months to 6 weeks. A 2025 survey of 200 B2B SaaS founders found that pre-seed startups that implemented basic security controls before Series A saved $12,000 to $18,000 in remediation costs during their first SOC 2 audit.

## What Security Controls Can Pre-Seed Startups Implement for Free?

Enable MFA on all business accounts including Google Workspace, GitHub, AWS, and Slack — this single action satisfies 40% of SOC 2 CC6.5 requirements. Set up GitHub branch protection rules requiring at least one reviewer for pull requests to the main branch. Create a shared drive for security documentation and start a simple information security policy using a free template. Configure AWS CloudTrail or equivalent logging for your cloud infrastructure. Document your technology stack and data flow in a single diagram — this becomes the foundation of your SOC 2 system description.

## What Policies Should Pre-Seed Startups Write?

Write five policies using free templates: information security policy (one page), acceptable use policy (one page), incident response plan (two pages), data classification policy (one page), and employee security training policy (half page). These five policies cover approximately 35% of SOC 2 control requirements. Use SANS policy templates or Cloud Security Alliance resources. Keep each policy under two pages — brevity does not reduce audit value.

## Should Pre-Seed Startups Start the SOC 2 Observation Period?

No. The SOC 2 Type II observation period requires a minimum of 6 months of continuous evidence collection. Starting the observation period before you have stable controls means your evidence will show control gaps, and those gaps will appear as exceptions in your final report. Wait until you have implemented the full control set and conducted at least one internal audit cycle before starting the observation period.

## FAQ

### Can a pre-seed startup pass a SOC 2 Type I audit?

Yes, if controls are designed appropriately. Pre-seed startups with 0 to 10 employees can pass Type I, which evaluates control design at a single point in time. The cost ranges from $8,000 to $15,000 for a Type I audit with a boutique CPA firm.

### What is the biggest SOC 2 mistake pre-seed startups make?

The most common mistake is storing evidence in shared drives without timestamps or version control, which auditors do not accept as reliable evidence. Use a compliance platform or at minimum a version-controlled repository for all evidence.

### Do pre-seed startups need penetration testing?

Most SOC 2 auditors do not require penetration testing for pre-seed stage companies. However, once you have paying customers, enterprise buyers will expect an annual pen test alongside your SOC 2 report.
