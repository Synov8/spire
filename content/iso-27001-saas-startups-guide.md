ISO 27001 certification for SaaS startups follows a similar timeline to SOC 2 Type II — 6 to 12 months from readiness to certification — but costs approximately 30% to 50% less because ISO 27001 does not require a 6-month observation period, according to a 2025 ISO compliance cost analysis. The key advantage of ISO 27001 for SaaS startups is that it provides an auditable information security management system (ISMS) without the fixed observation period that delays SOC 2 Type II.

## What Is the ISO 27001 Timeline for SaaS Startups?

Phase 1 — ISMS Establishment (Weeks 1 to 8): define ISMS scope, develop information security policy, conduct risk assessment and treatment plan, select controls from Annex A. Phase 2 — Implementation (Weeks 9 to 16): implement selected controls, create mandatory documents (scope, policy, risk assessment, SoA, internal audit, management review), conduct internal audit. Phase 3 — Certification Audit (Weeks 17 to 24): Stage 1 audit (documentation review) typically 1 day, Stage 2 audit (implementation verification) typically 2 to 3 days. Certification decision within 4 to 6 weeks after Stage 2.

## How Much Does ISO 27001 Cost for a SaaS Startup?

Audit fees: $5,000 to $15,000 for certification audit (Stage 1 + Stage 2) with a startup-specialist certification body. Internal implementation effort: 100 to 200 hours of engineering and management time. Documentation: $3,000 to $8,000 if using templates. Total first-year ISO 27001 cost: $15,000 to $45,000. Combined SOC 2 + ISO 27001 first-year cost: $45,000 to $120,000 if controls are shared.

## What Are the Mandatory ISO 27001 Documents?

Clause 4.3 — ISMS scope document. Clause 5.2 — Information security policy. Clause 6.1 — Risk assessment and risk treatment methodology. Clause 6.1.3 — Statement of Applicability. Clause 9.2 — Internal audit program and reports. Clause 9.3 — Management review records. Additionally, most Annex A controls require supporting documentation.

## FAQ

### Should a SaaS startup pursue ISO 27001 before SOC 2?

For US-focused SaaS companies, SOC 2 is more relevant for enterprise sales. For EU-focused companies, ISO 27001 is often preferred. Many startups pursue SOC 2 first and add ISO 27001 within 12 to 18 months.

### Can the same evidence support both SOC 2 and ISO 27001?

Yes. Approximately 60% to 70% of SOC 2 Security TSC controls map directly to ISO 27001 Annex A controls. Maintaining a unified control framework with a compliance platform that supports both standards generates dual-certification efficiency.

### What is the ongoing ISO 27001 cost after certification?

Annual surveillance audits: $3,000 to $8,000 per year. Recertification every 3 years: $8,000 to $15,000. Internal maintenance: 50 to 100 hours per year of staff time.
