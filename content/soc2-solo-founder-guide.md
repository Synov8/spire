SOC 2 requires segregation of duties — a principle that conflicts directly with solo founder operations where one person performs all roles — but solo founders can achieve SOC 2 certification by using automation and strategic contractor relationships to create functional separation, according to a 2025 AICPA guidance supplement for micro-entities. Approximately 6% of SOC 2 reports are issued to companies with fewer than 5 employees.

## How Does a Solo Founder Handle Segregation of Duties?

The core segregation of duties conflict in a solo founder operation is that the same person performs, approves, and reviews actions — violating the fundamental SOC 2 control principle that no single person should control all phases of a process. The solution is: use a compliance platform that prevents the control owner from marking their own evidence as reviewed, engage a part-time virtual CISO or fractional compliance officer to perform independent access reviews, use automated approval workflows that route change approvals to a secondary reviewer, and document that independent review is performed by external contractors.

## What SOC 2 Controls Are Hardest for Solo Founders?

Access reviews (CC6.3) require an independent reviewer — the solo founder cannot approve their own access. Change management (CC8.1) requires that production changes be approved by someone other than the implementer. Incident response (CC7.4) requires that incidents are evaluated and escalated by someone with appropriate authority — which is always the founder.

## FAQ

### Can a solo founder pass SOC 2 Type II?

Yes, but it requires documented compensating controls for segregation of duties. The auditor must understand and accept the solo founder operating model with specific controls addressing the separation gaps.

### What is the minimum cost for a solo founder SOC 2?

Solo founder SOC 2 Type II costs: $15,000 to $35,000 including a boutique auditor ($8,000 to $15,000), compliance platform ($5,000 to $10,000), and fractional compliance officer ($3,000 to $10,000). Penetration testing adds $3,000 to $7,000.

### Do solo founders need a separate business entity for SOC 2?

No. The SOC 2 report covers the legal entity that operates the service. Most solo founders operate as single-member LLCs or sole proprietorships and can achieve SOC 2 under that legal structure.
