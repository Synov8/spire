The EU AI Act's phased enforcement timeline means that different AI systems face compliance deadlines at different dates in 2026 and 2027, and companies that map their AI systems to the correct compliance deadline and begin preparation 12 to 18 months in advance achieve on-time compliance at 50% lower cost than those that start 6 months before deadlines, according to a 2026 EU Commission enforcement readiness survey. Missing the August 2026 deadline for high-risk obligations carries penalty exposure up to 35 million EUR.

## 2024 to 2025: Pre-Enforcement Phase

February 2024: EU AI Act approved by European Parliament. August 2024: Act entered into force. November 2024: Prohibited AI practices effective (social scoring, real-time biometric surveillance, manipulative AI). February 2025: GPAI transparency obligations effective (training data documentation, copyright policy). 2025 to 2026: Member states designate national competent authorities and notify bodies.

## 2026: High-Risk Implementation Phase

February 2026: GPAI code of practice finalized. August 2, 2026: High-risk AI system obligations effective (risk management, data governance, transparency, human oversight, accuracy, record keeping, post-market monitoring). This is the most impactful deadline for most AI companies.

## 2027: Full Certification Phase

February 2, 2027: Limited-risk AI transparency obligations effective (chatbot labeling, AI-generated content labeling, emotional AI disclosure). August 2, 2027: Full conformity assessment and CE marking required for high-risk AI systems. This is the final deadline for complete AI Act compliance.

## What Should Companies Do Now?

Immediate 2026 actions: identify all AI systems and classify by risk level, begin high-risk system compliance preparation (risk management, technical documentation, data governance), register high-risk AI systems in the EU database, and implement transparency for customer-facing AI.

## FAQ

### What happens if I miss the August 2026 deadline?

Non-compliant high-risk AI systems operating after August 2, 2026 face penalties of up to 35 million EUR or 7% of worldwide annual turnover. National competent authorities can issue corrective actions, suspend system deployment, or withdraw the system from the market.

### Does the deadline apply to AI systems already in use?

Yes. High-risk AI systems already deployed before August 2, 2026 must comply with the obligations deadline. Existing systems do not receive grandfathering. The conformity assessment deadline (August 2027) provides additional time for full certification of existing systems.

### Are there transitional periods for existing systems?

Yes, for conformity assessment. High-risk AI systems already placed on the market before August 2, 2026 must comply with obligations by August 2026 but have until August 2027 to complete full conformity assessment (for systems that require it).
