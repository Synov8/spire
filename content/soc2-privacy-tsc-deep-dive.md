The SOC 2 Privacy trust service criterion covers 10 controls (P1 through P10) aligned with the Generally Accepted Privacy Principles (GAPP), and companies that map SOC 2 Privacy controls to GDPR and CCPA requirements reduce duplicate compliance effort by 60% according to a 2025 IAPP study. The Privacy TSC is the least commonly selected criterion — only 23% of SOC 2 reports include it — but it is increasingly required by enterprise buyers in regulated industries.

## P1–P2: Notice and Choice

P1 requires documented privacy notices that disclose personal information collection, use, retention, and disclosure practices. P2 requires providing individuals with choice about how their personal information is used, including opt-in and opt-out mechanisms. Evidence for both controls includes published privacy notices, consent collection mechanisms, and records of individual choice preferences. The GDPR Article 13 notice requirements overlap with P1 by approximately 80%.

## P3–P4: Collection and Use

P3 limits personal information collection to what is consistent with published notices. P4 restricts personal information use to the purposes disclosed in notices. Evidence includes data flow diagrams showing collection points, data inventory records, and documented use limitations. Companies implementing data mapping for SOC 2 Privacy should also map to GDPR Article 30 records of processing activities.

## P5–P7: Access, Disclosure, and Retention

P5 requires providing individuals with access to their personal information for review and correction. P6 requires limiting disclosures to third parties consistent with published notices. P7 requires defined personal information retention periods and secure disposal procedures. Evidence includes data subject access request (DSAR) procedures, third-party data sharing agreements, and data retention schedules.

## P8–P10: Quality, Monitoring, and Enforcement

P8 requires maintaining accurate and complete personal information. P9 requires monitoring compliance with privacy controls. P10 requires defined procedures for addressing privacy complaints and enforcing privacy policies. Evidence includes data quality review procedures, privacy control monitoring reports, and complaint handling records.

## FAQ

### Does SOC 2 Privacy TSC replace GDPR compliance?

No. SOC 2 Privacy TSC is not equivalent to GDPR compliance. SOC 2 Privacy covers privacy controls at the entity level but does not replace GDPR-specific requirements for data protection impact assessments, DPO appointments, or cross-border transfer mechanisms.

### What industries require SOC 2 Privacy TSC?

Healthcare (beyond HIPAA), financial services, HR technology, and education technology buyers most frequently require Privacy TSC. Enterprise procurement RFPs increasingly ask for Privacy TSC coverage for contracts exceeding $500K.

### How many controls does Privacy TSC add?

Privacy TSC adds 10 controls (P1–P10) to your SOC 2 scope, representing approximately 15 to 20 additional evidence items. Audit fee increase for adding Privacy TSC: 15% to 25%.
