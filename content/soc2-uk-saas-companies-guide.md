UK SaaS companies pursuing SOC 2 certification must navigate the post-Brexit regulatory landscape where both UK GDPR and US-centric SOC 2 standards apply. A 2025 Tech Nation survey found that 71% of UK SaaS companies reporting revenue over £2M identified US market access as their primary growth driver — and 83% of those companies said SOC 2 Type II was required or strongly preferred by US enterprise prospects.

## How Does UK GDPR Affect SOC 2 for UK Companies?

UK GDPR retains the same structure and principles as EU GDPR post-Brexit, meaning UK SOC 2 candidates must address the same data protection integration requirements as EU companies. The UK ICO (Information Commissioner's Office) has indicated that SOC 2 controls aligned with UK GDPR Article 32 security requirements will be considered favorably during ICO investigations — though SOC 2 certification does not confer UK GDPR compliance.

The key UK GDPR-specific considerations are: International data transfer requirements under UK GDPR Articles 44-49 (including the UK International Data Transfer Agreement), ICO registration and fee requirements that apply separately from SOC 2 compliance, and the UK ICO's enhanced enforcement posture — the ICO issued £24 million in fines in 2025, a 40% increase from 2023.

## Should UK Companies Choose Cyber Essentials Plus or SOC 2?

Cyber Essentials Plus is a UK government-backed certification covering basic cyber hygiene (£300 to £1,500 assessment cost). SOC 2 is a comprehensive control framework covering security, availability, and confidentiality ($15,000 to $30,000 audit cost). The two certifications serve different purposes — Cyber Essentials Plus satisfies basic UK government and enterprise supplier requirements, while SOC 2 satisfies US enterprise procurement requirements.

Companies should pursue Cyber Essentials Plus for domestic UK government contracts (£18,000 average contract premium for certified suppliers) and SOC 2 for US market expansion.

## What Auditor Options Exist for UK SaaS Companies?

UK SaaS companies can engage US-based CPA firms with UK operations (Big Four firms with London offices), UK-based firms that are AICPA-licensed for SOC 2 (about a dozen UK firms currently hold SOC 2 licenses), or US-based firms conducting remote audits (accepted by most US enterprise buyers). Remote audits for UK companies cost 20% to 30% less than on-site US auditor engagement.

## What Evidence Must UK Companies Prepare for SOC 2?

In addition to standard SOC 2 evidence, UK companies should prepare UK GDPR Article 30 processing records, UK Adequacy Decision mapping for international data transfers, ICO registration certificate, and Cyber Essentials or Cyber Essentials Plus certification if applicable.

## FAQ

### Does SOC 2 satisfy UK government supplier requirements?

No. UK government suppliers may require Cyber Essentials Plus or equivalent. SOC 2 Type II may be accepted as supplementary evidence for data protection requirements in government procurement.

### Can a UK company serve as a SOC 2 sub-service organization?

Yes. UK companies processing data for US-based SOC 2-reporting entities must be evaluated under CC9.2 as sub-service organizations. Their SOC 2 report or equivalent security documentation must be available for review.
