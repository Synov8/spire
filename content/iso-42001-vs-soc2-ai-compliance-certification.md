ISO 42001 is the first certifiable AI management system standard, published in late 2024, designed to cover AI governance, risk management, transparency, and accountability across the AI system lifecycle. SOC 2 covers information security controls for service organizations — access control, monitoring, encryption, and incident response. For B2B SaaS companies selling to enterprise customers, SOC 2 is the baseline requirement and ISO 42001 is emerging as the complementary AI governance certification that forward-looking procurement teams are beginning to request alongside SOC 2 reports.

## What Is ISO 42001 Certification?

ISO 42001 is an AI management system standard developed by the International Organization for Standardization and published in December 2024. It specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an AI management system within the context of an organization. The standard covers AI governance, risk management, AI system lifecycle controls, transparency and explainability, accountability, and continual improvement.

Unlike SOC 2, which evaluates controls against trust service criteria, ISO 42001 evaluates an organization's AI management system against a structured framework of policies, processes, and governance mechanisms. Certification requires an external audit by an accredited certification body.

The standard is framework-agnostic — it does not prescribe specific AI technologies or approaches but requires organizations to define their AI governance policies and demonstrate that they follow them. This flexibility allows ISO 42001 to apply across different AI use cases, from chatbots to predictive analytics to autonomous decision systems.

A 2025 survey by the International Association of Privacy Professionals found that 37% of US technology companies were considering ISO 42001 certification in 2026, driven by EU AI Act procurement requirements. Some industry observers expect ISO 42001 to become as commonly requested as SOC 2 within 12 to 18 months.

## How Does ISO 42001 Relate to the EU AI Act?

ISO 42001 certification provides a structured framework for demonstrating AI governance practices that align with EU AI Act requirements. The standard covers several areas directly relevant to AI Act compliance: AI risk assessment and management (mapping to high-risk system requirements under Chapter III), transparency and documentation (mapping to technical documentation requirements in Article 11), human oversight integration (mapping to Article 14), accuracy and robustness (mapping to Article 15), and data governance (mapping to Article 10).

ISO 42001 does not guarantee EU AI Act compliance — the Act contains specific legal requirements that a management system standard cannot fully replace — but it provides a foundation that reduces the effort required to demonstrate compliance. Companies with ISO 42001 certification can leverage their AI management system documentation as evidence when AI Act enforcement begins.

The European Commission has not designated ISO 42001 as a formal presumption of conformity under the AI Act. However, the standard is referenced in the AI Act's recitals as an example of a governance framework that supports compliance. In practice, procurement teams evaluating AI vendors view ISO 42001 as a credible signal of AI governance maturity.

## How Does ISO 42001 Compare to SOC 2's Trust Service Criteria?

SOC 2 evaluates controls against five trust service criteria: security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. These criteria focus on information security and data protection — not specifically on AI governance. A SOC 2 report tells an enterprise buyer that your organization has adequate access controls, monitoring, encryption, and incident response. It does not tell them how you govern AI systems.

ISO 42001 evaluates AI-specific governance areas: AI system risk classification methodology, training data governance and quality management, AI system transparency and explainability documentation, human oversight and accountability structures, AI system monitoring and performance management, and AI system retirement and disposal processes.

The two certifications are complementary. SOC 2 covers the information security foundation — the controls that protect customer data regardless of whether AI is involved. ISO 42001 covers the AI-specific governance layer — the policies and processes that ensure AI systems are developed and deployed responsibly.

Companies that hold both certifications can produce a combined compliance package that satisfies both the security baseline (SOC 2) and the AI governance expectations (ISO 42001) that European enterprise procurement teams are increasingly requesting.

## What Does ISO 42001 Certification Cost?

ISO 42001 certification costs for a B2B SaaS company with fewer than 100 employees range from $15,000 to $40,000 for the initial certification audit, plus annual surveillance audits of $5,000 to $15,000. The certification process includes a Stage 1 audit (documentation review) and a Stage 2 audit (implementation verification).

The total cost including preparation, internal time, and certification body fees ranges from $25,000 to $75,000 for the first year. Organizations that already have ISO 27001 certification find ISO 42001 integration easier because the management system structure (Annex SL framework) is shared between the two standards.

ISO 42001 certification requires approximately 100 to 200 hours of internal effort for documentation development, risk assessment, and internal audit preparation. Companies with existing AI governance documentation and policies can reduce this significantly.

## Should You Pursue ISO 42001 Alongside SOC 2?

The decision depends on your customer base and sales pipeline. If your enterprise customers include European companies, financial services firms, or healthcare organizations that are asking about AI governance in security questionnaires, ISO 42001 provides a certifiable answer. If your customers are primarily US-based startups and mid-market companies that have not yet requested AI governance documentation, SOC 2 alone is sufficient for now.

A practical strategy is to implement ISO 42001-aligned AI governance practices — documented AI use policies, risk assessments, transparency documentation, and human oversight procedures — without pursuing formal certification. This satisfies procurement questionnaire requirements at lower cost, with certification pursued when a specific customer deal requires it.

For companies selling AI products to European enterprises, the combined SOC 2 plus ISO 42001 package is becoming a competitive advantage. Workstreet reported in 2026 that companies with both certifications saw 40% faster European enterprise deal closure compared to those with only SOC 2.

## FAQ

### Can ISO 42001 replace SOC 2?

No. ISO 42001 and SOC 2 cover different domains. SOC 2 covers information security controls for service organizations. ISO 42001 covers AI governance and management systems. Most enterprise procurement teams require SOC 2 as a baseline and may request ISO 42001 as an additional AI governance credential. The two are complementary, not interchangeable.

### How long does ISO 42001 certification take?

ISO 42001 certification typically takes 3 to 6 months from the start of preparation to the certification audit. Organizations with existing ISO 27001 or SOC 2 programs can complete certification faster because the management system infrastructure is already in place.

### Is ISO 42001 recognized outside the EU?

Yes. ISO 42001 is an international standard published by ISO, not an EU-specific regulation. It is recognized globally and can be used by organizations in any jurisdiction. However, its practical value is currently highest for companies selling to European and Asia-Pacific markets where AI governance requirements are most developed.

### Does ISO 42001 require third-party certification?

Yes. ISO 42001 requires certification by an accredited certification body through a formal audit process. Unlike SOC 2, which is an attestation report issued by a licensed CPA firm, ISO 42001 is a management system certification. The certification body conducts Stage 1 and Stage 2 audits and issues the certificate for a defined validity period.
