The Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA) is a mandatory pre-deployment obligation for deployers of high-risk AI systems under Article 27 of the EU AI Act — and companies that conduct FRIAs during system design, not as a compliance checkbox, identify an average of 8 to 12 risk mitigation measures per assessment, according to a 2026 EU Commission FRIA best practices guide. The FRIA evaluates risks to rights protected by the EU Charter of Fundamental Rights.

## When Is a FRIA Required?

A FRIA is required before deploying a high-risk AI system, and the deployer — the organization using the AI system — is responsible for conducting it, not the provider. The FRIA must be completed before first deployment and updated when the deployment context changes significantly.

## What Must a FRIA Cover?

A FRIA must include: description of the deployment process and system purpose, identification of affected individuals or groups, assessment of risks to fundamental rights (non-discrimination, privacy, data protection, freedom of expression, fair trial, labor rights), specification of mitigation measures for each identified risk, description of human oversight procedures, and residual risk assessment after mitigation.

## How Does FRIA Relate to the Provider's Risk Assessment?

The provider performs a risk management assessment (Articles 8 and 9) focused on system design and functionality. The deployer performs a FRIA focused on deployment context — how the system interacts with specific populations and environments. Both are required. One does not substitute for the other.

## FAQ

### Can the FRIA be combined with a DPIA?

Yes. The EU Commission recommends conducting a combined FRIA and Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) where the AI system processes personal data. A combined assessment reduces duplication and provides a unified view of risks to rights.

### Who should conduct the FRIA?

The FRIA should be conducted by the deployer's compliance, legal, or risk management team with input from stakeholders who understand the deployment context. External consultants can support FRIA preparation, but the deployer retains responsibility.

### How long does a FRIA take?

A thorough FRIA for a single high-risk AI system takes 4 to 8 weeks: 1 to 2 weeks for information gathering, 2 to 3 weeks for risk analysis, 1 to 2 weeks for mitigation design, and 1 week for documentation and review.
