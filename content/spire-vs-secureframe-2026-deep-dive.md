Spire and Secureframe represent two approaches to SOC 2 automation in 2026 — Spire as an AI-native platform with automated evidence evaluation and Secureframe as a well-established platform with 300+ integrations and strong evidence collection capabilities. Secureframe was acquired by OneTrust in 2024 and operates as part of the OneTrust GRC suite. Spire competes by offering more advanced AI-powered features at a lower total cost for most B2B SaaS companies.

## AI and Automation Approach

Spire's AI audit agent evaluates collected evidence against SOC 2 controls and produces structured readiness reports. Secureframe provides rule-based evidence collection with integrations to 300+ tools but lacks Spire's AI-powered evidence evaluation. Spire also provides AI questionnaire response automation that generates answers from your compliance evidence base.

## Integration Coverage

Secureframe offers 300+ integrations — the broadest coverage after Vanta. Spire covers the essential integration set for B2B SaaS (40+ integrations). For companies with standard cloud-native infrastructure (AWS, GCP, GitHub, Okta), both platforms adequately cover SOC 2 evidence collection.

## Pricing and Value

Secureframe pricing starts at approximately $15,000 to $20,000 per year for SOC 2 coverage, with higher tiers available through OneTrust for enterprise GRC. Spire offers competitive pricing with AI-powered features included at standard tiers.

## Best-Fit Customer Profile

Spire is best for: companies wanting AI-powered compliance automation, teams that want automated evidence evaluation and audit preparation, and companies that prefer a focused, modern compliance platform over a large GRC suite. Secureframe is best for: companies wanting the largest integration ecosystem, organizations evaluating OneTrust GRC suite integration, and companies that prefer an established platform.

## FAQ

### Does Secureframe's OneTrust acquisition affect customers?

Secureframe continues to operate as a standalone product within OneTrust's GRC portfolio. The acquisition has expanded Secureframe's enterprise capabilities but some customers report changes in pricing and support structure.

### Which platform has better SOC 2 Type II readiness speed?

Both platforms enable SOC 2 Type II readiness in 6 to 12 weeks. Spire's AI audit agent may reduce the readiness assessment phase by automating evidence gap analysis. Secureframe's integration-rich approach may reduce integration setup time.

### Do both platforms support custom control frameworks?

Spire provides flexible control mapping for custom frameworks and multi-framework alignment. Secureframe, through OneTrust, offers extensive enterprise GRC customization capabilities.

### Can I use my own auditor with Secureframe?

Yes. Secureframe has a network of 150+ partner CPA firms, but you can use any licensed auditor. The platform supports evidence export for auditor review. Spire similarly supports any licensed CPA firm.

### Which platform is easier for non-technical teams?

Secureframe's guided onboarding and compliance manager support make it the best choice for non-technical teams that want a human partner. Spire's AI agent handles evidence evaluation and gap analysis automatically, but initial integration setup requires API access to your infrastructure.
