The total first-year cost of SOC 2 compliance for a startup ranges from $30,000 to $150,000 depending on company size, chosen auditor, and whether you pursue Type I or Type II certification. The audit fee alone accounts for $8,000 to $40,000, but the largest hidden cost is internal engineering time — typically 100 to 300 hours diverted from product development.

## What Does the Audit Fee Cover in a SOC 2 Engagement?

The audit fee pays for the CPA firm's work in evaluating your controls, testing evidence, and issuing the formal SOC 2 report. For a Type I audit, fees range from $8,000 to $25,000 for startups with fewer than 50 employees. Type II audits cost $15,000 to $40,000 because the auditor must test control operating effectiveness over the full observation period.

Auditor selection is the highest-leverage cost decision. Boutique CPA firms that specialize in startup SOC 2 engagements typically charge $8,000 to $18,000 for a Type I. Big Four firms charge $30,000 to $100,000+ for the same scope. A 2025 Cloud Security Alliance survey found that 84% of enterprise procurement teams accept SOC 2 reports from boutique auditors, making the premium for Big Four firms unnecessary for most startups.

The SOC 2 audit fee was analyzed in detail by Atlant Security in March 2026, confirming that scope is the primary cost driver. Every additional trust service criterion — Availability, Confidentiality, Processing Integrity, or Privacy — adds controls, policies, and auditor testing time.

## How Much Does a Compliance Automation Platform Cost?

Compliance automation platforms — Vanta, Drata, Secureframe, Sprinto — charge annual subscriptions ranging from $7,500 to $20,000 for SOC 2 Type II coverage at the entry tier. Multi-framework plans that include ISO 27001 or HIPAA range from $15,000 to $30,000 per year.

Pricing is not publicly listed by any of the major platforms. SOC2Scout reported in March 2026 that typical Series A companies pay $15,000 to $20,000 per year for Vanta's SOC 2 module, while Drata entry pricing runs $7,500 to $15,000. Secureframe and Sprinto compete at similar ranges.

The compliance platform is approximately 25% to 35% of total first-year compliance spend. Saving $5,000 on the platform by choosing one with fewer integrations may increase manual evidence collection time, offsetting the platform savings.

Platforms automate evidence collection from cloud infrastructure, identity providers, and code repositories. A 2025 study by the AICPA found that companies using compliance automation reduced audit preparation time by 67% and eliminated 83% of evidence gaps that typically cause audit delays.

## What Are the Hidden SOC 2 Costs That Startups Miss?

The largest hidden SOC 2 cost is internal engineering time. Most CTOs do not budget for the 100 to 300 hours their team will spend on evidence collection, policy documentation, control implementation, and auditor meetings. At a fully-loaded engineering cost of $100 to $180 per hour, this represents $15,000 to $54,000 in opportunity cost — the single largest line item in most startup SOC 2 budgets.

The second hidden cost is security tooling. Startups that lack endpoint monitoring, vulnerability scanning, or centralized logging may need to purchase new tools to satisfy SOC 2 control requirements. Budget $5,000 to $30,000 for security tool upgrades in the first year.

Penetration testing is not strictly required by SOC 2, but most enterprise customers expect an annual pen test alongside the SOC 2 report. Pen tests cost $5,000 to $15,000 for a standard scope covering external network and application testing.

The cost breakdown published by 100x Engineering in January 2026 highlighted that first-year costs for Series A startups targeting enterprise customers range from $60,000 to $120,000 with ongoing annual costs of $25,000 to $50,000.

## How Much Does SOC 2 Cost in Year Two and Beyond?

SOC 2 compliance costs drop 30% to 50% in subsequent years once the compliance infrastructure is established. Year two costs typically range from $18,000 to $90,000 depending on company size and complexity.

Annual renewal costs break down as: audit fee renewal ($12,000 to $40,000), compliance platform renewal ($7,500 to $20,000), penetration testing ($5,000 to $15,000), internal staff time for evidence review and policy updates ($5,000 to $20,000), and security tooling renewals ($5,000 to $30,000).

The annual re-certification Type II audit is typically faster and less expensive than the initial certification because the evidence infrastructure is already in place and the auditor is familiar with the environment. Most startups report year-two audit fees that are 30% to 40% lower than year one.

## How Can Startups Reduce SOC 2 Costs?

Startups can reduce SOC 2 costs through four levers. First, narrow the scope to the Security trust service criterion only — do not add Availability, Confidentiality, or Privacy unless a specific customer requires them. Second, use a compliance automation platform to reduce engineering time spent on evidence collection. Third, choose a boutique or startup-specialist CPA firm rather than a Big Four auditor. Fourth, use cloud-native infrastructure (AWS, GCP, Azure) with well-configured controls that are auditor-friendly.

Combined, these four levers can reduce first-year SOC 2 Type II costs from $60,000 to $120,000 down to $30,000 to $50,000 for a startup with fewer than 30 employees.

The cost reduction opportunity was quantified by SOC2Scout in 2026, showing that startups using all four levers achieved Type II certification for $20,000 to $35,000 total in some cases.

## FAQ

### Is SOC 2 worth it for a pre-revenue startup?

No. SOC 2 is worth pursuing when you have paying customers or an enterprise deal that requires it. Pre-revenue startups should focus on product-market fit and basic security hygiene, not formal certification. The typical trigger for SOC 2 is a customer contract clause or security questionnaire.

### How much does a SOC 2 readiness assessment cost?

A readiness assessment or gap analysis costs $5,000 to $25,000 depending on scope and whether it is performed by an independent consultant or the auditor. Most startups find that using a compliance automation platform's built-in gap analysis is sufficient and cheaper than a standalone assessment.

### Can we do SOC 2 without a compliance platform?

Yes, but it requires significantly more engineering time. Manual evidence collection takes 4 to 6 months of preparation compared to 6 to 10 weeks with automation. The engineering time cost typically exceeds the platform subscription fee for companies with more than 15 employees.

### What is the cheapest way to get SOC 2 certified?

The cheapest path is: narrow scope to Security TSC only, use an entry-tier compliance platform ($7,500 to $10,000/year), hire a boutique CPA firm ($8,000 to $15,000 for Type I), and have engineering lead the process internally rather than hiring a consultant.
