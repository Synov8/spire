The Higher Education Community Vendor Assessment Toolkit (HECVAT) is the de-facto security assessment standard for universities and colleges, used by over 400 institutions across the United States, and edtech vendors that maintain HECVAT-ready responses reduce university procurement cycle time by 60% according to a 2025 Internet2 community survey. The HECVAT is the most comprehensive industry-specific security questionnaire, with over 200 questions across 11 domains.

## What Are the Three HECVAT Versions?

HECVAT Lite: approximately 80 questions for initial vendor screening. Used for quick assessments and low-risk vendors. HECVAT Full: approximately 200+ questions across all 11 domains. Required for vendors with access to institutional data. HECVAT Targeted: a custom version for specific procurement scenarios where only certain domains are in scope.

## What Are the 11 HECVAT Domains?

Data Stewardship and Governance, Security Program Management, Access Control, Data Encryption, Application Security and Patch Management, Network Security, Logging and Monitoring, Incident Response, Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery, Vendor and Third-Party Management, and Physical Security.

## What FERPA-Specific Questions Does HECVAT Include?

HECVAT includes FERPA-specific questions about: directory information handling (what data is designated as directory information), education record access controls (FERPA restricts access to education records), data sharing with third parties (FERPA consent requirements), and data deletion procedures for student records.

## FAQ

### Do all universities require HECVAT?

Over 400 US universities and colleges use HECVAT directly. Many more accept HECVAT as an alternative to their custom questionnaires. It is the most widely recognized higher education security assessment.

### How long does HECVAT Full take to complete?

HECVAT Full (200+ questions) takes 4 to 10 hours for a prepared team with existing response materials. First-time completion without prepared responses: 15 to 25 hours.

### What HECVAT score do universities expect?

HECVAT uses a traffic light scoring system (Green, Yellow, Red). Universities generally expect Green (fully compliant) or Yellow (partially compliant with documented remediation) across all domains. Red scores in any domain typically trigger additional review or rejection.
