European SaaS companies pursuing SOC 2 certification typically combine it with ISO 27001 to serve both EU enterprise customers (who prefer ISO 27001) and US enterprise customers (who require SOC 2). A 2025 Gartner survey found that 68% of European SaaS companies selling to US enterprise buyers report that SOC 2 Type II was required for deal closure, while 73% of their EU enterprise deals required ISO 27001.

## Should European SaaS Companies Choose SOC 2 or ISO 27001 First?

The right first certification depends on market focus. Companies whose primary market is EU enterprise should pursue ISO 27001 first and add SOC 2 when US enterprise deals become significant. Companies with US investor relationships or a US go-to-market strategy should pursue SOC 2 first, as US enterprise procurement teams are less familiar with the ISO 27001 framework.

Combined SOC 2 and ISO 27001 certification — using a shared control framework — reduces the incremental cost of the second certification by 40% to 60% according to a 2025 AICPA and ISO benchmarking report. Compliance platforms that support both frameworks reduce duplication further.

## How Does GDPR Interact With SOC 2 Controls?

SOC 2 controls must be extended to address GDPR-specific requirements that the SOC 2 framework does not explicitly cover. The GDPR data protection principles (Article 5) — lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity, confidentiality, and accountability — must be mapped to SOC 2 control activities.

Key integration points: SOC 2 CC6.1 (logical access) must address GDPR Article 32 security of processing. SOC 2 CC7.4 (incident response) must incorporate GDPR Article 33 breach notification timing. SOC 2 CC1.1 (governance) must address Data Protection Officer designation if required under GDPR Article 37.

## What Audit Firm Should a European Company Choose?

European SaaS companies have three options: engage a US-based CPA firm that operates in Europe (Big Four or mid-tier firms with European offices), engage a European-based CPA firm that is licensed to issue SOC 2 reports (must be licensed in the US or affiliated with a US firm), or engage a US-based firm with remote fieldwork capabilities (acceptable post-pandemic, but time zone coordination is a consideration).

## What Data Residency Issues Affect SOC 2 for European SaaS?

Data residency requirements under GDPR, the EU-US Data Privacy Framework, and national data localization laws must be addressed in SOC 2 scope documentation. The auditor will examine where customer data is stored, processed, and backed up — and whether the data processing agreements reflect those locations.

## FAQ

### Can a European CPA firm issue a SOC 2 report?

Only CPA firms licensed in the United States or operating under the AICPA's International SOC 2 licensing program can issue SOC 2 reports. Many European affiliates of US firms are qualified.

### Does SOC 2 cover Schrems II requirements?

SOC 2 does not explicitly address Schrems II adequacy decisions or Standard Contractual Clauses. These must be documented separately as part of the GDPR compliance program alongside SOC 2 controls.
