A structured security questionnaire knowledge base — containing 100 to 300 pre-written, reviewed, and approved answers — reduces per-questionnaire response time from 10 to 20 hours down to 2 to 5 hours, and companies with maintained knowledge bases answer questionnaires with 95% consistency compared to 70% for ad-hoc responses, according to a 2025 Shared Assessments knowledge management study. The knowledge base is the single highest-ROI investment for questionnaire management.

## What Answers Should Go in the Knowledge Base?

Include answers for the top 100 to 200 questions that appear most frequently across your security questionnaires. Segment by topic: general security program (certifications, scope, team structure), access control (authentication, authorization, MFA, provisioning), encryption (data at rest, data in transit, key management), incident response (plan, testing, notification timeline), business continuity and disaster recovery (RTO, RPO, DR testing), vendor management (assessment process, tiering, monitoring), data privacy (data classification, retention, subject rights, cross-border transfers), and infrastructure security (cloud provider, network security, vulnerability management).

## How Should the Knowledge Base Be Structured?

Organize by topic category (not by question), with each entry containing: question(s) the answer addresses, standard response (2 to 5 sentences answering the question), technical details (specific configuration values, tool names, version numbers), evidence reference (link to supporting evidence document or control), last reviewed date and reviewer.

## How Often Should Knowledge Base Answers Be Reviewed?

Review all answers quarterly for accuracy. Update immediately when infrastructure, tools, or policies change. Mark answers as reviewed with a timestamp. Flag stale answers (older than 6 months without review) for priority review.

## FAQ

### How long does it take to build an initial knowledge base?

2 to 4 weeks of dedicated effort to build a 100-answer knowledge base. Use past questionnaire responses as the source material. Prioritize the most frequently asked questions first.

### Who should maintain the knowledge base?

The compliance team owns the knowledge base. Subject matter experts in engineering, legal, and privacy review and approve answers in their domain. A single designated owner ensures consistency and coordinates updates.

### Should the knowledge base be shared with customers?

Selectively. The general security program answers and certification information are safe to share publicly. Configuration-specific answers with security-sensitive details should be kept internal and shared only under NDA.
