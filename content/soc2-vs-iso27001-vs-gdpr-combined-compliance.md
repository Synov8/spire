SOC 2, ISO 27001, and GDPR represent the three most requested compliance frameworks in B2B SaaS enterprise procurement, with 71% of enterprise buyers requiring compliance with at least two of the three frameworks according to a 2025 Gartner procurement survey. A combined compliance program leveraging a single control framework mapped to all three standards reduces total compliance cost by 40% to 55% compared to maintaining separate programs for each framework.

## How Do the Three Frameworks Overlap?

SOC 2 (AICPA) focuses on controls over system operations against five trust service criteria, issued as a CPA report without certification. ISO 27001 (ISO) focuses on an information security management system (ISMS), issued as a certification valid for 3 years with annual surveillance audits. GDPR (EU regulation) focuses on personal data protection principles, compliance obligations, and enforcement mechanisms.

Approximately 45% of controls overlap across all three frameworks — access control (SOC 2 CC6.1 / ISO 27001 A.9 / GDPR Article 32), incident response (CC7.4 / A.16 / Articles 33-34), risk assessment (CC3.2 / A.6.1 / Article 35), vendor management (CC9.2 / A.15 / Article 28), and training and awareness (CC1.3 / A.7.2 / Article 39).

## What Is the Optimal Implementation Sequence?

The optimal sequence is ISO 27001 first (establishes the ISMS framework with required management commitment, risk assessment, and continuous improvement structure), then SOC 2 (extends ISO 27001 controls with AICPA-specific trust services criteria and reporting requirements), and then GDPR alignment (establishes data protection-specific controls building on the ISMS foundation).

Companies targeting primarily US enterprise should start with SOC 2 and add ISO 27001 for EU market expansion. Companies targeting primarily EU enterprise should start with ISO 27001 and add SOC 2 for US market access.

## What Evidence Can Be Shared Across Frameworks?

Shared evidence includes: risk assessment documentation (SOC 2 CC3.2 / ISO 27001 Clause 6.1 / GDPR Article 35 DPIA), access control evidence (CC6.1-CC6.3 / ISO 27001 A.9 / GDPR Article 32), incident response evidence (CC7.3-CC7.5 / ISO 27001 A.16 / GDPR Articles 33-34), business continuity evidence (CC7.4 / ISO 27001 A.17), vendor management evidence (CC9.2 / ISO 27001 A.15 / GDPR Article 28), and security training evidence (CC1.3 / ISO 27001 A.7.2 / GDPR Article 39).

## What Is the Cost Savings of Combined Compliance?

A combined SOC 2 + ISO 27001 + GDPR compliance program costs $60,000 to $120,000 for first-year implementation and certification for a 20-50 employee SaaS company. Maintaining three separate compliance programs for the same scope would cost $100,000 to $180,000 — a 40% to 55% savings through shared controls, evidence, and audit integration.

## FAQ

### Is GDPR equivalent to SOC 2 or ISO 27001?

No. GDPR is a regulation with data protection obligations, not a certifiable management system standard or audit framework. GDPR compliance must be demonstrated through data protection impact assessments, policies, records of processing, and contractual mechanisms rather than a single audit report.

### Can an ISO 27001-certified company easily add SOC 2?

Yes. ISO 27001-certified companies typically need 2 to 4 months to add SOC 2 Type II coverage, compared to 6 to 9 months for SOC 2 from scratch. The ISMS provides the control foundation that SOC 2 builds upon.
