SOC 2 CC6.1 requires employee security awareness training, and a 2026 KnowBe4 security awareness report found that organizations with effective training programs reduce phishing susceptibility from 34% to under 5% within 12 months. Security awareness training is one of the highest-ROI compliance activities — a small investment in training prevents incidents that could cost millions. This guide covers building an effective SOC 2 security awareness training program.

## What SOC 2 Training Requirements Apply?

CC6.1 requires that personnel are "trained on their information security responsibilities." Specific requirements include: initial security training upon hire, annual refresher training, role-specific training (engineers need different training than sales), training on specific policies (acceptable use, data handling, password, incident reporting), and training completion tracking and evidence. Training must be documented, tracked, and verifiable for audit evidence.

## What Should Your Training Curriculum Include?

Annual training topics: information security policy overview and key requirements, acceptable use policy (company devices, personal devices, internet usage), password policy and MFA requirements, phishing and social engineering awareness, data classification and handling procedures, incident reporting procedures (what to report and how), remote work security requirements, and role-specific training (engineers: secure coding, CI/CD security; customer-facing: data privacy, customer data handling).

## How Often Should Training Be Delivered?

Training cadence: initial security training within the first week of employment. Annual comprehensive refresher training (all employees). Quarterly security awareness micro-trainings (5-minute modules on specific topics). Monthly phishing simulation emails. Just-in-time training when policy changes or incidents occur. The most effective programs combine annual comprehensive training with ongoing micro-training and phishing simulations.

## What Phishing Simulation Program Is Required?

Implement quarterly phishing simulations: send simulated phishing emails to all employees. Track who reports the email (correct behavior) vs who clicks (needs retraining). Immediate remedial training for clickers. Track organizational phishing susceptibility rate (target under 5%). Use diverse phishing scenarios (credentials, attachments, links, urgency-based). Never punish employees who click — use it as a coaching opportunity. Report phishing metrics to management quarterly.

## How Do You Track Training Completion?

Track for each training: employee name, training completed date, training score (if tested), and next training due date. Maintain 100% completion rate for annual training. Track completion by department. Escalate non-completion to managers after 14 days overdue. Report training completion metrics monthly in compliance dashboard. Store training records for at least 2 years for SOC 2 evidence.

## What Evidence Should You Prepare for Your Auditor?

Your auditor will request: security awareness training policy documenting requirements and frequency, training curriculum description, annual training records showing 100% completion, phishing simulation results showing susceptibility rate below 5%, role-specific training records for engineering and customer-facing staff, new hire training completion within first week, and training improvement documentation showing year-over-year improvement in phishing susceptibility.
