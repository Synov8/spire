A compliance risk register — documenting identified risks, their scores, and mitigation controls — satisfies SOC 2 CC3.1 through CC3.3 requirements and provides a structured basis for the annual risk assessment, yet 56% of first-time SOC 2 candidates create a risk register that is either too high-level or too IT-focused according to a 2025 AICPA risk management review. The risk register must cover all risk categories, not just technology risks.

## What Risks Should the Register Include?

The risk register should document strategic risks (competition, market changes, funding, partnership dependencies), operational risks (system reliability, personnel dependencies, process failures, supply chain), technology risks (security breaches, data loss, infrastructure failure, legacy system risk), compliance risks (regulatory changes, contractual non-compliance, certification lapses), and financial risks (fraud, budget overruns, revenue concentration).

## What Fields Should Each Risk Entry Include?

Each risk entry should include: risk ID and description, risk category, inherent likelihood (1 to 5) and impact (1 to 5), inherent risk score (likelihood × impact), identified date and owner, control reference(s) mapping to this risk, residual likelihood (1 to 5) and impact (1 to 5), residual risk score, risk treatment (accept, mitigate, transfer, avoid), and review date.

## How Often Should the Risk Register Be Reviewed?

At minimum annually, aligned with the SOC 2 risk assessment cycle. Leading practice is quarterly review with the compliance committee to capture emerging risks.

## FAQ

### What is an acceptable residual risk score?

Risk scores above 15 (out of 25) typically require active treatment plans. Scores below 8 may be accepted. The compliance committee should define the risk appetite threshold — the score above which treatment is required.

### Should the risk register include risks from subservice organizations?

Yes. Risks introduced by subservice organizations — cloud provider outage, subservice security breach — should be included in the risk register with mitigating controls.

### How do you link the risk register to SOC 2 controls?

Each risk in the register should reference the specific SOC 2 controls that mitigate it. Each SOC 2 control should reference the risks it addresses. This bidirectional mapping is a key auditor review item.
