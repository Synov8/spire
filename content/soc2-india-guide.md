India's SaaS ecosystem is the fastest growing globally, with a 2026 NASSCOM report finding that 73% of Indian SaaS companies targeting US and European customers now pursue SOC 2 certification — up from 41% in 2024. India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) and CERT-In cybersecurity requirements align with SOC 2 controls, making certification achievable for Indian SaaS companies with proper planning. This guide covers SOC 2 compliance for Indian SaaS companies.

## How Does SOC 2 Align with India's DPDPA?

The DPDPA's data protection requirements align with SOC 2 controls: reasonable security safeguards (Section 8) maps to SOC 2 Security criteria including CC6.1 and CC6.7. Personal data breach notification maps to CC7.3 incident response. Data principal rights including access and correction map to SOC 2 access controls. Data retention limitation maps to CC6.6 data backup and retention policies.

## What CERT-In Requirements Apply to Indian SaaS Companies?

CERT-In directions require reporting of cybersecurity incidents within 6 hours. Align your SOC 2 incident response procedures (CC7.3) with CERT-In notification timelines. Document your CERT-In incident classification and reporting process. Implement audit logging (CC7.2) that captures information needed for CERT-In reports. Include CERT-In notification templates in your incident response playbooks.

## What MeitY Guidelines Should You Follow?

The Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) provides guidelines for cloud services and information security. Key areas include: data localization requirements for sensitive personal data, information security practices aligned with ISO 27001, and cybersecurity incident reporting procedures. Map MeitY guidelines to SOC 2 controls in your system description.

## How Do You Handle Data Localization for Indian Customers?

DPDPA permits cross-border data transfers subject to certain restrictions. Document where customer data is processed and stored. Implement data residency controls using Indian cloud regions (AWS Mumbai, Azure Central India, GCP Mumbai). Verify cloud provider SOC 2 reports for Indian regions. Document cross-border data transfer provisions in customer agreements.

## What International Data Transfer Considerations Apply?

DPDPA allows cross-border data transfer to certain notified countries. Document your cross-border transfer framework. Implement appropriate safeguards for data transfers. Include cross-border transfer disclosures in your privacy policy and data processing agreements. Map data flows by jurisdiction in your system description.

## What Evidence Should an Indian SaaS Company Prepare for SOC 2?

Your auditor will request: DPDPA compliance documentation mapped to SOC 2 controls, CERT-In incident reporting procedures within 6-hour timelines, MeitY guideline alignment documentation, data localization controls showing Indian customer data storage, cloud provider SOC 2 reports for Indian regions, cross-border data transfer documentation, and data protection impact assessments for high-risk processing activities.
