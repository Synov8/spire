A documented compliance roles and responsibilities matrix — typically structured as a RACI chart — demonstrates to SOC 2 auditors that the organization has assigned clear ownership for each control and compliance activity, and companies with a published RACI matrix receive 30% fewer control ownership findings according to a 2025 audit quality review. The matrix should cover all in-scope controls and supporting compliance activities.

## What Activities Should the RACI Matrix Cover?

The RACI matrix should cover policy management (development, review, approval, communication), risk assessment (identification, analysis, response, monitoring), control activities (design, implementation, operation, monitoring), evidence collection (automated configuration, manual upload, quality review), audit management (scope definition, evidence delivery, auditor coordination, finding remediation), vendor risk management (assessment, approval, periodic review), training and awareness (development, delivery, completion tracking), and incident response (identification, containment, notification, post-mortem).

## Who Should Be Responsible vs Accountable?

Responsible roles perform the work — the control owner who implements and monitors the control. Accountable roles own the outcome — the compliance manager or control lead who verifies the control is effective. For access reviews: the system administrator is responsible for running the review reports; the compliance manager is accountable for ensuring reviews are completed.

## How Does RACI Change as the Company Grows?

At under 25 employees, one person (the engineering lead) is responsible and accountable for most activities. At 25 to 50 employees, responsibilities distribute: engineering owns technical controls, compliance manager owns administrative controls. At 50+ employees, the full RACI separate model applies with distinct responsible, accountable, and consulted roles.

## FAQ

### Does SOC 2 require a RACI matrix?

SOC 2 does not explicitly require a RACI matrix, but CC1.2 requires defined roles and responsibilities. A RACI matrix is the most common and auditor-friendly way to satisfy this requirement.

### How often should the RACI matrix be updated?

Update the RACI matrix when organizational changes occur — new hires, role changes, team restructuring — and at least annually alongside the risk assessment update.

### Should contractors be included in the RACI matrix?

Yes. Any contractor performing compliance activities or acting as a control owner should be included in the RACI matrix with their scope of responsibility clearly defined.
