GDPR compliance for B2B SaaS platforms serving EU data subjects requires a comprehensive data protection program that intersects with SOC 2 Privacy TSC requirements — and companies with structured GDPR compliance programs complete data subject access requests (DSARs) in an average of 12 days compared to 35 days for ad-hoc processes, according to a 2025 IAPP GDPR benchmarking study. GDPR enforcement continues to accelerate with €4.5 billion in fines issued since 2018.

## What GDPR Obligations Apply to SaaS Platforms?

If your SaaS platform is a data controller (determines purpose and means of processing), you must register with the relevant supervisory authority, appoint a DPO if processing at scale, maintain records of processing activities per Article 30. If your platform is a data processor (processes on behalf of a controller), you must have a data processing agreement (DPA) with each controller, only process data on documented instructions, and assist controllers with their compliance obligations.

## How Do You Handle Data Subject Access Requests?

DSARs must be responded to within one month (Article 12, extension possible to 2 months for complex requests). DSAR handling requires processes to verify requester identity, locate all personal data across systems, redact third-party data, provide the response in a commonly used electronic format, and document the request and response for compliance records. Automated DSAR handling tools reduce response time by 60% to 80%.

## What Cross-Border Transfer Mechanisms Are Valid?

Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) issued by the EU Commission remain the most common transfer mechanism. Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs) are an alternative for multinational groups. EU-US Data Privacy Framework (DPF) provides a certification mechanism for US companies. Transfer Impact Assessments (TIAs) are required alongside SCCs to assess the legal environment of the recipient country.

## FAQ

### Does SOC 2 Privacy TSC satisfy GDPR requirements?

Partially. SOC 2 Privacy TSC covers some GDPR requirements (data inventory, access controls, breach notification procedures) but does not address GDPR-specific obligations including DPO appointment, records of processing activities, DSAR procedures, DPIAs, or cross-border transfer mechanisms.

### What are the GDPR fine tiers?

Lower tier: up to 10 million EUR or 2% of annual global turnover. Upper tier: up to 20 million EUR or 4% of annual global turnover. Upper tier applies to violations of data subject rights, cross-border transfers, and non-compliance with processing principles.

### Do I need a DPO for my SaaS platform?

A DPO is required if your core activities involve large-scale processing of special category data or systematic monitoring of data subjects on a large scale. For most B2B SaaS platforms processing customer data for service delivery, a DPO may not be legally required but is strongly recommended as a best practice.
