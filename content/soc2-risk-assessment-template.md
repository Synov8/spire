SOC 2 controls CC3.1 through CC3.3 require a documented risk assessment that identifies business and compliance risks, evaluates their likelihood and impact, and maps controls to each identified risk — and 68% of first-time SOC 2 candidates receive findings because their risk assessment lacks sufficient detail or does not align with COSO principles, according to a 2025 AICPA audit findings database. The risk assessment must be entity-wide, not limited to IT risks.

## What Should a SOC 2 Risk Assessment Cover?

The risk assessment must cover strategic risks (competition, market changes, regulatory changes), operational risks (system failure, personnel changes, process failures), compliance risks (regulatory non-compliance, contractual breach), financial risks (fraud, misappropriation, financial reporting errors), and technology risks (security breaches, data loss, system downtime).

Each risk must be documented with: risk description and source, inherent likelihood rating (1 to 5) and impact rating (1 to 5), inherent risk score (likelihood × impact), control(s) mitigating the risk, residual likelihood and impact after controls, and residual risk score.

## What Risk Assessment Methodology Does SOC 2 Require?

SOC 2 does not mandate a specific risk assessment methodology, but the COSO ERM framework is the most commonly used. The methodology must include risk identification (at least annually), risk analysis (using qualitative or quantitative scoring), risk evaluation (comparing risk scores to risk appetite), and risk response (accept, mitigate, transfer, or avoid).

## How Does the Risk Assessment Link to Controls?

Each control in your SOC 2 control set should map to at least one identified risk in the risk assessment. If a control does not map to any risk, either the control is unnecessary or the risk assessment is incomplete. This mapping is a critical auditor review item.

## FAQ

### How often must the risk assessment be updated?

At least annually. More frequent updates are recommended when significant changes occur — new product launches, infrastructure changes, regulatory changes, or security incidents.

### Who should participate in the risk assessment?

The risk assessment should involve cross-functional stakeholders: engineering leadership, product management, legal/compliance, finance, and executive leadership. The board or board committee should review and approve the risk assessment annually.

### What is the difference between inherent and residual risk?

Inherent risk is the risk level before controls are applied. Residual risk is the risk level after controls are applied. The SOC 2 auditor evaluates whether residual risk is within the entity's risk appetite.
