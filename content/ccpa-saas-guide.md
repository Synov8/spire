The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), applies to any for-profit business that collects California residents' personal information and meets certain thresholds, and approximately 60% of B2B SaaS companies serving California customers fall under CCPA scope, according to a 2025 California Privacy Protection Agency analysis. The CCPA is the most comprehensive US state privacy law and has served as the model for 12+ other state privacy laws.

## Does CCPA Apply to Your SaaS Platform?

CCPA applies if your business has annual gross revenue over $25 million, annually buys, receives, or sells personal information of 100,000+ California residents or households (reduced from 50,000 under CPRA), or derives 50%+ of annual revenue from selling or sharing consumers' personal information. The business-to-business (B2B) exemption that previously exempted B2B communications expired January 1, 2026, meaning B2B SaaS platforms must now comply with CCPA for all business contacts.

## What Consumer Rights Does CCPA Grant?

Right to know: consumers can request the categories and specific pieces of personal information collected, sources, business purpose, and third parties with whom it is shared. Right to delete: consumers can request deletion of personal information collected. Right to opt-out: consumers can opt out of the sale or sharing of their personal information. Right to non-discrimination: businesses cannot discriminate against consumers exercising CCPA rights. Right to correct: consumers can request correction of inaccurate personal information. Right to limit use: consumers can limit use of sensitive personal information (CPRA).

## What Privacy Notice Requirements Apply?

CCPA requires a compliant privacy notice that includes categories of personal information collected and the source of each category, business purpose for collection, categories of third parties with whom information is shared, consumer rights under CCPA, and contact information for submitting requests and contacting the Privacy Officer.

## FAQ

### Does SOC 2 Privacy TSC satisfy CCPA requirements?

Partially. SOC 2 Privacy TSC covers some CCPA requirements (privacy notice, data inventory) but does not address CCPA-specific obligations including the right to opt-out of sale/sharing, sensitive personal information limits, or the 15-business-day response SLA.

### What are CCPA penalty amounts?

Civil penalties: $2,500 per violation (non-intentional) and $7,500 per violation (intentional). Private right of action: statutory damages of $100 to $750 per consumer per incident for data breach violations.

### How does CCPA interact with other US state privacy laws?

CCPA serves as the baseline for comprehensive state privacy laws in Colorado (CPA), Connecticut (CTDPA), Virginia (VCDPA), Utah (UCPA), Texas (TDPSA), Oregon, Montana, Iowa, Indiana, Tennessee, and others. Compliance with CCPA provides a strong foundation for multi-state US privacy compliance.
