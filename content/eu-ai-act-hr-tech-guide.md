AI systems used in human resources — including candidate screening, employee performance evaluation, promotion decisions, and workforce analytics — are explicitly classified as high-risk under the EU AI Act Annex III, affecting an estimated 5,000+ HR technology platforms operating in the European market according to a 2026 EU Commission impact assessment. HR tech companies face the most urgent AI Act compliance deadlines because their systems directly impact individual employment rights.

## Why Is HR AI Classified as High-Risk?

The EU AI Act designates AI systems used for recruitment (candidate sourcing, screening, ranking), employee evaluation (performance assessment, promotion decisions), and workforce management (task allocation, monitoring) as high-risk because these systems significantly impact individuals' career opportunities and employment rights. High-risk designation triggers the most stringent compliance requirements including conformity assessment, risk management, human oversight, transparency obligations, and fundamental rights impact assessments.

## What HR Tech Systems Are In-Scope?

In-scope HR tech systems include AI-powered candidate resume screening and ranking, AI-driven skills assessment and testing platforms, automated video interview analysis tools, employee performance scoring and evaluation AI, promotion and career development recommendation systems, workforce analytics that predict employee behavior or performance, and compensation and reward optimization AI.

## What Compliance Timeline Does HR Tech Face?

The EU AI Act enforcement timeline for high-risk systems: February 2025 — Act entered into force. August 2026 — high-risk AI system obligations effective (risk management, data governance, transparency, human oversight, accuracy). August 2027 — full conformity assessment requirement with CE marking. HR tech companies must achieve compliance by August 2026 for the obligations phase and August 2027 for full certification.

## FAQ

### Do HR tech companies need a fundamental rights impact assessment?

Yes. High-risk AI system deployers — companies using HR AI to evaluate employees — must conduct a Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA) before deployment. The FRIA evaluates risks to fundamental rights (non-discrimination, privacy, data protection) and identifies mitigation measures.

### Does the EU AI Act apply to US-based HR tech companies?

Yes, if their AI systems are used by EU-based employers or process data of EU job applicants and employees. Extraterritorial scope applies to both providers and deployers of AI systems in the EU market.

### What penalties apply for non-compliance?

Fines for high-risk AI system non-compliance: up to 35 million EUR or 7% of worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher. HR tech companies should prioritize compliance given the high-risk classification.
