A structured compliance roadmap prevents the last-minute scramble before each audit — a 2026 Gartner compliance planning survey found that organizations with published compliance roadmaps complete their audits 30% faster and with 50% fewer follow-up questions. This template provides a 12-month compliance roadmap structure that you can customize for your organization.

## Q1: Foundation and Assessment

January: complete annual risk assessment update. Review and update all policies for the new year. Assign control owners and confirm their understanding of responsibilities. Schedule annual compliance activities (penetration test, training, access reviews). February: complete information security policy review and re-acknowledgement. Begin employee security awareness training rollout. Review vendor risk assessments — identify any with expiring SOC 2 reports. March: complete Q1 access review. Complete Q1 vulnerability scanning. Complete employee training by March 31. Review evidence quality and fill any gaps from Q4 of the previous year.

## Q2: Testing and Preparation

April: schedule and conduct annual penetration test. Begin pre-audit readiness assessment. Identify control gaps from penetration test results. Begin evidence collection for the current audit period. May: complete Q2 access review. Complete Q2 vulnerability scanning. Continue penetration test remediation. Finalize evidence organization by control. Conduct pre-audit walkthrough with internal stakeholders. June: contact audit firm to confirm fieldwork dates. Prepare audit evidence packages. Verify all evidence is complete and accurate. Conduct employee auditor interview preparation sessions.

## Q3: Audit and Remediation

July: conduct annual SOC 2 audit fieldwork. Support auditor evidence requests and employee interviews. Attend opening and closing meetings. Receive preliminary findings. August: complete Q3 access review. Complete Q3 vulnerability scanning. Address any audit findings with remediation plans. Provide remediation evidence to auditor. Obtain final audit report. September: conduct annual business continuity and disaster recovery test. Document test results. Review and update incident response procedures based on lessons learned.

## Q4: Review and Planning

October: complete Q4 access review. Complete Q4 vulnerability scanning. Begin evidence collection for next year's audit. Review year's compliance metrics and trends. November: complete annual compliance program effectiveness assessment. Review all policies for annual updates. Update system description for any changes during the year. Conduct annual risk assessment update. December: finalize year-end compliance report for board. Archive all evidence from the past year. Plan next year's compliance roadmap. Celebrate the year's compliance achievements with the team.

## Key Milestones and Dependencies

Penetration test must be completed before July audit (schedule in April). Access reviews must be completed quarterly before the end of each quarter. Vulnerability scans must be completed quarterly before the end of each quarter. Evidence collection is ongoing but must be finalized before the June audit. Policy reviews must be completed in January for current-year policies. Risk assessment must be completed by end of Q1. Business continuity testing must be completed in Q3.
