SOC for Cybersecurity (SOC for Cyber) was introduced by the AICPA as a complementary framework to SOC 2 — a 2026 AICPA survey found that while SOC 2 adoption reached 68% among B2B SaaS companies, SOC for Cyber adoption was only 12%, but growing 35% year over year. The two frameworks serve different purposes and can be used together for comprehensive assurance. This guide compares SOC 2 and SOC for Cybersecurity.

## What Is the Difference in Focus?

SOC 2 focuses on controls related to the five trust service criteria (Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, Privacy) as they relate to a specific service or system. SOC for Cybersecurity focuses on the effectiveness of an organization's overall cybersecurity risk management program, providing a broader enterprise-wide perspective. SOC 2 is a controls audit. SOC for Cyber is a program maturity assessment.

## Who Is the Audience for Each?

SOC 2 report audience: existing customers, prospective customers, and procurement teams evaluating a specific service. SOC for Cybersecurity report audience: board of directors, executive management, investors and insurers, and regulators requiring cybersecurity program assurance. SOC for Cyber is primarily an internal governance tool, while SOC 2 is primarily a customer-facing certification.

## What Are the Report Contents?

SOC 2 report includes: auditor opinion on control effectiveness, system description, control descriptions and testing results, and findings and exceptions. SOC for Cybersecurity report includes: auditor opinion on cybersecurity risk management program effectiveness, description of the program's components (governance, risk management, processes), and no detailed control testing. SOC for Cyber is descriptive — it describes the program, not specific control testing.

## What Are the Cost and Time Differences?

SOC 2 Type II: $15K-$40K for first year, 3-6 months preparation, annual thereafter. SOC for Cybersecurity: $30K-$75K for first year depending on organization size, 4-8 months preparation, annual refresh recommended. SOC for Cyber is typically 50-100% more expensive because it covers the entire organization rather than a defined scope of systems.

## Which Should Your SaaS Company Choose?

SOC 2 should be your first certification for B2B SaaS — it's what customers ask for in procurement. SOC for Cybersecurity should be added when: you need board-level assurance on cybersecurity program effectiveness, your investors or insurers require it, you're in a highly regulated industry needing cybersecurity program validation, or you have already achieved SOC 2 and want additional assurance. Most SaaS companies prioritize SOC 2 first and consider SOC for Cybersecurity at a later stage.

## Can You Use Both Together?

Yes, and they complement each other well. SOC 2 demonstrates control effectiveness for customer-facing services. SOC for Cyber demonstrates cybersecurity program maturity for governance stakeholders. Together they provide comprehensive assurance: SOC 2 for operational controls and SOC for Cyber for strategic cybersecurity management. Some organizations produce both reports from the same audit engagement, reducing cost compared to separate engagements.
