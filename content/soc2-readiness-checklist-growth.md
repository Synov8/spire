Growth-stage companies with 50 to 200 employees managing SOC 2 across multiple trust service criteria require a dedicated compliance team of 2 to 4 people and annual compliance costs of $120,000 to $300,000, according to a 2025 IANS Research survey. At this stage, SOC 2 expands from a certification project to an ongoing compliance program with continuous monitoring, multi-framework alignment, and board-level reporting.

## What Trust Service Criteria Should Growth-Stage Companies Cover?

Growth-stage companies serving enterprise customers should implement all five trust service criteria: Security (mandatory), Availability (for customer SLA commitments), Confidentiality (for handling sensitive customer data), Processing Integrity (for data processing accuracy guarantees), and Privacy (for regulated data handling). Each additional criterion adds 15 to 25 controls and increases annual audit cost by 20% to 30%.

## What Compliance Team Structure Works at Growth Stage?

The compliance team at growth stage typically includes a compliance manager (full-time), a security engineer supporting evidence automation (full-time or shared), a legal or privacy counsel for Privacy TSC coverage (shared), and a board-level compliance sponsor for governance reporting. Total compliance team cost at growth stage: $250,000 to $450,000 annually including salaries and tools.

## How Should Growth-Stage Companies Handle Auditor Relationships?

Growth-stage companies should rotate auditors every 3 to 5 years or conduct a formal auditor RFP every 3 years to ensure competitive pricing and fresh perspective. Annual audit fees at this stage range from $40,000 to $80,000 for full-scope Type II audits covering all five trust service criteria.

## FAQ

### How many controls does a growth-stage company need?

Growth-stage companies with all five TSC in scope typically manage 120 to 160 controls. Approximately 80 to 100 can be automated; 40 to 60 require manual evidence such as board minutes and training records.

### What frameworks do growth-stage companies typically add?

ISO 27001 is the most common framework added after SOC 2, pursued by 52% of growth-stage SaaS companies. HIPAA, GDPR compliance programs, and SOC 3 follow depending on customer requirements.

### How often should growth-stage companies run penetration testing?

Growth-stage companies should run penetration testing at least annually, with quarterly vulnerability scanning. Enterprise customers increasingly require pen testing every 6 months for contracts exceeding $500K ACV.
