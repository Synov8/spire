Continuous compliance — where evidence is collected automatically on an ongoing schedule rather than manually assembled before each audit — reduces SOC 2 preparation time by 67% and eliminates 83% of evidence gaps that cause audit delays, according to a 2025 AICPA automation impact study. The fundamental difference is whether compliance is a periodic project or an ongoing operational state.

## What Is Point-in-Time Compliance?

Point-in-time compliance is the traditional approach: the company prepares evidence during the 2 to 4 months before the audit or observation period begins. Evidence is collected manually — screenshots, exported logs, manually compiled reports — and organized into spreadsheets or document repositories for auditor review. Between audits, compliance effort drops to near zero, meaning there is no early warning if controls drift out of compliance.

## What Is Continuous Compliance?

Continuous compliance uses automated evidence collection from infrastructure APIs on a recurring schedule — daily, weekly, or monthly — with real-time alerts when configurations drift outside policy boundaries. Evidence is stored with cryptographic timestamps and organized by control. The compliance platform provides continuous readiness assessment so gaps are identified and remediated before the auditor sees them.

## What Are the Cost and Risk Differences?

Point-in-time compliance has lower annual tool cost ($0 to $5,000) but higher periodic effort cost ($15,000 to $50,000 per audit cycle in engineering time) and higher risk of evidence gaps. Continuous compliance has higher annual platform cost ($7,500 to $20,000) but lower ongoing effort ($5,000 to $15,000 per year) and significantly lower audit risk.

## FAQ

### Does continuous compliance eliminate the need for auditors?

No. Continuous compliance replaces manual evidence collection and readiness assessment but does not replace the licensed CPA firm that issues the SOC 2 opinion. The auditor still reviews evidence and issues the report.

### Can I switch from point-in-time to continuous compliance mid-cycle?

Yes. You can implement continuous evidence collection at any point. The remaining observation period evidence will be collected automatically. The switch does not require restarting the observation period.

### What is the ROI of switching to continuous compliance?

ROI analysis: companies switching from manual to continuous compliance save $15,000 to $45,000 per year in engineering time, reduce audit preparation effort by 60% to 80%, and eliminate evidence gap-related audit delays. Payback period: 3 to 6 months.
