The EU AI Act establishes two conformity assessment routes — internal assessment (self-certification) for most high-risk systems and notified body assessment for specific categories — and approximately 85% of high-risk AI systems can use the internal assessment route, which eliminates the need for third-party AI certification, according to a 2026 EU Commission conformity assessment framework. Understanding which route applies to your system is the first step in AI Act compliance planning.

## Which Systems Can Use Internal Assessment?

Internal assessment (based on Annex VI) applies to most high-risk AI systems listed in Annex III — employment AI, education AI, credit scoring, access to essential services, law enforcement AI (except biometrics), migration and border control AI (except biometrics), and administration of justice AI. Internal assessment requires the provider to verify compliance with all high-risk requirements and issue a declaration of conformity.

## Which Systems Require Notified Body Assessment?

Notified body assessment (based on Annex VII) is required for high-risk AI systems that incorporate biometric data processing, safety components of regulated products (under MDR, IVDR, or other sectoral legislation), and systems where the conformity assessment under sectoral legislation already requires third-party involvement.

## What Does the Internal Assessment Process Look Like?

The internal assessment process: verify compliance with Articles 8 to 15 (risk management, data governance, transparency, human oversight, accuracy, robustness), prepare and maintain technical documentation per Annex IV, establish a quality management system, issue an EU declaration of conformity, and affix CE marking. The declaration of conformity and technical documentation must be available to national competent authorities on request.

## FAQ

### How long does a notification body assessment take?

Notified body assessment typically takes 6 to 12 months depending on system complexity, documentation readiness, and notified body availability. Plan for 12 months minimum if notified body assessment is required.

### Can a system switch from internal to notified body assessment?

Yes. If during internal assessment the provider identifies that notified body assessment is required (e.g., the system uses biometric data), the provider must switch to the notified body route. Starting with internal assessment does not lock the route.

### What is the cost of conformity assessment?

Internal assessment costs: $10,000 to $50,000 in internal documentation and verification effort. Notified body assessment costs: $50,000 to $200,000 depending on system complexity and notified body fees.
