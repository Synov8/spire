SOC 2 and SOC 3 reports share the same underlying audit — a 2026 AICPA survey found that 85% of organizations that commission a SOC 2 audit choose to also produce a SOC 3 report for broader distribution. The key difference is SOC 3 is a publicly distributable summary version of the detailed SOC 2 report. This guide compares SOC 2 and SOC 3 to help you decide which you need.

## What Is the Difference Between SOC 2 and SOC 3?

SOC 2 Type II is a detailed audit report containing: auditor opinion, system description, control descriptions, testing procedures and results, and detailed findings and exceptions. SOC 3 is a summary report containing: auditor opinion, general system overview, a seal of compliance, and no detail on control testing or findings. SOC 2 reports are confidential and distributed under NDA. SOC 3 reports are publicly distributable without restriction.

## Who Is the Audience for Each Report?

SOC 2 report audience: enterprise procurement teams (during vendor security reviews), existing customers conducting annual vendor assessments, prospective customers with formal security review processes, and regulatory bodies requiring detailed compliance evidence. SOC 3 report audience: website visitors (trust center), marketing materials, prospects in early-stage evaluation, industry analysts and partners, and any party not willing to sign an NDA.

## How Does the Cost Compare?

SOC 2 Type II audit cost: $15K-$40K depending on scope and complexity. Adding a SOC 3 report to an existing SOC 2 engagement: $500-$3,000 additional. The SOC 3 incremental cost is minimal because the underlying audit work is identical. Most companies choose to add SOC 3 as a cost-effective way to provide public proof of their SOC 2 certification without distributing their confidential SOC 2 report.

## When Should You Choose SOC 2 Only?

Choose SOC 2 only when: your customers always sign NDAs for security reviews, your sales team directly manages prospect security evaluation, you have no public trust center or marketing need for a seal, regulatory requirements don't require public compliance evidence, or cost sensitivity makes even the small SOC 3 add-on unnecessary. Most B2B SaaS companies still benefit from SOC 3 for their trust center.

## When Should You Add SOC 3?

Add SOC 3 when: you maintain a public trust center, prospects begin security evaluation before signing an NDA, your marketing team wants to display a compliance seal, you participate in industry analyst evaluations, you compete against companies displaying compliance seals, or you want to reduce the number of NDA transactions required for basic compliance verification.

## What Dual Reporting Strategy Works Best?

Recommended approach: commission SOC 2 Type II as your primary audit. Add SOC 3 for public distribution. Use your SOC 2 report for active prospect and customer security reviews under NDA. Use your SOC 3 report on your trust center and marketing materials. Provide SOC 2 to enterprise prospects who complete NDA. Provide SOC 3 to earlier-stage prospects and for general marketing. This dual strategy satisfies both detailed and summary compliance verification needs at minimal incremental cost.
