SOC 2 CC7.5 requires that the entity identifies and responds to system breaches, including timely notification of affected parties and relevant authorities. The AICPA's expectation aligns with GDPR Article 33's 72-hour notification requirement for EU data subjects and the 30-day notification standard common in US state breach notification laws. A 2025 Bitkom study found that companies with documented breach notification procedures resolved legal exposure 43% faster than those without predefined notification workflows.

## What Triggers a Breach Notification Under CC7.5?

Breach notification is triggered when the entity confirms unauthorized access to or acquisition of customer data, customer credentials, or system configurations that could enable further unauthorized access. The determination must be made by the incident response team based on the classification matrix established under CC7.3.

Not every security incident requires customer notification. A phishing attempt that no employee fell for does not trigger notification. A phishing attempt that compromised an employee account with access to customer data does. The distinction must be documented in the incident response plan with clear criteria.

## What Timeframes Apply to Breach Notification?

The SOC 2 framework requires "timely" notification but defers to applicable laws and regulations for specific timeframes. GDPR Article 33 mandates notification to supervisory authorities within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach. All 50 US states have breach notification laws with varying requirements — most require notification within 30 days.

The Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 also influences SOC 2 expectations for publicly traded companies, requiring disclosure of material cybersecurity incidents within 4 business days under SEC rules effective December 2023.

## How Should Customer Notification Be Structured?

Each customer notification must include the nature of the breach, the data types involved (PII, credentials, financial data), the measures taken to contain the breach, the remediation steps, and contact information for the response team. Notifications should be drafted with legal counsel and approved before sending.

A 2025 Gartner analysis found that companies using pre-approved notification templates reduced the average notification delay from 19 days to 4 days compared to drafting notifications from scratch during incidents.

## What Evidence Does CC7.5 Require?

The auditor needs documented breach notification procedures with defined criteria and timeframes, evidence that procedures were followed during the Type II period (or a statement that no breaches occurred), legal notification records if a breach occurred, and post-incident review reports. The absence of procedures — even with no breaches — constitutes a CC7.5 deficiency.

## FAQ

### Does CC7.5 require us to notify customers of every incident?

No. CC7.5 requires notification only when the incident constitutes a breach — actual or reasonably likely unauthorized access to customer data. The determination criteria must be documented and consistently applied.

### Do SOC 2 breaches need to be reported to the AICPA?

No. The AICPA does not maintain a breach notification registry. CC7.5 refers to notification of affected customers and regulatory authorities — not the AICPA itself.
