The EU AI Act and GDPR form a complementary regulatory framework for AI systems processing personal data, with the AI Act governing the systemic safety and fundamental rights implications of AI while GDPR governs the data protection aspects. The European Data Protection Board (EDPB) and the European AI Board issued a joint opinion in March 2026 clarifying that both regulations apply simultaneously to AI systems processing personal data — companies cannot claim compliance with one as a substitute for the other.

## Where Do the AI Act and GDPR Overlap?

The most significant overlap areas are: transparency obligations — AI Act Article 50 requires AI interaction disclosure while GDPR Article 13 requires data processing transparency; automated decision-making — AI Act high-risk classification overlaps with GDPR Article 22 (solely automated decision-making); data governance — AI Act Article 10 requires training data quality assessment while GDPR Article 5 requires data minimization and accuracy; and accountability and documentation — both frameworks require documented compliance evidence and governance structures.

The overlap creates a compliance efficiency opportunity. GDPR documentation for automated decision-making can be extended to meet AI Act transparency requirements. AI Act risk assessments can incorporate GDPR data protection impact assessment findings.

## Where Do the AI Act and GDPR Diverge?

The AI Act regulates the AI system itself — its design, development, training data, and deployment context — regardless of whether the system processes personal data. GDPR regulates the processing of personal data regardless of whether AI is involved. An AI system that processes no personal data (pure synthetic data generation with no identifiable information) is still subject to the AI Act but not GDPR.

The enforcement mechanisms also differ. GDPR is enforced by national data protection authorities (one per member state). The AI Act introduces a new enforcement structure with the European AI Board and national market surveillance authorities, creating a dual supervisory framework.

## How Should Companies Manage Combined Compliance?

Establish a unified compliance governance structure that addresses both regulations simultaneously, map AI Act and GDPR requirements to shared control evidence, conduct joint risk assessments covering both fundamental rights impacts and data protection risks, and maintain combined documentation that serves both regulatory frameworks.

## What Are the Joint Enforcement Risks?

A company may face parallel investigations from a data protection authority (GDPR) and a market surveillance authority (AI Act) for the same AI system. Coordinated enforcement between the two authorities is mandated by the AI Act's Article 64, which requires cooperation between market surveillance authorities and data protection authorities.

## FAQ

### Can GDPR compliance substitute for AI Act compliance?

No. The two regulations serve different purposes and have distinct requirements. GDPR compliance covers data protection obligations. AI Act compliance covers AI system safety, transparency, and governance.

### Which regulation takes precedence if they conflict?

Neither regulation takes inherent precedence. The AI Act Article 2(7) provides that data protection obligations under GDPR are not affected by the AI Act, and the AI Act's obligations apply without prejudice to GDPR requirements.
