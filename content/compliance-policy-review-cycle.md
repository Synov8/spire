SOC 2 auditors expect policies to be current, approved, and acknowledged — a 2026 AICPA audit quality study found that 28% of SOC 2 findings relate to stale or unapproved policies. A structured policy review cycle ensures your policies remain current and audit-ready throughout the year. This guide covers the complete policy review lifecycle for SOC 2 compliance.

## What Policies Must Be Reviewed Annually?

Core SOC 2 policies requiring annual review: information security policy (the foundational policy), acceptable use policy, data classification and handling policy, password policy, incident response policy, business continuity and disaster recovery policy, vendor management policy, change management policy, access control policy, and remote work and mobile device policy. Industry-specific policies may apply (HIPAA, PCI, data privacy). Each policy should have a version number, last-review date, and next-review date.

## What Is the Annual Review Process?

The policy review process: schedule reviews for January each year. Each policy owner reviews their policy for accuracy and currency. Update for any regulatory, organizational, or technical changes. Verify references to current controls and systems. Remove outdated references. Submit updated policy for compliance approval. Obtain executive approval (CISO or equivalent). Re-publish and request employee acknowledgement. Archive the previous version. Document the review in your compliance system.

## How Do You Manage Policy Versioning?

Use semantic versioning: major version changes (1.0 to 2.0) for significant policy rewrites, minor version changes (1.0 to 1.1) for minor updates or corrections. Include the version number and last-review date in the policy header. Maintain a policy change log documenting what changed and why. Store current and previous versions for audit evidence. Never overwrite a policy without preserving the previous version. Most auditors want to see at least 2 years of policy history.

## What Approval Workflow Should You Use?

Policy approval workflow: policy owner drafts or updates the policy. Compliance team reviews for completeness and alignment with control framework. Legal review (for data protection, employment-related policies). CISO or security lead approval. CEO approval for the information security policy (SOC 2 requires executive sign-off). Track all approvals with dates and signatures in your compliance system.

## How Do You Manage Employee Policy Acknowledgement?

After policy updates: notify all employees of updated policies. Require acknowledgement within 14 days. Send automated reminders to non-acknowledgers after 7 and 14 days. Escalate to managers after 21 days. Store signed acknowledgements as SOC 2 evidence in your compliance platform. Track acknowledgement completion rate as a compliance metric (target 100%). Re-run acknowledgement campaigns annually for all policies.

## What Policy Evidence Should You Maintain for SOC 2?

Your auditor will request: current versions of all SOC 2 policies with version numbers and last-review dates, previous version(s) to demonstrate continuity, policy change log showing what changed and when, policy approval evidence with dates and approvers, employee acknowledgement records showing completion rate, and policy review cycle documentation showing scheduled reviews were completed. Well-managed policy evidence is one of the simplest ways to demonstrate compliance maturity.
