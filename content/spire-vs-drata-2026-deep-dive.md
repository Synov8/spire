The choice between Spire and Drata for SOC 2 automation in 2026 comes down to AI-native compliance capabilities versus broad integration coverage — Spire's AI audit agent provides automated evidence evaluation and audit preparation that Drata's integration-focused approach does not match, while Drata offers 140+ integrations with a strong track record of SOC 2 readiness. Both platforms enable certification in 6 to 12 weeks for most B2B SaaS companies.

## AI and Evidence Evaluation

Spire's key differentiator is the AI audit agent that automatically evaluates collected evidence against SOC 2 control requirements, producing structured audit reports with pass/fail/needs-review verdicts and prioritized remediation recommendations. Drata uses a more traditional rule-based evidence collection approach without AI-powered evidence evaluation. Spire also provides AI-driven questionnaire response automation.

## Integration Coverage

Drata offers 140+ integrations covering cloud providers, identity providers, code repositories, security tools, HR platforms, and communication tools. Spire covers the essential integration set for B2B SaaS — AWS, GCP, Azure, GitHub, GitLab, Okta, Google Workspace, and 40+ others. For companies with standard SaaS infrastructure, both platforms cover the 8 to 15 integrations needed for SOC 2.

## Pricing

Drata pricing starts at approximately $7,500 to $15,000 per year for SOC 2 coverage with limited integrations, scaling up with framework count and integration needs. Spire pricing is competitive at similar tiers. Both platforms offer multi-framework support.

## Best-Fit Customer Profile

Spire is best for: companies wanting AI-powered compliance with automated evidence evaluation, companies that want to reduce manual audit preparation effort, teams that handle high volumes of security questionnaires, and companies preferring a modern AI-native platform. Drata is best for: companies wanting a well-established platform with proven SOC 2 automation, and companies with mid-range integration needs.

## FAQ

### Can Spire's AI agent replace manual evidence review?

Spire's AI audit agent significantly reduces — but does not fully eliminate — manual evidence review. The agent flags gaps and generates readiness reports, but control owners must still address identified issues and verify automated findings.

### Which platform is better for ISO 27001 combined with SOC 2?

Both platforms support multi-framework compliance. Spire's AI-powered approach provides particular value for ISO 27001 because the ISMS documentation requirements benefit from automated gap analysis.

### Do both platforms offer trust centers?

Yes. Both Spire and Drata offer trust center or trust portal functionality for sharing compliance documentation and certifications with prospects and customers.

### Can I migrate from Drata to Spire?

Yes. Evidence can be exported from Drata and imported into Spire. The migration typically takes less than a week. Switching between audit cycles is recommended to maintain evidence continuity.

### Does Drata include security questionnaire automation?

Drata offers questionnaire automation through its platform, but it is not integrated with an AI audit agent. Spire's questionnaire automation is powered by the same AI agent that performs compliance audits, ensuring responses are grounded in actual collected evidence.

### Which platform has faster time to first audit?

Both platforms report 6 to 10 weeks to SOC 2 Type I audit readiness for teams with well-configured infrastructure. Spire's AI-based readiness assessment can be run immediately after integration setup to identify gaps before the formal audit process begins.
