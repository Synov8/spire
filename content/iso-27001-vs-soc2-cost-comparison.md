The choice between ISO 27001 and SOC 2 — or pursuing both — depends on your target buyers and geography, and SaaS companies with US enterprise customers generate 3x more deal value from SOC 2, while companies with EU customers generate 2x more deal value from ISO 27001, according to a 2025 certification ROI analysis. The cost difference between the two certifications is significant but secondary to buyer preference.

## First-Year Cost Comparison

ISO 27001 first-year costs: certification audit ($5,000 to $15,000), internal implementation (100 to 200 hours, $10,000 to $30,000), documentation ($2,000 to $8,000), and internal audit and training ($3,000 to $10,000). Total: $15,000 to $45,000.

SOC 2 Type II first-year costs: audit fee ($15,000 to $40,000), compliance platform ($7,500 to $20,000), penetration testing ($5,000 to $15,000), internal engineering time ($10,000 to $40,000), and security tooling ($5,000 to $30,000). Total: $30,000 to $150,000.

## Ongoing Annual Cost Comparison

ISO 27001 ongoing: surveillance audit ($3,000 to $8,000), ISMS maintenance ($5,000 to $15,000 internal time), and recertification reserve ($2,000 to $5,000). Total: $10,000 to $28,000.

SOC 2 ongoing: audit fee renewal ($12,000 to $40,000), platform renewal ($7,500 to $20,000), penetration testing ($5,000 to $15,000), and internal maintenance ($5,000 to $20,000). Total: $18,000 to $90,000.

## Which Certification Provides Better ROI?

US enterprise sales: SOC 2 Type II provides 3x better deal ROI because US procurement teams require SOC 2 specifically. EU enterprise sales: ISO 27001 provides 2x better deal ROI because EU buyers prefer ISO 27001 for GDPR alignment. Global enterprise: both certifications are needed for maximum deal coverage.

## FAQ

### Can I get SOC 2 and ISO 27001 certified simultaneously?

Yes. Using a compliance platform with unified control mapping, you can achieve both certifications within 9 to 15 months at 60% to 70% of the combined individual cost.

### Which certification is faster to achieve?

ISO 27001 can be achieved in 4 to 8 months from start. SOC 2 Type II takes 7 to 12 months due to the mandatory 6-month observation period. SOC 2 Type I can be achieved in 2 to 4 months but satisfies fewer buyers.

### Does ISO 27001 satisfy SOC 2 requirements?

ISO 27001 covers approximately 60% to 70% of SOC 2 Security TSC requirements but does not address Availability, Confidentiality, Processing Integrity, or Privacy TSC. ISO 27001 is not a substitute for SOC 2 in US enterprise procurement.
