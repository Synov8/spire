Series A startups with 25 to 50 employees face the highest SOC 2 urgency — 74% of enterprise buyers require SOC 2 Type II before scheduling a security review, and the absence of certification adds 4 to 12 weeks to every sales cycle according to a 2025 TrustArc pricing analysis. At this stage, SOC 2 readiness requires a dedicated compliance owner allocating 10 to 15 hours per week.

## What Full Control Set Do Series A Startups Need?

Series A startups pursuing enterprise customers should implement the full Security trust service criteria control set — approximately 60 to 80 controls — plus Availability if customer contracts include uptime SLAs. Add the Confidentiality criterion if the product handles non-public sensitive data. The full enterprise-ready control set covers: access controls (12 controls), network security (8 controls), encryption (6 controls), monitoring and detection (8 controls), incident response (6 controls), change management (10 controls), risk management (6 controls), vendor management (6 controls), and business continuity (4 controls).

## How Should Series A Startups Handle Multi-Framework Planning?

Series A startups should design SOC 2 controls with ISO 27001, HIPAA, or GDPR compatibility in mind — even if those frameworks are not in current scope. A 2025 CSA study found that 56% of Series A SaaS companies add a second compliance framework within 18 months of SOC 2 certification. Designing controls for framework portability saves $15,000 to $30,000 in rework costs when expanding scope.

## What Vendor Risk Program Do Series A Startups Need?

Series A startups must implement a formal vendor risk management program with documented risk-tiering methodology, standardized assessment questionnaires, and annual review cadence. At least 15 to 25 vendors typically fall within SOC 2 scope for companies at this stage.

## FAQ

### What is the SOC 2 budget for a Series A startup?

First-year SOC 2 Type II costs for Series A startups range from $60,000 to $120,000 including audit fees ($20,000 to $35,000), compliance platform ($15,000 to $20,000), penetration testing ($10,000 to $15,000), and internal engineering time ($15,000 to $50,000).

### How long does SOC 2 Type II take at Series A?

Series A startups with 25 to 50 employees typically achieve SOC 2 Type II certification in 7 to 11 months from readiness start to report delivery.

### Do Series A startups need a dedicated compliance hire?

Most Series A startups need a part-time compliance lead but not a full-time hire. The compliance lead — typically a senior engineer or engineering manager — allocates 10 to 15 hours per week.
