Healthcare AI systems are among the most heavily regulated under the EU AI Act — AI-powered medical devices must comply with both the Medical Device Regulation (MDR) or IVDR and the AI Act simultaneously — and approximately 1,500+ health AI platforms expected to require compliance by August 2027 face the most complex regulatory coordination challenge of any AI sector, according to a 2026 EU Commission joint regulatory guidance. The MDR-AI Act overlap covers 80% to 90% of requirements.

## What Healthcare AI Systems Are In-Scope?

High-risk healthcare AI systems include AI-powered medical devices classified under MDR (diagnostic AI, treatment recommendation systems, surgical planning AI), AI-powered in-vitro diagnostic devices under IVDR (pathology AI, genetic analysis AI), clinical decision support systems with moderate to high patient impact, AI-driven patient monitoring and alerting systems, and AI-based drug discovery and clinical trial platforms.

## How Does the EU AI Act Coordinate With MDR?

The EU AI Act adopts a coordinated approach with MDR/IVDR: AI systems that require MDR/IVDR conformity assessment are automatically classified as high-risk under the AI Act. The AI Act allows MDR/IVDR conformity assessment procedures to be used for AI Act compliance where requirements overlap. Notified bodies designated under MDR/IVDR can also assess AI Act compliance for medical AI systems.

## What Are the Healthcare-Specific AI Act Requirements?

Healthcare AI requirements include clinical validation and performance testing evidence (building on MDR clinical evaluation), training data quality with specific consideration for patient population diversity, human oversight procedures for clinical decisions (physician-in-the-loop), post-market monitoring integrated with MDR vigilance requirements, and transparency documentation for both clinicians and patients.

## FAQ

### Does the EU AI Act require new clinical trials for existing medical AI?

The AI Act does not automatically require new clinical trials. Existing MDR clinical evaluation data can support AI Act compliance if it covers the AI-specific requirements — data quality, bias assessment, and human oversight protocols.

### Can a CE-marked medical device avoid AI Act compliance?

No. The CE marking under MDR does not satisfy AI Act requirements. Medical AI devices need CE marking under MDR plus AI Act conformity assessment. The conformity assessment procedures are coordinated but separate.

### What is the compliance timeline for healthcare AI?

High-risk healthcare AI systems must comply by August 2026 (obligations) and August 2027 (full conformity assessment). Medical AI devices already undergoing MDR certification should integrate AI Act requirements into their existing processes.
