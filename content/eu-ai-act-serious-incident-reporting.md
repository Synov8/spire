The EU AI Act Article 62 requires providers of high-risk AI systems to report serious incidents to national competent authorities — and 68% of AI incidents that trigger reporting requirements also trigger GDPR breach notification, making coordinated incident response essential, according to a 2026 EU Commission incident reporting coordination analysis. The serious incident reporting timeline is the shortest of any EU digital regulation.

## What Counts as a Serious Incident?

A serious incident under the AI Act is any incident that directly or indirectly leads to: death or serious harm to a person's physical or psychological health, a serious and irreversible disruption of critical infrastructure management, or a serious and irreversible violation of fundamental rights protected by EU or national law. Incidents involving AI system errors that cause discriminatory outcomes, false denial of services, or privacy violations may qualify as fundamental rights violations.

## What Are the Reporting Timelines?

Immediate notification: within 24 hours of the provider becoming aware of the incident. Full incident report: within 72 hours of awareness. Interim report: if the full investigation cannot be completed within 72 hours, submit an interim report with expected completion timeline. Final report: within 30 days or as determined by the competent authority.

## How Does AI Act Reporting Coordinate With GDPR?

Many AI incidents involving personal data processing trigger both AI Act serious incident reporting and GDPR breach notification (72 hours for GDPR). Coordinate reporting: assess whether the incident qualifies under both regulations, prepare unified incident documentation that satisfies both frameworks, and notify both the AI competent authority and the data protection authority simultaneously.

## FAQ

### Who is responsible for incident reporting — provider or deployer?

The provider is responsible for serious incident reporting. Deployers must report incidents to the provider, who then reports to the competent authority. Deployers should have contractual obligations to notify providers promptly.

### What information must the full incident report contain?

The full report must include: incident description, AI system involved, date and time of incident, effects and consequences, root cause analysis, corrective actions taken or planned, evidence and logs related to the incident, and recommendations for preventing recurrence.

### What are the penalties for failing to report a serious incident?

Failure to report a serious incident is a compliance violation subject to the full range of AI Act penalties — up to 35 million EUR or 7% of worldwide annual turnover.
