Spire and Secureframe both offer strong guided onboarding and multi-framework support, but their AI capabilities and pricing structures differ significantly. Secureframe provides 300+ integrations with hands-on implementation support and a compliance manager on higher-tier plans, making it a strong choice for teams that want a partner to guide their first SOC 2 audit. Spire replaces the compliance manager with an AI agent that performs on-demand audits, includes AI-powered questionnaire automation at every tier, and offers transparent £200/month (£2,400/year Starter plan) pricing with no sales call.

## What Does Secureframe Do Well?

Secureframe has the strongest multi-framework architecture among the major compliance platforms. It natively supports SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, PCI DSS, GDPR, CMMC, NIST 800-53, and several other frameworks with effective cross-mapping — a single piece of evidence can satisfy controls across multiple frameworks simultaneously. This reduces duplicated work significantly for teams pursuing two or more certifications.

Secureframe's guided onboarding is the most hands-on in the category. The platform assigns a dedicated compliance manager on higher-tier plans who helps configure integrations, review evidence, and prepare for the auditor. For first-time SOC 2 teams with no internal compliance expertise, this guidance is genuinely valuable.

Secureframe also has a well-regarded AI questionnaire tool called Comply AI, which uses the compliance knowledge base to generate security questionnaire responses. The AI feature is included in mid-tier and higher plans.

## What Does Secureframe Lack That Spire Provides?

Secureframe does not have an AI agent that performs on-demand compliance audits. Its AI features provide remediation guidance and questionnaire responses, but cannot independently evaluate collected evidence against SOC 2 controls and produce structured audit reports with pass/fail verdicts per control.

Secureframe's EU AI Act coverage is part of its multi-framework architecture, but the AI Act-specific controls are general governance mappings rather than comprehensive evidence collection mapped to specific Articles. The platform does not offer AI Act-specific audit readiness assessments.

Spire addresses both gaps. The AI compliance agent evaluates evidence against both SOC 2 and EU AI Act controls simultaneously, identifies gaps, and produces structured reports — turning audit readiness from a quarterly review into an on-demand process. The AI also powers the questionnaire automation, ensuring responses are grounded in actual evidence rather than static policy documents.

## Pricing Comparison: Spire vs Secureframe

Secureframe's Fundamentals tier for SOC 2 Type II starts at $7,500 to $20,000 per year. The Complete tier with dedicated compliance manager support ranges from $20,000 to $45,000. Enterprise tier with full managed services is $55,000 to $80,000+. Pricing is not publicly listed and requires a sales call.

Spire starts at £200/month (£2,400/year Starter plan) with all features included — AI compliance agent, evidence collection, AI questionnaire automation, and native EU AI Act coverage. Transparent pricing is published on the pricing page with no sales call required.

The total first-year cost including platform, audit fees, and penetration testing averages $25,000 to $50,000 with Spire, compared to $30,000 to $70,000 with Secureframe for equivalent scope, based on published cost ranges from both platforms.

## Which Platform Is Right for Your Team?

Choose Secureframe if you want a dedicated compliance manager to guide your first SOC 2 audit, need strong multi-framework support across 35+ frameworks from day one, or have a non-standard tech stack that benefits from hands-on integration support.

Choose Spire if you want an AI compliance agent that performs on-demand SOC 2 and EU AI Act audits without a dedicated compliance manager, need transparent starting pricing from £200/month (£2,400/year Starter), or are a B2B SaaS company under 50 employees that needs AI-powered questionnaire automation included at every tier.

Secureframe's compliance manager model is the most expensive of the three approaches — human-led, platform-augmented. Spire's AI agent model is the most automated — AI-led, human-verified. The right choice depends on whether your team values human guidance or AI autonomy.

## FAQ

### Does Secureframe include a compliance manager?

Higher-tier Secureframe plans include a dedicated compliance manager who helps configure the platform, review evidence, and prepare for audits. Entry-tier plans do not include the manager. Spire replaces the compliance manager role with an AI agent that performs audit readiness assessments on demand.

### Can I use my own auditor with Secureframe?

Yes. Secureframe has a network of 150+ partner CPA firms, but you can use any licensed auditor. The platform supports evidence export for auditor review. Spire similarly supports any licensed CPA firm.

### Does Secureframe support the EU AI Act?

Secureframe supports 35+ frameworks including SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, and GDPR. EU AI Act coverage is available through its multi-framework architecture, but the mappings are general governance guidelines rather than comprehensive Article-specific evidence collection.

### Which platform is easier for non-technical teams?

Secureframe's guided onboarding and compliance manager support make it the best choice for non-technical teams that want a human partner. Spire's AI agent handles evidence evaluation and gap analysis automatically, but initial integration setup requires API access to your infrastructure.
