Compliance managers are the operational backbone of SOC 2 certification — a 2026 Compliance Week survey found that organizations with dedicated compliance managers maintain certification with 60% less engineering time drain than those without. While executives set the strategy, compliance managers execute the day-to-day activities that keep SOC 2 controls operating effectively. This guide covers the compliance manager's operational role in SOC 2.

## What Does a Compliance Manager Do Daily?

Daily activities include: monitoring automated evidence collection for failures, reviewing security alerts and confirming response actions, verifying backup completion, checking vulnerability scan results, and responding to control owner questions. Weekly activities include: reviewing change management records, verifying access review completion, checking employee training completion, and updating compliance dashboards. Monthly activities include: generating compliance metrics reports, reviewing control effectiveness, and preparing for quarterly access reviews.

## How Do You Build an Evidence Collection Schedule?

Create a calendar-based evidence collection schedule: hourly (system health monitoring), daily (backup verification, threat detection), weekly (vulnerability scans, change management reviews), monthly (access control verification, training completion), quarterly (access reviews, penetration tests, vendor risk assessments), and annually (business continuity tests, policy reviews, risk assessments). Automate as many collection tasks as possible through your compliance platform.

## How Do You Coordinate Control Owners?

Assign a named control owner for each SOC 2 control. Provide control owners with clear documentation including the control objective, required evidence, and their specific responsibilities. Schedule regular check-ins with control owners (monthly for critical controls, quarterly for all controls). Create a control owner handbook with templates, timelines, and escalation procedures. Recognize and reward control owners who maintain strong compliance practices.

## What Auditor Communication Should Compliance Managers Handle?

Handle all operational auditor communication including: scheduling fieldwork dates, providing evidence requests, coordinating control owner interviews, managing evidence room access, responding to follow-up questions, and scheduling closing meetings. Maintain an auditor contact log. Prepare evidence packages before each audit phase. Proactively identify and communicate potential issues before the auditor discovers them.

## How Do Compliance Managers Handle Audit Findings?

When an auditor identifies a finding: document the finding with control reference and details, determine root cause, develop a remediation plan with owner and deadline, implement and test the fix, provide remediation evidence to the auditor, and update procedures to prevent recurrence. Track all findings in a remediation register with status, owner, and timeline. Report finding resolution to leadership monthly.

## What Metrics Should Compliance Managers Track?

Operational compliance metrics include: evidence collection success rate (target 99%), control owner response time, audit finding count by severity (tracking to zero), policy review completion rate, training completion rate, third-party risk assessment timeliness, and compliance dashboard accuracy. Present these in monthly compliance reviews with trend analysis and action items.
