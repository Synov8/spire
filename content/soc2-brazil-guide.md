Brazil is Latin America's largest SaaS market, and a 2026 Brasscom survey found that 69% of Brazilian SaaS companies targeting international expansion now pursue SOC 2 certification. Brazil's Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (LGPD) closely mirrors GDPR and aligns strongly with SOC 2 controls. Brazilian SaaS companies face additional requirements from the Central Bank for fintech services and ANPD guidelines. This guide covers SOC 2 for Brazilian SaaS companies.

## How Does SOC 2 Align with Brazil's LGPD?

LGPD's security requirements (Article 46) requiring administrative, technical, and security measures map directly to SOC 2 Security criteria. LGPD incident notification (Article 48) with ANPD timelines maps to CC7.3 incident response. LGPD data subject rights including access, correction, and deletion map to SOC 2 access controls. LGPD data protection officer requirements map to SOC 2 governance controls. Brazilian companies already LGPD-compliant have a strong SOC 2 foundation.

## What ANPD Guidelines Should You Follow?

Brazil's ANPD (Autoridade Nacional de Proteção de Dados) provides guidelines on security incident reporting, data protection officers, and international data transfers. Map ANPD guidelines to SOC 2 controls in your system description. Document your DPO designation and contact information. Implement LGPD-compliant incident notification timelines in your incident response procedures. Include ANPD guidelines in your compliance monitoring program.

## What Central Bank Requirements Apply for Fintech?

If your SaaS serves Brazilian financial institutions, the Central Bank of Brazil (BCB) cybersecurity resolution (BCB Resolution No. 85/2021) applies. Key requirements include: cybersecurity policy aligned with SOC 2 CC6.1, incident response plan aligned with CC7.3, business continuity plan aligned with A1.2, data backup aligned with CC6.6, and mandatory incident reporting to BCB within 24-72 hours depending on severity. Map BCB requirements to SOC 2 controls.

## How Do You Handle Data Localization?

LGPD does not require data localization but restricts cross-border data transfers without adequate safeguards. Document where customer data is processed and stored. Implement data residency using Brazilian cloud regions (AWS Sao Paulo, Azure Brazil South, GCP Sao Paulo) for customers requiring local data storage. Implement standard contractual clauses for cross-border data transfers.

## What International Data Transfer Considerations Apply?

LGPD restricts international data transfers to countries without adequate protection levels. Document your cross-border transfer framework. Implement LGPD-compliant transfer mechanisms including Standard Contractual Clauses. Include cross-border transfer provisions in customer data processing agreements. Map data flows by jurisdiction in your system description.

## What Evidence Should a Brazilian SaaS Company Prepare for SOC 2?

Your auditor will request: LGPD compliance documentation mapped to SOC 2 controls, ANPD security incident notification procedures, DPO designation documentation, BCB cybersecurity resolution alignment if serving financial services, data residency controls for Brazilian customer data, cross-border data transfer mechanisms, data processing agreements with LGPD provisions, and incident response procedures with ANPD and BCB notification timelines.
