A structured vendor due diligence checklist — scaled to vendor criticality — reduces third-party risk assessment time by 50% while maintaining comprehensive coverage, and companies that implement tiered vendor assessments complete 90% of assessments within 2 weeks compared to 4 to 6 weeks for uniform deep-dive assessments, according to a 2025 Shared Assessments Program benchmarking study. The checklist below provides three tiers of assessment depth.

## Tier 1: Basic Assessment (15 Questions)

Basic assessment applies to low-risk vendors — no access to customer data, no production system integration. Questions cover: basic security practices, SOC 2 or equivalent certification status, data deletion policy, business continuity practices, and primary data storage location.

## Tier 2: Standard Assessment (25 Questions)

Standard assessment applies to medium-risk vendors — access to non-sensitive customer data or integration with non-critical systems. Adds questions on: access control (MFA enforcement, SSO capability), encryption (data at rest and in transit standards), incident response (notification timeline, incident history), subcontractor management, data processing agreements, and right to audit clauses.

## Tier 3: Enhanced Assessment (35 Questions)

Enhanced assessment applies to high-risk vendors — processing customer data, production system integration, or infrastructure providers. Adds questions on: penetration testing frequency and results, vulnerability management SLAs, employee background checks, physical security (for data center providers), IP and data ownership terms, exit assistance and transition support, and compliance with specific regulatory frameworks (GDPR, HIPAA, PCI DSS).

## FAQ

### How often should vendor due diligence be repeated?

Low-risk vendors: every 2 years. Medium-risk vendors: annually. High-risk vendors: annually with quarterly monitoring of SOC report changes.

### What if a vendor refuses to complete the questionnaire?

If a critical vendor refuses a questionnaire, request their SOC 2 report or equivalent certification. If neither is available, conduct a standalone security assessment with their cooperation.

### Should vendor due diligence be conducted pre-contract or post-contract?

Pre-contract is preferred — assessment findings should inform contract terms and risk acceptance decisions. Post-contract assessments are acceptable for vendors already in place but should be completed within 90 days.
