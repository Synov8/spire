SaaS companies face a choice between three dominant security frameworks — NIST CSF 2.0, SOC 2, and ISO 27001 — and each serves a different purpose at a different cost, yet 40% of companies overinvest in frameworks their buyers do not require, according to a 2025 Gartner compliance framework selection study. Understanding which framework to prioritize depends on your buyers, geography, and company stage.

## Framework Purpose and Structure

NIST CSF 2.0: voluntary risk management framework, no certification, no audit required. Cost: $0 to $20,000 (self-implementation). Best for: demonstrating security maturity, supporting enterprise questionnaires, government contractor readiness.

SOC 2: attestation report issued by CPA firm, Type I (point in time) or Type II (6+ months). Cost: $30,000 to $150,000 first year. Best for: US enterprise sales, financial services, healthcare.

ISO 27001: certifiable ISMS standard, requires certification body audit. Cost: $15,000 to $45,000 first year. Best for: EU enterprise sales, global organizations, government procurement outside US.

## Which Framework Do Your Buyers Actually Want?

US enterprise buyers: SOC 2 Type II is required by 74% of enterprise procurement teams. ISO 27001 is accepted by some but not preferred. NIST CSF is referenced but not required.

EU enterprise buyers: ISO 27001 is the standard requirement. SOC 2 is increasingly recognized but not equivalent. NIST CSF is less relevant in EU procurement.

Government buyers: NIST CSF and FedRAMP (US), ISO 27001 (EU).

## What Multi-Framework Strategy Works?

Start with SOC 2 Type II if your primary market is US enterprise. Add ISO 27001 within 12 to 18 months if expanding to EU. Implement NIST CSF 2.0 as a complementary risk management framework regardless of primary certification choice.

## FAQ

### How do these frameworks interact?

SOC 2 and ISO 27001 controls overlap by 60% to 70%. NIST CSF 2.0 provides a risk management overlay that enhances both. Unified control frameworks can support all three simultaneously.

### Which framework is most cost-effective for a seed-stage startup?

SOC 2 Type I for $8,000 to $15,000 provides an attestation that satisfies early enterprise buyers. NIST CSF self-implementation at $0 to $5,000 provides questionnaire support without certification cost.

### Can I use NIST CSF to prepare for SOC 2?

Yes. Implementing NIST CSF 2.0 as a risk management framework creates the governance and control structure that directly supports SOC 2 compliance. Many SaaS companies implement CSF 2.0 first and add SOC 2 certification later.
