Government buyers follow procurement security standards that go beyond standard SOC 2 — FedRAMP for federal, StateRAMP for state and local, CMMC for defense contractors — and companies that prepare government-specific questionnaire responses close public sector deals 3 to 5 months faster than those that learn government requirements during the sales cycle, according to a 2025 GSA procurement analysis. Government security questionnaires are the most complex and detailed in any industry.

## What Government Security Standards Apply?

FedRAMP (Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program) applies to cloud services used by federal agencies. Three authorization levels: Low Impact, Moderate Impact, High Impact. FedRAMP authorization requires a 3PAO assessment and typically takes 6 to 18 months. StateRAMP applies to state and local government cloud procurement, modeled on FedRAMP with a faster authorization timeline of 3 to 9 months.

CMMC (Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification) applies to DoD contractors and subcontractors. Levels 1 to 5 with increasing maturity requirements. Level 2 is the most common target for SaaS companies.

## How Do Government Questionnaires Differ from Commercial?

Government questionnaires are longer (100 to 400 questions vs 30 to 100 for commercial), reference specific frameworks (NIST SP 800-53, FedRAMP controls, CMMC practices), require artifact submission (evidence of each control, not just attestation), include supplier-level security requirements, and require FedRAMP or StateRAMP authorization for award.

## What Responses Should Non-Authorized Companies Provide?

Companies without FedRAMP authorization should provide: SOC 2 Type II report as the compliance baseline, NIST SP 800-53 control mapping showing SOC 2 to NIST alignment, FedRAMP or StateRAMP readiness assessment (if pursuing authorization), and authorization timeline if actively pursuing FedRAMP or StateRAMP.

## FAQ

### Can SOC 2 satisfy FedRAMP requirements?

SOC 2 provides a compliance baseline but does not satisfy FedRAMP requirements. FedRAMP requires NIST SP 800-53 control compliance assessed by an accredited 3PAO. SOC 2 and FedRAMP can share evidence but have separate assessment processes.

### What is the cost of FedRAMP authorization?

FedRAMP Moderate authorization costs $500,000 to $1.5 million including 3PAO assessment ($100,000 to $300,000), internal engineering time ($200,000 to $600,000), and documentation and remediation ($200,000 to $600,000).

### Do state governments accept FedRAMP authorization?

Many states accept FedRAMP authorization through StateRAMP or direct reciprocity. StateRAMP provides a streamlined path for FedRAMP-authorized vendors to sell to state and local governments.
