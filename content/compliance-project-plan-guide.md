A structured 12-month SOC 2 Type II project plan — divided into 48 weekly milestones with defined owners and deliverables — increases the likelihood of on-time certification by 65% compared to ad-hoc compliance approaches, according to a 2025 AICPA project management study. The plan breaks into four phases aligned with the SOC 2 certification lifecycle.

## Phase 1: Readiness Preparation (Weeks 1 to 8)

Week 1: Scope definition and trust service criteria selection. Week 2: Auditor selection and engagement letter. Week 3: System description first draft. Week 4: Policy gap analysis and documentation kickoff. Week 5 to 6: Policy drafting for all in-scope policies. Week 7: Control mapping and evidence source identification. Week 8: Readiness assessment report and remediation plan.

## Phase 2: Remediation (Weeks 9 to 16)

Week 9 to 10: Infrastructure integration setup (cloud provider, identity provider, code repo). Week 11 to 12: Access control implementation and MFA enforcement. Week 13: Vendor risk assessment completion. Week 14: Penetration testing. Week 15: Employee security training. Week 16: Remediation completion verification.

## Phase 3: Observation Period (Weeks 17 to 42)

Week 17: Observation period begins — start continuous evidence collection. Month 3: First internal audit cycle. Month 4: Evidence quality review. Month 5: Second internal audit cycle. Month 6: Pre-audit walkthrough.

## Phase 4: Audit Fieldwork and Report (Weeks 43 to 48)

Week 43: Auditor kickoff meeting and evidence delivery. Week 44 to 46: Auditor fieldwork and control testing. Week 47: Auditor findings review and response. Week 48: Final report issuance.

## FAQ

### What if my readiness assessment finds critical gaps?

Pause the observation period start and remediate critical gaps first. Starting the observation period with unresolved critical gaps means those gaps will appear as exceptions in the Type II report.

### Can the remediation phase run in parallel with the observation period?

Some low-risk remediations can run during the observation period, but the standard approach is to complete all remediations before the observation period starts.

### How do I track project plan progress?

Use a shared project management tool (Asana, Linear, Jira) with weekly status reviews. Assign each milestone to a specific owner with a defined due date.
