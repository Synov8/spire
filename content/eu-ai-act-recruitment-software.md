Recruitment AI software — from resume screening to video interview analysis — is explicitly classified as high-risk under the EU AI Act Annex III, and companies using AI hiring tools face dual obligations: vendors must ensure their systems comply as providers, and employers must comply as deployers, according to a 2026 EU Commission guidance on AI in employment. Non-compliance penalties can reach 35 million EUR or 7% of global revenue.

## What Recruitment AI Systems Are High-Risk?

High-risk recruitment AI includes AI-powered resume screening and candidate ranking, automated skills assessment and testing platforms, AI video interview analysis (facial expression, tone, language), candidate matching and recommendation algorithms, personality and psychometric testing AI, social media screening for recruitment purposes, and AI-driven compensation and offer optimization.

All of these systems are high-risk because they directly impact individuals' employment opportunities — one of the protected domains under EU Charter of Fundamental Rights.

## What Are Vendor vs Deployer Obligations?

Vendor obligations: conformity assessment (risk management, technical documentation, data governance, transparency, human oversight, accuracy), CE marking, registration in EU database, post-market monitoring. Deployer obligations: fundamental rights impact assessment before deployment, human oversight of AI hiring decisions, transparency to candidates about AI use in the hiring process, right to explanation for AI-driven hiring decisions, and incident reporting for AI system problems.

## How Do You Ensure Bias-Free Recruitment AI?

Recruitment AI must comply with Article 10 of the AI Act requiring training data to be relevant, representative, free from errors, and complete. Bias mitigation requirements include: regular bias testing and fairness auditing, demographic parity monitoring across protected characteristics, documented bias mitigation methodology, and human review of AI recommendations before final hiring decisions.

## FAQ

### Can candidates request an explanation of AI-driven hiring decisions?

Yes. High-risk AI system deployers must provide meaningful explanations of AI-driven decisions to affected individuals. Candidates have the right to know how the AI evaluated them and the factors influencing the hiring decision.

### Does the EU AI Act ban automated CV screening?

The EU AI Act does not ban automated CV screening but requires it to comply with high-risk system obligations — bias testing, transparency, human oversight, and fundamental rights assessment. Automated screening without these safeguards is not compliant after August 2026.

### What constitutes "meaningful human oversight" in hiring AI?

Meaningful human oversight means a human can override or reverse the AI decision, understand the AI's reasoning, and has the authority to make independent judgments. Simply having a human click "approve" without understanding the AI output does not satisfy the requirement.
