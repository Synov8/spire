SOC 2 and ISO 27001 are the two most requested security certifications for B2B SaaS companies — a 2026 Gartner security certification survey found that 82% of enterprise procurement teams accept either SOC 2 or ISO 27001, while 34% require both. The choice between them depends on your customer base, geographic markets, and compliance timeline. This guide provides a detailed comparison to help you decide which certification to pursue.

## What Are the Key Differences?

SOC 2 is a compliance audit (attestation) focused on specific trust service criteria and controls. ISO 27001 is a management system certification focused on the Information Security Management System (ISMS). SOC 2 is issued by a CPA firm and specific to US markets. ISO 27001 is issued by an accredited certification body and recognized globally. SOC 2 requires annual re-certification. ISO 27001 requires annual surveillance audits with recertification every 3 years.

## How Do Costs Compare?

First-year SOC 2 Type II: $15K-$40K audit fees plus $12K-$36K compliance platform plus $10K-$20K preparation costs. First-year ISO 27001: $20K-$50K certification body fees plus $15K-$30K implementation support plus $12K-$36K compliance platform plus $15K-$25K preparation costs. ISO 27001 is typically 30-50% more expensive than SOC 2 in the first year due to the ISMS implementation requirements.

## What Are the Timeline Differences?

SOC 2 Type I: 3-4 months from start. SOC 2 Type II: 6-12 months depending on audit period. ISO 27001: 6-12 months from start depending on ISMS readiness. Companies with existing security programs can achieve both within 12 months. Most companies choose to pursue SOC 2 first (faster, narrower scope) and add ISO 27001 later if customer demand justifies the investment.

## Which Certification Do Customers Prefer?

US enterprise customers: SOC 2 is the default expectation. EU enterprise customers: ISO 27001 is more commonly requested. Global customers: either is accepted, with ISO 27001 preferred outside North America. Regulated industries: financial services prefers SOC 2, healthcare prefers both, government contracts may specify either. Geographic expansion should drive your certification strategy — if you're expanding to Europe, prioritize ISO 27001.

## Can You Leverage One for the Other?

Yes — SOC 2 and ISO 27001 share approximately 70% control overlap. If you achieve SOC 2 first, you can typically achieve ISO 27001 in 3-4 months by extending your existing controls. If you achieve ISO 27001 first, you can achieve SOC 2 in 2-3 months. Integrated audits (both certifications from the same engagement) can reduce total cost by 25-35% compared to separate audits.

## What Should You Choose?

Recommended decision framework: SOC 2 only if your primary market is US-based B2B SaaS and you have no immediate EU expansion plans. ISO 27001 only if your primary market is EU-based B2B SaaS. Both if you serve or plan to serve both US and EU enterprise customers, operate in regulated industries requiring both, or have enterprise customers that specifically require both. Most US-based SaaS companies should start with SOC 2.
