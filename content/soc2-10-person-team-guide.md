A 10-person team pursuing SOC 2 Type II should target 70% to 80% automated evidence collection across 8 to 12 infrastructure integrations, requiring approximately 15 to 20 engineering hours per week during readiness dropping to 5 to 8 hours per week for ongoing maintenance, according to a 2025 compliance operations survey. At 10 employees, the compliance load is distributed enough that no single person spends more than 20% of their time on compliance.

## How Should a 10-Person Team Structure Compliance?

Designate an engineering lead with 3 to 5 hours per week for compliance — typically a senior engineer or engineering manager. This person owns evidence automation, technical control implementation, and control owner coordination. A non-engineering team member — the CEO, COO, or operations lead — owns policy documentation, risk assessment, training, and vendor management. This two-track structure ensures both technical and administrative controls have dedicated ownership.

## What Controls Should Be Automated at the 10-Person Stage?

Automate: cloud infrastructure monitoring (AWS, GCP, Azure), identity provider configuration (Okta, Google Workspace, Azure AD), code repository access and change management (GitHub, GitLab), vulnerability scanning (Snyk, Wiz, CrowdStrike), endpoint security monitoring (Jamf, Kandji, Intune), employee training assignment and tracking, and vendor SOC report collection and review.

## How Does a 10-Person Team Handle On-Call and Incident Response?

At the 10-person stage, you should have a documented on-call rotation with at least two people. Incident response procedures should define primary and secondary responders. Test incident response procedures quarterly with tabletop exercises.

## FAQ

### What is the cost for a 10-person team SOC 2?

First-year Type II costs: $40,000 to $80,000 including audit ($18,000 to $30,000), compliance platform ($12,000 to $18,000), penetration testing ($8,000 to $12,000), and engineering time ($10,000 to $25,000).

### Should a 10-person team add ISO 27001?

Most 10-person teams should not add ISO 27001 in the first SOC 2 cycle. Wait until SOC 2 is stable and recurring before expanding frameworks. The exception is European customer demand for ISO 27001, which may justify simultaneous certification.

### What is the biggest compliance risk for a 10-person team?

Single points of failure. If only one person understands the compliance infrastructure, their departure creates significant risk. Cross-train at least two people on the compliance platform, evidence collection, and audit preparation procedures.
