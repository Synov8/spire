The EU AI Act phases in over several years, with different requirements becoming enforceable at different dates — a 2026 Gartner AI compliance survey found that 61% of organizations are unprepared for the phased deadlines, risking enforcement action for non-compliance. This calendar provides a year-by-year breakdown of AI Act deadlines, action items, and preparation activities through full implementation.

## 2025: Foundational Compliance Activities

January 2025 to July 2025: conduct comprehensive AI inventory across all business functions. Classify each AI system by risk level (prohibited, high-risk, limited, minimal). Identify and cease any prohibited AI practices (social scoring, manipulative AI, real-time biometric surveillance). Designate an AI compliance team or officer. Begin documentation for high-risk AI systems. Establish AI governance framework. By 2 February 2025: prohibited AI practices ban takes effect.

## August 2025: Transparency Obligations

By 2 August 2025: AI systems that interact with humans must provide transparency — chatbots must be identifiable as AI. AI-generated content (deepfakes, synthetic text, AI-generated images) must be labeled as AI-generated. Emotion recognition and biometric categorization systems must inform individuals. AI systems used to generate or manipulate images, audio, or video content must disclose AI generation. General-purpose AI models must comply with transparency and copyright requirements.

## August 2026: High-Risk AI Rules

By 2 August 2026: high-risk AI systems listed in Annex III must comply with full requirements — risk management system established and documented. Technical documentation completed and maintained. Training, validation, and testing data governance implemented. Transparency and provision of information to deployers. Human oversight mechanisms. Accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity measures. Conformity assessment completed (self-assessment for most Annex III systems). EU declaration of conformity issued. CE marking applied where required.

## August 2027: Extended High-Risk Rules

By 2 August 2027: high-risk AI systems referenced in Annex I (safety components of regulated products) must comply with AI Act requirements. This covers AI systems that are safety components of machinery, medical devices, toys, and other regulated products. These systems require third-party conformity assessment through notified bodies. Plan for longer assessment timelines — notified body capacity is limited and may face backlogs.

## 2028: Governance and Enforcement

AI governance requirements are fully operational: Member State market surveillance authorities active. AI Office at European Commission operational. National competent authorities for AI designated. Significant penalties in effect (up to 7% of global annual turnover for prohibited practices, 3% for other non-compliance). AI regulatory sandboxes operational across member states. Codes of conduct for voluntary application of requirements to non-high-risk AI systems.

## 2029-2030: Full Implementation and Review

By August 2029: European Commission completes review of the AI Act, assessing: whether the list of high-risk AI systems should be expanded, the effectiveness of the governance framework, implementation costs and benefits, and whether procedural AI requirements should be extended. By 2030: the AI Act is fully implemented across all provisions. AI standardization (harmonized standards) is mature. Best practices are well-established. Enforcement is consistent across member states. Regular compliance activities become business-as-usual.
