Here are cold outreach scripts that actually fit how SOC 2 / security pain is discussed in real SaaS companies. The goal is not “clever wording” — it’s timing + relevance + immediate payoff. These are written so you can copy/paste and lightly personalize.
I’ll give you 4 variants depending on the trigger.

---
1. SOC 2 in progress (highest conversion)
LinkedIn / Email
> Hey {{firstName}} — saw you’re working through SOC 2.
We’ve been building something that connects to AWS + GitHub + Google Workspace and auto-generates most of the audit evidence + keeps it continuously updated (instead of last-minute prep).
Curious if you’d be open to a quick 15-min chat? I can show how teams are cutting SOC 2 prep from months → days.



---
Why this works:
mirrors their current pain (“in progress”)
no hype words
outcome-based (“months → days”)


---
2. Security questionnaire blocker (VERY high urgency)
LinkedIn DM
> Hey {{firstName}} — quick one.
Are you currently dealing with security questionnaires for enterprise deals?
We’ve been building a tool that auto-fills ~70–90% of those using live system data (AWS/GitHub/etc.) + generates evidence automatically.
If this is still a pain point for you, happy to show it — it’s been saving teams a lot of time during procurement.



---
Why this converts:
starts with question (forces cognitive engagement)
ties directly to revenue blocking
avoids “AI fluff”


---
3. “Lost deal” trigger (highest urgency segment)
Email subject: Quick question about enterprise security reviews
> Hey {{firstName}},
I came across {{company}} while looking at SaaS teams dealing with enterprise security reviews.
A lot of companies we’re speaking with are losing deals or getting delayed because security questionnaires + SOC 2 evidence collection are taking too long to complete manually.
We’re building a system that automates most of that directly from AWS + GitHub so teams can respond in hours instead of weeks.
If this is something you’re running into, happy to show you how it works.



---
Why it works:
assumes pain is already happening
doesn’t ask them to imagine it
connects to revenue loss (key trigger)


---
4. Consultant / advisor partnership outreach (secret weapon channel)
LinkedIn message
> Hey {{firstName}} — quick one.
I’m building a tool that automates SOC 2 evidence collection + security questionnaire responses using live infrastructure data.
I’m speaking with a lot of early-stage SaaS teams going through audits, and I’m looking to partner with a few security consultants/fractional CTOs who already advise these companies.
Would you be open to a quick chat? Happy to offer referral terms if it’s useful for your clients.



---
Why this works:
not selling to them directly
opens revenue stream for them
positions you as “tool, not competitor”


---
5. Follow-up message (where most deals actually close)
Most founders don’t reply on first touch. This is the follow-up that works:
> Hey {{firstName}} — just bumping this in case it got buried.
If SOC 2 / security reviews are already under control, no worries.
But if it’s still taking significant time, I can show you a quick demo of how we’re automating most of the evidence + questionnaire work from live systems.



---
Why this works:
removes pressure
gives easy exit
still reopens pain


---
6. The “short brutal version” (for high-signal leads)
Use this when someone clearly fits (SOC2 tweet, hiring security engineer, etc.)
> Hey — quick one.
Are you still manually handling SOC 2 / security questionnaires?
We’re automating most of it directly from AWS + GitHub. Happy to show if relevant.



---
Key conversion principles behind ALL of these
If you strip everything down, they all do 5 things:
1. Trigger known pain
SOC 2, questionnaires, procurement delays
2. Tie to revenue or time loss
Never talk about “compliance” alone
3. Show integration-based credibility
AWS, GitHub, GCP → signals realism
4. Offer “show, not sell”
Always demo-oriented
5. Keep cognitive load low
No long explanations, no technical overreach

---
What NOT to do (kills response rate)
Avoid:
“AI-powered compliance platform”
long product descriptions
vague productivity claims
asking for “feedback” instead of offering value
